# Competitor Research: Incorporate123.co

## Executive Summary

Incorporate123.co (also known as Nevada123.com), operated by Nevada Corporation Services Ltd., is a niche incorporation service specializing in Nevada and Wyoming business formation with a strong emphasis on privacy, nominee services, and asset protection — including international/offshore structures. Founded in 2000 and based in Reno, Nevada, the company occupies a distinct position in the market by combining domestic incorporation with privacy-focused services traditionally associated with offshore providers. This report identifies and analyzes competitors across two tiers: direct niche competitors (privacy-focused incorporation and asset protection) and broader online LLC formation services.[^1][^2]

### Core Services

Incorporate123.co offers a tiered package system for Nevada and Wyoming entities, bundling formation with privacy and virtual office services:[^2][^5]

- **Nevada Bronze Package** — LLC from $1,000 / Corp from $1,825. Basic incorporation with registered agent and record keeping. No privacy or office features.[^6]
- **Nevada Silver Package** — LLC from $1,275 / Corp from $1,575. Adds Lake Tahoe virtual office via Kingsbury Executive Suites.[^7]
- **Nevada Gold Package** — LLC from $1,800 / Corp from $2,100. Full nominee officer/director/manager services for anonymous operation, plus virtual office.[^5]
- **Wyoming Packages** — Gold LLC/Corp from $1,275 with nominee services and mail forwarding. Silver at $900 with registered address and mail forwarding but no nominee.[^8][^9]
- **Shelf Companies** — Pre-aged Nevada and Wyoming corporations and LLCs starting from $1,500.[^4]
- **Banking Services** — Assistance opening corporate bank accounts in Nevada.[^10]
- **International/Offshore Services** — Bahamas Asset Protection Trusts, Nevis Corporations/LLCs, BVI Business Companies for international asset protection.[^11][^12]

### Key Differentiators

Incorporate123.co's main competitive advantage is its "internationalized" approach to privacy. After 9/11 and the USA Patriot Act, the company chose to resist sharing client information upon informal government requests — a practice the company claims most Nevada service providers adopted. All packages include state filing fees with no hidden costs, which the company highlights as a transparency advantage over competitors.[^13][^1][^5]

## Competitive Landscape Analysis

### Market Positioning Map

Incorporate123.co sits in the **mid-price, high-privacy** quadrant of the market. Its competitors can be mapped along two axes:

- **Price:** Ranges from $0 (ZenBusiness, Bizee) to $2,750+ (Anderson Advisors)
- **Privacy/Asset Protection Depth:** Ranges from basic formation only (ZenBusiness, LegalZoom) to full nominee + international trust structures (Incorporate123.co)

### Incorporate123.co's Competitive Advantages

1. **International/Offshore Integration** — The combination of domestic Nevada/Wyoming formation with Bahamas trusts, Nevis LLCs, and BVI structures is relatively rare among U.S. incorporators. Most niche competitors (NCH, Corporate Direct, Laughlin) focus exclusively on domestic entities.[^12][^11]

2. **All-Inclusive Pricing** — Including all state filing fees in package prices eliminates surprise costs. The company explicitly positions this against competitors who hide "2nd tier" filings.[^13][^5]

3. **Lake Tahoe Virtual Office** — The partnership with Kingsbury Executive Suites in Douglas County, Nevada (which does not require a county or city business license) provides a strategic location advantage.[^7][^5]

4. **Privacy Philosophy** — The company's stated refusal to share client information upon informal government requests differentiates it from incorporators who comply more readily.[^1]

### Incorporate123.co's Competitive Vulnerabilities

1. **Scale and Brand Recognition** — Against NCH (250K+ businesses formed), Laughlin (53 years in business), or ZenBusiness (850K+ businesses formed), Incorporate123.co lacks the volume and brand awareness to compete on trust signals alone.[^44][^14][^34]

2. **Website and Digital Presence** — The website appears dated compared to modern competitors like ZenBusiness, NCH, and Northwest Registered Agent. The domain is hosted in Sweden, which may raise trust concerns for U.S.-focused customers.[^4]

3. **No Attorney Credentials** — Unlike Corporate Direct (Garrett Sutton, Esq.) or Anderson Advisors (full advisory team), Incorporate123.co does not prominently feature attorney-led services, which matters in the asset protection and privacy niche.[^41][^19]

4. **Regulatory Risk** — The Corporate Transparency Act and increased federal scrutiny of nominee services and privacy structures create an uncertain regulatory environment. Competitors like NCH and Anderson Advisors appear better positioned to adapt due to their larger legal and compliance teams.[^49]

5. **Wyoming Migration Trend** — Nevada's increasing fees ($650/year for corporations, up from $325) are driving clients toward Wyoming, where annual fees are just $50. While Incorporate123.co does offer Wyoming packages, competitors like Wyoming Corporate Services have deeper Wyoming-specific expertise.[^50][^51][^28][^26]

## Strategic Recommendations

### Opportunities

- **Content Marketing & SEO** — Competitors like NCH and ZenBusiness invest heavily in educational content (blog posts, guides, FAQs). Incorporate123.co's Learning Center exists but appears underutilized. Creating authoritative content on asset protection, nominee services, and privacy strategies could capture organic search traffic from high-intent prospects.

- **International Niche Expansion** — The combination of domestic and offshore services is a genuine differentiator. Few competitors offer Bahamas trusts or Nevis LLCs alongside Nevada formation. Leaning into this as a primary positioning angle could attract high-net-worth individuals and professionals (especially attorneys and real estate investors) seeking comprehensive protection.[^11]

- **Website Modernization** — The current website design lags behind competitors significantly. A modern, trust-building design with transparent pricing, client case studies, and professional credentialing would improve conversion rates.

- **Wyoming Emphasis** — Given the trend of businesses migrating from Nevada to Wyoming due to fee increases, Incorporate123.co could more aggressively market its Wyoming packages as a cost-effective alternative while maintaining privacy and protection.[^51][^50]

### Threats

- **Corporate Transparency Act (CTA)** — Federal beneficial ownership reporting requirements may fundamentally change the value proposition of nominee services and privacy-focused incorporation. All players in this niche face this risk.[^49]

- **Price Pressure from Mass-Market Competitors** — $0 formation from ZenBusiness and Bizee resets consumer expectations on pricing. Incorporate123.co must clearly articulate why its premium pricing delivers proportionally greater value.[^44]

- **Nevada Fee Increases** — Ongoing increases in Nevada state fees (from $325 to $650 for corporations) could push more clients toward Wyoming or other jurisdictions, requiring adaptation of the product mix.[^51]

- **Reputation Risk** — The niche of privacy-focused incorporation operates in a gray area that attracts regulatory scrutiny. A Hacker News thread specifically criticized Incorporate123.co's nominee services as potentially facilitating tax avoidance. Managing this perception while maintaining the privacy value proposition is an ongoing challenge.[^54]
