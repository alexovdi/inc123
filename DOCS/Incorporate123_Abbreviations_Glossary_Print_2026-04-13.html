02 · Entity Types

Abbr. Full form Context
LLC Limited Liability Company Primary entity type offered. Combines liability protection with tax flexibility. Referenced in every tier and every package variant ("Wyoming Gold LLC," "Nevada Silver LLC," etc.).
Corp / Inc. Corporation / Incorporated Formal entity type with shareholders, directors, and officers. Offered as an alternative to LLC on every tier via the entity-type toggle.

S-Corp S Corporation Pass-through tax classification elected via IRS Form 2553. Generally advantageous above ~$80K annual net income.
DBA Doing Business As Fictitious-name filing (also "trade name," "assumed name"). Offered as a $50 add-on service.
IBC International Business Company Offshore entity structure, referenced on Offshore pages for BVI, Nevis, Panama formations.

10 · Legal & Governance Terms

Full-word terms (not abbreviations) that appear across the site and require a shared definition. Included here because they are domain-specific and carry legal weight.

Entity Formation

Articles of Incorporation / Organization
Foundational legal document filed with the Secretary of State to officially create a Corporation (Articles of Incorporation) or LLC (Articles of Organization).
Certificate of Authority
State-issued document confirming an entity is authorized to operate in a given state. Required for foreign-state registration.
Good Standing Certificate
Official state document certifying an entity's registration is current and all filings / fees are paid. Required for banking and foreign registration.
Shelf Company
Pre-formed entity that has never conducted business. Used when an older formation date matters (contracts, licensing, business credit).
Domestication
Moving an existing entity from one state to another without dissolving and re-forming. Preserves EIN, contracts, and history.
Foreign State Registration
Registering an entity in a state where it was not formed but conducts business. Required when operations or employees are physically in that state.

Ownership & Governance

Member / Manager
Owner of an LLC. A manager-managed LLC delegates operations; member-managed LLCs involve all owners equally.
Nominee (Officer / Director / Manager)
Person who appears on public state filings in place of the actual owner. Year-round nominee service is the Incorporate123 differentiator vs. "nominee for a day."
Operating Agreement
Internal LLC governance document specifying ownership, voting rights, profit allocation, and transfer rules.
Bylaws
Internal corporate governance document specifying board procedures, stockholder rights, and officer duties.
Corporate Minutes
Formal written records of board or member decisions. Absence can lead to corporate veil piercing.
Membership Interest
Ownership stake in an LLC — equivalent to shares in a corporation.

Tax Classification

Pass-Through Taxation
Income taxed only on the owner's personal return, not at the entity level. Default for LLCs and S-Corps.
Disregarded Entity
Single-member LLC treated as an extension of the owner for tax purposes. No separate return.
Franchise Tax
Annual state tax for the privilege of operating. WY ~$62, NV $350+, CA $800 minimum regardless of income.
Phantom Income
Tax owed on allocated income that was never distributed in cash. Can function as an asset-protection strategy.

Liability & Asset Protection

Limited Liability
Legal protection preventing owners from personal liability for entity debts. Core benefit of LLCs and corporations.
Piercing the Corporate Veil
Court action holding owners personally liable despite the entity. Prevented by proper minutes, separate finances, and timely filings.
Charging Order
Court order that limits a creditor to LLC distributions only — never entity assets or voting rights. Exclusive remedy in WY and NV.
Holding LLC
A separate LLC that owns other LLCs or valuable assets. Separates operating liability from asset protection.
Joint and Several Liability
Doctrine making each defendant liable for the full judgment. Abolished in Nevada — liability is proportional to fault.
Anonymous LLC
LLC where member / manager names do not appear in public Secretary of State records. Strongest in Wyoming.

Services & Compliance

Registered Office
Physical address where the registered agent receives legal documents and maintains required records.
Annual Report / Annual List
Required yearly filing confirming entity information. Late filing risks administrative dissolution.
Offshore Records Custodian
Third-party outside US jurisdiction maintaining corporate records. Foreign subpoenas cannot easily compel production.
Offshore Nominee
Nominee officer / director based outside the US, beyond the reach of US court subpoena power.
