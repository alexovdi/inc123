# Wyoming Privacy

Wyoming is the strongest domestic jurisdiction for business privacy. It is the only state that combines three critical features: no statutory requirement to disclose LLC member names, the lowest annual compliance costs of any privacy-friendly state, and the nation's strongest charging order protection for LLC interests. For business owners who want their personal information completely absent from public records, Wyoming is the formation state of choice.

Incorporate123 has been forming private entities in Wyoming for over two decades, and it remains our most recommended jurisdiction for clients whose primary concern is keeping their name out of searchable state databases.

## Why Wyoming Leads on Privacy

Wyoming's privacy advantages are structural — built into the state's LLC Act and business statutes — not the result of workarounds or creative filing strategies.

**No public member disclosure.** Under the Wyoming LLC Act, articles of organization do not require the names of members. The Secretary of State does not collect, record, or publish LLC member information. This is a statutory protection, not an administrative practice that could change with a new administration. Your ownership interest in a Wyoming LLC is simply not part of the public record.

**Minimal filing requirements.** Wyoming's annual report is a one-page form that requires the entity name, registered agent, and principal office address. It does not ask for member names, manager names (beyond what was in the original filing), or financial information. The annual report fee is $62 — the lowest of any privacy-friendly formation state.

**Strongest charging order protection.** Wyoming's LLC Act provides that a charging order is the exclusive remedy available to a judgment creditor against a member's LLC interest. This means a creditor cannot force the sale of your membership interest, cannot force distributions, and cannot seize the LLC's assets. Wyoming was the first state to provide this level of protection, and its statute has been tested and upheld in court.

**No state income tax.** Wyoming has no personal income tax, no corporate income tax, and no franchise tax. While this is primarily a tax advantage, it also eliminates an entire category of state-level filings that could potentially expose ownership information.

## How Wyoming Privacy Works in Practice

The privacy structure has several layers, each addressing a different point where personal information could become public.

**Formation filings.** The articles of organization list the entity name, registered agent, and the organizer. With our service, the organizer is our firm — your name does not appear. For manager-managed LLCs, the initial manager listed is our nominee. No member names are filed.

**Annual reports.** The Wyoming Secretary of State annual report requires the entity name, registered agent address, and principal office address. We use our Reno, Nevada office address (1795 Meadow Wood Lane) as the principal office. No member or manager names are updated or disclosed.

However, our operating agreement is structured to contain no personal ownership information. **Day-to-day operations.** You operate the company from a non-public executive position (CEO or Executive Vice President) with full authority. The nominee manager handles any interactions that touch the public record. For banking, you are identified as the beneficial owner to the bank under federal requirements, but this information is reported to FinCEN — not published in any state database.

## Wyoming vs. Nevada for Privacy

Both states offer strong privacy protections, and we form entities in both. The choice depends on your specific priorities.

**Wyoming advantages:** Lower formation cost ($1,275 vs. $1,800 for Gold packages), lower annual report fee ($62 vs. $325+ for Nevada), no state income tax, statutory non-disclosure of member names, strongest charging order protection.

**Nevada advantages:** Established business court system with judges who specialize in entity law, longer track record as a formation state (though Wyoming's LLC Act actually predates Nevada's), nominee-friendly statutes explicitly referenced in the code (NRS 78.105, NRS 86.241), and no state income tax.

For most clients focused primarily on privacy and cost efficiency, we recommend Wyoming. For clients who value Nevada's established court system or have specific Nevada-related business reasons, Nevada Gold is the better fit.

**Does Wyoming really not require member names in public filings?**
Correct. The Wyoming LLC Act does not require member names in articles of organization. The Secretary of State does not collect this information. This is a permanent statutory protection.

**Is Wyoming privacy limited to LLCs?**
No. Wyoming also offers privacy advantages for corporations, though the LLC structure provides the strongest combination of privacy and asset protection. We form anonymous corporations in Wyoming as well — see our [Anonymous Corporation](/anonymous-corporation/) page.

**Can a court order Wyoming to reveal my LLC membership?**
In active litigation involving your LLC, a court can order discovery that may reveal ownership. Wyoming's privacy protections apply to public records — they prevent casual searches and public exposure. They do not prevent court-ordered discovery in a legitimate lawsuit.

**What is charging order protection and why does it matter for privacy?**
A charging order is the only remedy available to a creditor against your Wyoming LLC membership interest. The creditor cannot seize the LLC's assets or force a sale. This matters for privacy because it means even if someone discovers your LLC ownership, they cannot use a judgment to take the LLC's property — significantly reducing the incentive to pursue litigation.

**How long does Wyoming formation take?**

**What about the Corporate Transparency Act — does that affect Wyoming privacy?**
The Corporate Transparency Act requires beneficial ownership reporting to FinCEN (a federal agency). This is a federal requirement that applies regardless of formation state. However, FinCEN reports are not public records — they are not searchable by the general public and are only accessible to law enforcement and certain financial institutions. Your Wyoming state filings remain private.
