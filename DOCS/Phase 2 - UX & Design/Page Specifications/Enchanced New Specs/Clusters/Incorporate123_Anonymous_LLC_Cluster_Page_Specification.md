**Content:**

```
Eyebrow: UNDERSTANDING ANONYMOUS LLCs

H2: What Is an Anonymous LLC?

Definition (visible, 80% view):
An anonymous LLC is a limited liability company formed in a state
that does not require public disclosure of its members (owners) or
managers. When properly structured, no one — competitors, attorneys,
data brokers, or the general public — can determine who owns or
controls the entity by searching state business records.

The term "anonymous" refers specifically to ownership privacy in
public records. The LLC itself is registered with the state and
operates legally. Its Articles of Organization are filed publicly.
But the names of the people behind it do not appear in those filings.

Two states make this possible for LLCs:

• Wyoming — Does not require disclosure of members or managers
  in Articles of Organization or annual reports. The strongest
  statutory privacy for LLCs in the United States.

• Nevada — Does not require disclosure of LLC members. However,
  corporations in Nevada must disclose officers and directors.
  For LLCs specifically, Nevada matches Wyoming on formation
  privacy.

Key Distinction (callout box, blue left border):
"Anonymous at formation" is not the same as "anonymous ongoing."
Many providers file your LLC with a nominee's name, then immediately
replace it with yours in subsequent filings — annual reports,
amendments, registered agent changes. Within weeks, your name
is on public record. A genuinely anonymous LLC requires year-round
nominee services that maintain privacy on EVERY filing, not just
the initial one.

[Expandable: "Statutory basis — Wyoming and Nevada LLC privacy laws"]
Wyoming: W.S. § 17-29-201 — Articles of Organization require only
the name of the LLC, registered agent, and organizer. No member or
manager names required.

Nevada: NRS 86.161 — Articles of Organization require the name of
the LLC, registered agent, and name/address of organizer. No member
names required. Note: NRS 86.263 requires an annual list of managers
or managing members, but a nominee manager satisfies this requirement.

[Expandable: "How anonymous LLCs differ from other privacy structures"]
Anonymous LLC vs. Anonymous Corporation: LLCs have simpler formation
and no officer/director disclosure. Corporations in Nevada require
officer/director names (solved with nominees) and have more formal
compliance requirements.

Anonymous LLC vs. Trust: A trust provides estate planning benefits
but living trusts have zero asset protection. An LLC provides both
privacy and structural asset protection via charging order protection.

Anonymous LLC vs. Offshore Entity: Offshore entities (Nevis, BVI)
provide jurisdictional separation but involve more complexity and
cost. Domestic anonymous LLCs are simpler, less expensive, and
sufficient for most privacy needs.
```

Eyebrow: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

H2: Anonymous LLC Questions Answered

Q: Is an anonymous LLC legal?
A: Yes. Forming an LLC in a state that doesn't require public ownership
disclosure is entirely legal. Wyoming and Nevada have specifically
structured their LLC statutes to allow private formation. Using nominee
services to maintain that privacy on subsequent filings is also legal
and has been standard practice for decades.

Q: Can the government still find out who owns an anonymous LLC?
A: Federal agencies (IRS, FinCEN) can access ownership information
through tax filings and, in some cases, Beneficial Ownership Information
(BOI) reports. An anonymous LLC protects you from public records
searches — the kind conducted by competitors, attorneys, data brokers,
and individuals. It does not hide ownership from law enforcement with
proper legal process.

Q: What is the difference between an anonymous LLC and nominee services?
A: An anonymous LLC is the result; nominee services are the mechanism.
An anonymous LLC is formed in a state (Wyoming or Nevada) that doesn't
require member disclosure. Nominee services maintain that anonymity
by placing a nominee's name on all ongoing filings — annual reports,
amendments, and any other state documents. Without year-round nominee
services, your name will eventually appear on a subsequent filing.
→ Full nominee services explanation (/nominee-services/)

Q: Can I open a bank account with an anonymous LLC?
A: Yes. Banks require the identity of the actual beneficial owner(s)
for Know Your Customer (KYC) compliance, but this information is held
by the bank under their privacy policies — it is not part of the public
state record. Your anonymous LLC can open and operate bank accounts
normally. Incorporate123 includes bank account opening assistance with
every package.

Q: How much does an anonymous LLC cost?
A: Wyoming Gold (anonymous LLC with year-round nominees): $1,275
formation, $525/yr renewal. Nevada Gold: $1,800 formation, $725/yr
renewal. Both include state filing fees, registered agent, EIN, operating
agreement, nominee services, and offshore records custodian. These are
all-inclusive prices — no hidden fees.

Q: What's the difference between an anonymous LLC and an anonymous corporation?
A: Both use nominee services for privacy, but LLCs are simpler — no
board of directors, no stock requirements, no officer/director
disclosure requirements (in WY). Corporations in Nevada require annual
disclosure of officers and directors, which nominees satisfy but add
compliance complexity. For most privacy-seeking individuals, an LLC is
the better choice.
→ Anonymous Corporation guide (/anonymous-corporation/)

Q: Do I need to live in Wyoming or Nevada to form an anonymous LLC there?
A: No. Any U.S. or international resident can form an LLC in Wyoming
or Nevada regardless of where they live. Your registered agent (included
in every package) provides the required in-state address. If you operate
your business in another state, you may need foreign registration in
that state — we handle this for California and Florida specifically.
→ California private incorporation (/california-private-incorporation/)
→ Florida private incorporation (/florida-private-incorporation/)

Q: Will my anonymous LLC protect me from lawsuits?
A: An anonymous LLC provides privacy protection — it prevents people
from connecting your name to your business assets via public records.
This is your first line of defense because most lawsuits begin with a
public records search to identify targets. For direct asset protection
against judgments, you also need charging order protection — which
Wyoming and Nevada LLCs provide. Privacy and asset protection are
complementary strategies, not substitutes.
→ Asset protection overview (/asset-protection/)
→ Charging order protection explained (/charging-order-protection/)

Q: What happens to my privacy if I need to amend my LLC?
A: With year-round nominee services, any amendments filed with the
state continue to use the nominee's name — not yours. This includes
name changes, address changes, manager changes, and any other state-
level modification. Your privacy is maintained through every filing
event, not just the initial formation.

Q: Can I convert an existing LLC to an anonymous LLC?
A: Yes. If you have an existing LLC in Wyoming or Nevada, we can add
nominee services and restructure the entity for privacy. If your LLC
is in another state, we can domesticate it to Wyoming or Nevada (moving
the entity without dissolving it) and then add privacy features.
→ Domestication guide (/domestication/)

Q: What about the Corporate Transparency Act and BOI reporting?
A: Federal BOI reporting requires disclosure of beneficial owners
to FinCEN, but this information is held in a confidential federal
database with strict access controls. It does NOT appear in the
state business registry that the public can search. Your state-level
anonymity through nominee services remains fully intact.

Q: How is Incorporate123 different from other anonymous LLC services?
A: Three things. First, year-round nominee services — our nominees
remain on every filing, every year, not just at formation. Second,
