# Kickoff Meeting Questions for David
## Incorporate123 Website Redesign — BIT Studios

**Prepared by:** Alex / BIT Studios  
**Purpose:** Confirm open strategic decisions, fill knowledge gaps, and align on priorities before Discovery begins.

---

## 1. STRATEGIC DIRECTION & POSITIONING

These questions confirm the foundational decisions the entire project is built on.

1. **Pillar Priority Confirmation:** We've positioned Business Privacy as the lead pillar since it's your biggest differentiator and has the strongest SEO opportunity (5,500–9,500 monthly searches). Do you agree Privacy should lead, with Asset Protection second?

2. **Compliance as a Pillar:** Should Compliance be a full standalone pillar, or would those topics (registered agent, annual reports, foreign registration, domestication) work better as supporting content within the other three pillars? Our strategy doc flags this as an open question.

3. **Repositioning Comfort Level:** The strategy moves you from "Nevada incorporation company" to "Privacy, Asset Protection & Business Formation Specialists." Are you fully comfortable with that shift, or are there aspects of the Nevada-first identity you want to preserve?

4. **Gold Package Positioning:** Should Gold packages be positioned as the "standard" recommendation (with Silver as a basic/entry option), or do you prefer presenting them as equals?

5. **Offshore Services Strategy:** Offshore (Nevis, BVI, Panama, Hong Kong) is currently scoped as de-emphasized — accessible via secondary navigation but not a main pillar. GILTI complexity has reduced US demand. Is that the right balance, or do you want offshore featured more prominently?

---

## 2. PACKAGES & PRICING

Package restructuring is a core deliverable. We need clarity on the final structure.

6. **Current Package Inventory:** Can you walk us through every current package and what's included? We need the definitive list to map the consolidation from overlapping packages → base packages + add-ons.

7. **How Many Base Packages?** You confirmed "base packages (plural)" with optional add-ons. Are you envisioning 2 tiers? 3 tiers (Basic/Executive/MAX)? Something else?

8. **Add-On Services — Complete List:** Beyond what's at incorporate123.co/services/add-on-services/, are there any add-on services that aren't currently listed online?

9. **Pricing Flexibility:** Will pricing stay the same ($1,800–$2,100 range), or is this redesign also an opportunity to adjust pricing? Should we design the package pages to accommodate future price changes easily?

10. **Shelf Company Inventory:** You have aged entities dating back to 2000 ($2,000–$12,000). How do you want these presented? Browsable inventory with real-time availability, or inquiry-based? How many are typically available at any given time?

11. **LLC vs. Corporation Split:** How do you want to handle the LLC/Corporation distinction on package pages? Separate packages, or a toggle/selector within the same package page?

---

## 3. CONTENT & MESSAGING

Content is client-supplied per the SOW. These questions shape the content briefs we provide.

12. **"Nominee for a Day" Messaging:** This is a key differentiator — competitors skip ongoing minute maintenance. How aggressive do you want to be in calling this out? Direct competitor comparison, or educational without naming names?

13. **Fear-Based Language:** The current site uses phrases like "VERY BAD DAY" and "BULLET PROOF." The brief flags this as a problem. What tone do you want instead — professional/authoritative, approachable/educational, or somewhere in between?

14. **Asset Protection Education — Depth:** You've explained the LLC vs. living trust distinction, irrevocable trust dynamics, and the ownership/control dilemma. How deep should the site go on legal education? Are there compliance concerns about how much legal detail you can publish without it being construed as legal advice?

15. **Case Studies / Client Stories:** Can we create anonymized case studies showing how clients use your services? (e.g., "A California real estate investor who needed privacy" — without identifying details.) These would be powerful trust signals.

16. **Existing Content Worth Preserving:** What content from the current site absolutely must be preserved word-for-word versus content that should be rewritten? Are there specific pages David considers his best work?

17. **Industry-Specific Pages:** Should we create pages targeting specific professions (real estate investors, medical professionals, consultants, tech entrepreneurs)? If so, which industries are your most common clients?

18. **Learning Center / FAQ Content:** The current Learning Center has ~111 FAQ items across overlapping categories. Are these still accurate and worth migrating, or should we start fresh? Any that are outdated?

---

## 4. TRUST & SOCIAL PROOF

The current site has essentially zero third-party trust signals. This is a competitive vulnerability.

19. **Review Platform Decision:** You were going to research BBB alternatives (G2, Trustpilot, etc.). Have you made a decision? What platform works for this type of business?

20. **Testimonials Inventory:** Do you have existing client testimonials beyond what's on the current site (which has spam text issues)? Can we get fresh testimonials from satisfied clients before or during the project?

21. **Trust Signals Priority:** Rank these in order of importance for your audience: 25 years in business, real physical office in Reno, offshore/international expertise, year-round nominee services, cryptocurrency payments accepted, all-inclusive transparent pricing.

22. **Professional Affiliations / Certifications:** Are there any industry certifications, memberships, or professional affiliations we should feature?

---

## 5. DOMAIN & TECHNICAL

These decisions affect SEO migration planning and development.

23. **Primary Domain Decision:** Have you decided between incorporate123.co (existing SEO history) and incorporate123.com? Irish was going to confirm the migration impact — have you discussed?

24. **Standalone Domain Strategies:** Does `businesswithoutborders.global` or `offshore123.com` warrant its own content strategy, or should everything point to the primary domain?

25. **CMS Preference:** The SOW allows for WordPress or Payload CMS, to be decided after Discovery. Do you have a preference or prior experience with either? Any strong feelings about the platform?

26. **Payment Gateway:** The checkout flow needs Stripe and/or PayPal. Do you already have merchant accounts with these providers, or do we need to set them up?

27. **Cryptocurrency Payments:** You currently accept Bitcoin, Ethereum, XRP, Litecoin, and Monero. Should crypto payment stay on the new site? If so, what processor do you use?

28. **Hosting:** Where is the current site hosted? Do you have a preference for hosting the new site, or should we recommend options?

29. **Google Analytics / Search Console:** These were being installed fresh (since David was locked out of the old account). Has that been completed? Who needs admin access?

30. **Email / Contact Systems:** What happens when someone submits a contact form or places an order currently? What email system do you use? Any CRM?

---

## 6. BRAND & DESIGN

Design starts in Week 2. We need direction.

31. **Brand Assets:** Do you have current logos, brand colors, fonts, or a style guide? Or is this a fresh brand identity as part of the redesign?

32. **Design Inspiration:** Are there any websites (competitor or otherwise) whose design you particularly like or dislike? Any visual style preferences?

33. **Photography / Imagery:** Do you have professional photos of the office, team, or David? Should we plan for stock photography, custom illustrations, or a brand photo shoot?

34. **David's Bio / About Page:** How prominent should your personal story and expertise be on the site? Many privacy specialists lead with the founder's authority. Are you comfortable being the face of the brand?

---

## 7. OPERATIONS & PROCESS

These affect how the site functions day-to-day post-launch.

35. **Consultation Flow:** When someone clicks "Schedule a Consultation" or "Get Started," what's the ideal process? Phone call, form submission, calendar booking tool (Calendly, etc.)?

36. **Order Fulfillment:** Once someone completes checkout, what's the fulfillment workflow? Who processes orders, what's the turnaround, and how does the client get their documents?

37. **Content Responsibility — Ongoing:** After launch, who will update the site? Do you have someone technical on your team, or will you need BIT Studios for ongoing maintenance?

38. **Client Communication Preferences:** During the project, what's your preferred way to communicate — email, phone, Slack, scheduled calls? How frequently do you want updates?

---

## 8. TIMELINE & PRIORITIES

39. **Hard Deadlines:** Is there a specific date you need the new site live by, or is quality more important than speed?

40. **Phase Priority:** If we need to make trade-offs, which is more important to launch with: the full 71-page architecture, or a polished subset of the most critical pages with the rest following?

41. **Competitor Urgency:** Are any competitors making moves that create urgency? Is there a market window we should be aware of?
