# Incorporate123 — Homepage Content & Rules

Everything a designer needs to build this homepage. Nothing about how it should look.

---

## Brand

**Company:** Incorporate123 (the "123" is visually distinct from "Incorporate")
**Position:** Premium self-service privacy, asset protection, and business formation. Not budget. Not consultation-gated.
**Founded:** ~2000. 25 years in business.
**Office:** Kingsbury Executive Suites, Reno, Nevada (real physical office — a differentiator)

**Voice:** Authoritative. Educational. Transparent. Reassuring. Precise. Every number is specific and verifiable.

**Always say:** "Business Incorporation" (not "Company Formation"), "All-inclusive" (not "Starting at"), "Year-round nominees" (not just "Nominee services"), "Privacy" (not "Anonymity"), specific prices like "$1,275" (never "affordable" or "competitive pricing"), "Protect Your Privacy" (not "Hide Your Identity")

**Never do:** Fear-based selling. Geography-first messaging. Stock photos of people. Price apology. Countdown timers. Urgency language.

## Three Pillars

Each pillar has its own visual identity. Privacy always dominates. The identities must be distinct from each other and from CTA styling.

**Privacy (#1):** The lead. Highest visual weight, always first.
**Asset Protection (#2):** For individuals with $500K+ in assets.
**Business Incorporation (#3):** Important but not the lead message. Always last of three.
**Compliance (optional):** Supporting only. Never equal weight to the three above.

## What must appear on the page (in this order)

### 1. Persistent Navigation

Present on every viewport. Contains:
- Logo → `/`
- Nav: Privacy (dropdown), Asset Protection (dropdown), Formation (dropdown), Packages (direct link → `/compare-packages/`), About (dropdown: About, Contact, Testimonials)
- Client Login → `/login/` — always visible, never buried
- Phone → `tel:7755551234` — always one tap away
- "Get Started" → `/compare-packages/` — secondary style, never competing with page CTAs

On mobile: hamburger preserving pillar structure (not alphabetical), Packages and Login immediately reachable, phone one tap away, compact on scroll.

### 2. Opening Statement

The first thing anyone sees. A CTA must be visible without scrolling.

**Headline (h1):** Max 8 words. Privacy-first, outcome-focused. Never geography-first or entity-type-first.

**Supporting line:** Max 25 words. Must include at least one specific number.
*Placeholder: "25 years specializing in business privacy, asset protection, and incorporation for Wyoming and Nevada. All-inclusive packages — no hidden fees."*

**Primary action:** "Protect Your Privacy" → `/privacy/`
**Secondary action:** "View Packages & Pricing" → `/compare-packages/`
**Escape hatch:** Phone number as subtle text, not a button. → `tel:7755551234`

**Trust proof:** Three facts — "Trusted Since 2000" / "25 Years Specialized" / "Real Reno, Nevada Office"

**Visual element:** Abstract privacy/shield/vault motif. No people. No stock photography.

## CTA Rules (non-negotiable)

1. One primary + one secondary action per area. Never three competing buttons.
2. Phone/consultation always appears near self-service actions as subtle text — never a competing button.
3. At least one action visible without scrolling on all devices.
4. Primary action repeats before the footer.
5. Navigation CTA is always secondary — never competes with page actions.
6. Gold is the only package with a primary action.
7. Phone number appears three times: navigation, trust reinforcement, closing.

---

## Mobile

- Persistent action bar after scrolling past the opening: primary CTA + phone
- 44×44px minimum tap targets
- Body text minimum 16px

---

## Accessibility

- WCAG 2.1 AA contrast
- One h1, hierarchy follows
- Keyboard navigable, focus order matches reading order
- ARIA labels on icon-only elements
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`

---

## What is NOT specified (and shouldn't be)

No colors. No fonts. No spacing. No layout. No grid. No shapes. No dark/light preference. No animations. No component patterns. No "cards" or "strips" or "bars" or "blocks" or "badges." The designer decides all of this.
