# California Business Formation

California is the largest economy in the United States and home to millions of businesses. However, California's tax structure and limited native privacy protections make it one of the more challenging states for business owners who value confidentiality and asset protection. The most effective approach for California-based entrepreneurs is to form your entity in a privacy-friendly state like Wyoming or Nevada, then register it as a foreign entity in California to operate locally.

## Why Form Outside California?

California imposes costs and disclosure requirements that privacy-conscious business owners should understand:

- **$800 Annual Franchise Tax** — Every LLC and corporation doing business in California owes a minimum $800 franchise tax per year, regardless of revenue or profit. This applies even to entities formed in other states that register in California.
- **Limited Privacy Protections** — California requires the disclosure of manager or member names for LLCs in the Statement of Information, which is a public record.
- **Higher Tax Burden** — California imposes both personal and corporate income taxes, with top marginal rates among the highest in the nation.
- **LLC Fee on Gross Revenue** — LLCs with California-sourced gross revenue exceeding $250,000 pay an additional annual fee ranging from $900 to $11,790.

These realities do not mean you should avoid doing business in California. They mean you should structure your California business presence strategically.

## The California Private Strategy

Our recommended approach for California-based business owners:

1. **Form in Wyoming or Nevada** — Establish your LLC or corporation in a state with strong privacy protections, low formation costs, and favorable asset protection laws.
2. **Register as a foreign entity in California** — File a Statement and Designation by Foreign LLC (or equivalent for corporations) with the California Secretary of State. This gives your entity the legal right to conduct business in California.
3. **Operate locally with out-of-state protections** — Your entity's governing law is that of the formation state (Wyoming or Nevada), including its privacy protections and charging order statutes. California sees the entity as a registered foreign company.

This is a standard, widely used corporate structure. Hundreds of thousands of businesses operating in California are formed in other states.

## Quick Facts

- **California Franchise Tax:** $800/year minimum (applies to all entities doing business in CA)
- **California LLC Fee:** $900-$11,790 based on gross revenue over $250,000
- **Foreign Entity Registration:** Required if doing business in California
- **Statement of Information:** Required every 2 years (LLC) or annually (Corporation)
- **Privacy Level (native CA entity):** Limited — officer and manager names in public record
- **Privacy Level (WY/NV + CA foreign reg):** Strong — formation state records remain private

## Privacy & Nominee Services for California Businesses

When you form in Wyoming or Nevada with nominee services and register in California as a foreign entity, the California filing only shows the entity name and California registered agent. Your personal information remains protected in the formation state's records, where nominee officers/directors/managers appear in your place.

This layered approach provides privacy protections that a native California entity simply cannot match.

## Asset Protection for California Residents

California's charging order protections are weaker than Wyoming's or Nevada's, and California courts have shown willingness to apply California law to the membership interests of California residents even in entities formed elsewhere. However, the formation state's protections still provide significantly more insulation than a native California LLC.

For clients with substantial assets, combining a Wyoming or Nevada domestic structure with an [offshore entity](/offshore/) creates multiple jurisdictional barriers that are difficult for any single creditor to overcome.

## Compliance

Operating a foreign entity in California requires:

- Annual or biennial Statement of Information filing with the California Secretary of State
- $800 minimum franchise tax payment to the Franchise Tax Board
- California tax returns if the entity has California-sourced income
- Compliance with the formation state's requirements (annual reports, registered agent, etc.)

## FAQ

**Do I still pay the $800 franchise tax if I form in Wyoming?**
Yes. Any entity doing business in California owes the $800 minimum franchise tax, regardless of where it was formed. The advantage of forming in Wyoming or Nevada is privacy and asset protection, not California tax avoidance.

**Can California courts pierce my Wyoming LLC's protections?**
California courts may attempt to apply California law to charging order disputes involving California residents. However, the formation state's law governs the internal affairs of the entity, and Wyoming's and Nevada's statutes provide stronger protections than California's. The legal analysis is complex, and this is an area where having a formation state with strong case law (like Nevada) can be particularly valuable.

**Is this structure legal?**
Absolutely. Forming in one state and operating in another through foreign registration is one of the most common business practices in the United States. Delaware, Wyoming, and Nevada are the most popular formation states for exactly this reason.

**How long does California foreign registration take?**
California processes foreign entity registrations in approximately 5-7 business days. Expedited processing is available for an additional state fee.

**Do I need a California registered agent?**
Yes. Any entity registered as a foreign company in California must designate a California registered agent for service of process. Our California Private Package includes this.

**Can I hold California real estate through a Wyoming LLC?**
Yes. Many of our California Private clients use this structure specifically for holding real estate. The Wyoming LLC owns the property, and the California foreign registration authorizes the entity to own property in California. Your name stays off both the state corporate records and the deed.

**What about the California LLC fee on gross revenue?**
LLCs with California-sourced gross revenue exceeding $250,000 pay an additional annual fee ranging from $900 to $11,790. This fee applies regardless of where the LLC is formed. The California Private structure does not avoid this fee — it provides privacy and asset protection benefits, not tax avoidance.

**Can I form in Nevada instead of Wyoming for my California entity?**
Yes. The California Private Package can use either Wyoming or Nevada as the formation state. Wyoming is typically recommended because of its lower costs and stronger single-member LLC protections. Contact us to discuss which formation state is best for your situation.

**Is there a first-year exemption from the $800 franchise tax?**
California has periodically offered first-year exemptions from the $800 minimum franchise tax for new LLCs. Check with the California Franchise Tax Board or your tax professional for current exemption availability, as these provisions change.

**How long does the full California Private process take?**
Wyoming formation typically completes in 1-2 business days. California foreign registration takes an additional 5-7 business days. The complete process from order to fully registered entity is usually 7-10 business days.
