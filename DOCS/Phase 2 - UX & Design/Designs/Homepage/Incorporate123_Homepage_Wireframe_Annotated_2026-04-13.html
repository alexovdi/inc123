04
Agitate
"Why Privacy Matters" Bridge
What it is
Editorial 2-column section teaching the problem that privacy solves — how public filings expose owner names to lawsuits, scrapers, and competitors. Ends with a soft pivot into "here's how we fix that."
Why this position (hook → agitate)
The hero makes the promise; this section earns the right to sell by establishing urgency. Problem must come before the packages or pricing feels premature.
Content purpose
Specific, visceral facts — "your home address is public," "$30K–$100K in defense costs," "scraped within 48 hours of filing." Turns abstract privacy into concrete risk.
Section 2 · bg-background · Editorial 2-col

Why Privacy Matters

Public Filings Expose More Than You Think.

When you file an LLC, most states publish your full legal name, home address, and personal details within 24 hours — forever. Scrapers, solicitors, lawsuit plaintiffs, and competitors find you before your first customer does.

⚠
Your Home Address Is Public
Default state filings list the registered agent and member — often the home address of a first-time founder.
⚖
Lawsuits Target Visible Owners
$30K–$100K in typical defense costs. Plaintiff attorneys search public entity databases first.
🕷
Scraped Within 48 Hours
Every state filing is indexed by data brokers, debt collectors, marketers, and private investigators.
♻
You Can't Unring the Bell
Public records are cached forever. The only real fix is not appearing in them to begin with.

06
Solution · Guided
Package Finder Wizard — All 5 Steps + Result (shown statically)
What it is (live behavior)
An interactive 2-branch decision tree rendered as one card. In production it starts collapsed as a CTA bar, expands on click, and advances one step at a time with a dot progress indicator and auto-advance (no "Continue" button). This wireframe shows all steps unfolded vertically so the flow is legible at a glance.
Why right after pillar cards
Pillar cards are for researchers; the wizard is for deciders. Pairing them right after each other lets every visitor self-select without backtracking. The wizard is the site's primary conversion instrument — surfacing it before packages is deliberate: we want a recommendation, not a pricing comparison, to be the first sale touchpoint.
Content purpose
2 clicks → result for Asset Protection path (fastest). 3 clicks → result for Formation + Virtual. 4 clicks → result for Formation + Brick-and-Mortar (longest). The privacy question auto-determines tier (Gold if yes, Silver/Bronze if no). AP path auto-recommends Gold (no privacy question needed). Recommendation cards show primary + 2 alternatives with specific reasons for each.

Appendix
Conversion Funnel Summary
Full flow
Hook → Trust → Agitate → Educate → Guided Solution → Process → Transparent Pricing → Why Us → Social Proof → Close. Each section earns the right to the next.
Two conversion paths
Guided: Hero CTA → Wizard → Result → Checkout (fewest decisions).
Transparent: Hero → Pillar Cards → Comparison → Tier page → Checkout (researchers).
Why this order
Hook before agitate, agitate before educate, educate before price, price before justification, justification before proof, proof before close. Swapping any of these makes the following section feel premature.
