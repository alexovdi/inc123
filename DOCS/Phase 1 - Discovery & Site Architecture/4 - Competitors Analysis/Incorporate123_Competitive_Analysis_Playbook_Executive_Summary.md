# Competitive Website Analysis Playbook — Executive Summary

**Project:** Incorporate123 Website Redesign  
**Prepared by:** BIT Studios  
**Date:** February 2026  
**Scope:** Top 5 competitor analysis across site structure, SEO positioning, and package presentation

---

## Objective

Systematically analyze the websites of NCH Inc, Anderson Advisors, ShieldCorp, Wyoming Corporate Services, and ZenBusiness (benchmark) to inform Incorporate123's repositioning from "Nevada incorporation company" to **Privacy, Asset Protection & Business Formation Specialists.**

Every data point collected must answer one question: *How should this influence our site design, structure, or content?*

---

## Three Analysis Dimensions

### 1. Site Structure Analysis

Reverse-engineer each competitor's information architecture, navigation patterns, page hierarchy, and internal linking strategy.

**Key questions to answer:**

- How does each competitor categorize services — by state, entity type, or use case?
- What is the ratio of transactional pages to educational content?
- How many clicks from homepage to "start your formation"?
- What state-specific page architecture exists?

**Method:** Crawl each site with Screaming Frog, extract all URLs/titles/headings/internal links, map hierarchy from URL folder paths, and document navigation structure. Supplement with Sitebulb for visual crawl maps and manual 15–20 minute browsing sessions per competitor.

**Deliverable:** Side-by-side master spreadsheet (indexed pages, max depth, CMS, content categories) plus color-coded visual sitemaps.

### 2. SEO Positioning Analysis

Identify keyword gaps, content gaps, backlink opportunities, and SERP feature ownership to shape the new content architecture.

**Key questions to answer:**

- Which privacy/asset protection keywords do competitors rank for that Incorporate123 doesn't?
- What content topics does no competitor cover well (blue-ocean opportunities)?
- What's the domain authority gap, and what's the backlink acquisition target?
- Who owns featured snippets and AI Overviews for target queries?

**Method:** Run SEMrush Keyword Gap (5 domains simultaneously) or Ahrefs Content Gap. Categorize gaps as Missing, Weak, or Shared. Prioritize using Impact × Confidence × Ease scoring. Conduct link intersect analysis for outreach targets.

**Deliverable:** Keyword gap matrix organized by search intent, content gap inventory with quality scores, backlink gap analysis with acquisition targets, and SERP feature ownership map.

### 3. Package/Pricing Presentation Analysis

Deconstruct how competitors present pricing tiers, feature comparisons, CTAs, trust signals, and conversion funnels.

**Key questions to answer:**

- What pricing architecture does each competitor use (loss-leader vs. consultative vs. transparent)?
- How do competitors guide users toward the recommended tier?
- What trust signals appear on pricing and checkout pages?
- What is the complete conversion funnel from landing to purchase?

**Method:** Apply the five-criteria framework (Clarity, Value Communication, User Guidance, Visual Hierarchy, Trust Elements) to each competitor's pricing page. Screen-record the full purchase journey. Document CTA copy, upsell sequences, and trust signal placement.

**Deliverable:** Pricing architecture comparison matrix, conversion funnel maps, trust signal inventory, and UX scorecards.

## Analysis Frameworks

**Feature Comparison Matrix** — Primary backbone. Score 5 competitors across 12–15 criteria (UX, content quality, SEO, pricing transparency, conversion, trust signals, mobile, performance) on a 1–5 scale with color-coded cells.

**Individual SWOT Per Competitor** — Adapted for redesign context. Strengths = superior UX elements, strong rankings. Weaknesses = confusing navigation, content gaps. Opportunities = uncovered topics, unadopted UX patterns. Threats = emerging competitor design trends.

**Competitive Positioning Map** — Two axes: Privacy/Asset Protection Expertise (low → high) × Digital Experience Quality (basic → advanced). The upper-right quadrant (high expertise + high digital quality) is Incorporate123's target — and likely unoccupied.

**Spider/Radar Charts** — For executive presentations. Plot all competitors across 8–10 dimensions simultaneously. Best for stakeholder deck, not detailed analysis.

**Dimension Weighting:**

- UX & Conversion: 30%
- Content & Messaging: 25%
- SEO: 20%
- Visual Design: 15%
- Technical Performance: 10%

## AI Integration Points

AI accelerates synthesis, not data collection. Never use AI to generate competitive metrics from scratch.

1. **Content & Messaging Analysis** — Paste all 5 competitors' homepage copy into Claude → compare value propositions, messaging themes, target audience signals
2. **SWOT Generation** — Feed collected data per competitor → request redesign-focused SWOT
3. **Pattern Identification** — Share complete analysis notes → surface common patterns, unique features, universal gaps
4. **Report Drafting** — Provide scored matrices + thematic findings → draft executive summary and strategic recommendations

---

## Expected Strategic Findings

Based on preliminary research, three high-leverage findings will likely emerge:

1. **Content Architecture Gap** — Most competitors organize by geography or entity type. None organize primarily by customer intent (privacy, asset protection, compliance). This validates Incorporate123's intent-first pillar architecture.

2. **Keyword & Content Blue Ocean** — Topics like comprehensive anonymous LLC state comparisons, interactive privacy calculators, deep nominee services education, and Corporate Transparency Act impact guides are underserved across the entire competitive landscape.

3. **Pricing Presentation White Space** — Budget providers have polished self-serve UX but no privacy expertise. Premium providers have expertise but dated websites and opaque pricing. The gap — transparent pricing with premium expertise and modern UX — is Incorporate123's positioning sweet spot.
