## 1. Page Purpose & Strategic Role

### 1.1 Primary Goal

Guide visitors through two foundational decisions — entity type (LLC vs. Corporation) and state (Wyoming vs. Nevada) — then route them to the right package. The secondary goal, and the one that makes this page strategically valuable beyond its face-value conversion, is educating formation visitors on *why* they should add privacy — driving the 40%+ Silver-to-Gold upgrade that transforms a commodity purchase into a premium one.

### 1.2 Role Within Site Architecture

Formation is the **third pillar** — labeled "competitive but necessary" in the strategy because this is the space where Incorporate123 goes head-to-head with $0 providers (ZenBusiness, LegalZoom, Bizee). The page cannot win on price. It wins on three things: (1) what's included, (2) privacy as a differentiator budget providers literally cannot offer, and (3) the trust and expertise that 25 years provides.

1. **SEO hub page** — Anchors the Formation content cluster (11 pages). Targets broad formation keywords (form LLC, Wyoming LLC, Nevada incorporation, LLC vs corporation). These are the highest-volume queries in the space — and the most competitive.
2. **Decision engine** — The page's primary UX job is resolving two decisions that block the purchase: entity type and state. Until a visitor knows "I want a Wyoming LLC," they cannot buy anything. This page must get them there efficiently.
3. **Privacy upsell funnel entry** — 40%+ of formation visitors can be upgraded to Gold once they understand what nominee services add. The page must plant that seed naturally within the formation education, not as a separate sales pitch.
4. **Dual-path architecture** — Must serve two distinct personas simultaneously: the Informed Buyer (1–2 page path, already knows what they want, needs fast access to packages) and the Formation Researcher (3–5 page path, needs LLC vs. Corp and WY vs. NV guidance first).

### 1.3 Target Personas

| Persona | Arrival Mindset | What They Need From This Page |
|---------|----------------|-------------------------------|
| **Formation Researcher** (primary) | "I need a business entity but haven't decided LLC vs. Corp or Wyoming vs. Nevada." | Entity type guidance → state comparison → package path |
| **Informed Buyer** (secondary) | "I know I want a Wyoming LLC. Show me pricing." | Fast shortcut to packages — one click from any section |
| **Privacy Upsell Candidate** (tertiary) | "I came here to form an LLC. I didn't know about privacy." | Education on what's included → nominee services cross-sell → Gold |

### 1.4 Success Metrics

- **Primary:** Click-through to Silver package pages (WY/NV Incorporation)
- **Secondary:** Click-through to Gold package pages (visitor upsold to privacy during the formation journey)
- **Tertiary:** Click-through to cluster pages (LLC vs Corp, WY vs NV, state-specific guides)
- **Upsell target:** 40%+ of formation visitors exposed to privacy cross-sell messaging
- **Bounce target:** < 50% (competitive with ZenBusiness-quality UX on a harder keyword set)
- **Time on page target:** 2–4 minutes (shorter than Privacy/AP — formation visitors are more action-oriented)

## 2. Narrative Arc & Section Order

Formation visitors are the most action-oriented of all four pillars. They're not researching a concept (like privacy) or driven by fear (like asset protection). They're executing a decision: "I need to form a business." The page's arc is shorter and more decision-focused — less educational depth, faster path to comparison and conversion.

| # | Section | Narrative Role | Visitor Mindset at Entry |
|---|---------|---------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | Pillar Hero | **Hook** — Validate and orient | "I need to form a business entity. Can you do this?" |
| 2 | Quick-Path Shortcut | **Accelerate** — Catch informed buyers | "I already know what I want — where's the pricing?" |
| 3 | Two Decisions Framework | **Guide** — Entity type + state | "LLC or Corporation? Wyoming or Nevada?" |
| 4 | What's Included | **Differentiate** — Total value vs. competitors | "What do I actually get for the price?" |
| 5 | Cluster Navigation Grid | **Explore** — Route to depth | "I want to learn more about [specific topic]" |
| 6 | Privacy Upsell Bridge | **Reveal** — The upgrade most clients make | "Wait — should I add privacy?" |
| 7 | State Comparison Snapshot | **Compare** — WY vs NV quick view | "Which state is better for my situation?" |
| 8 | Package Preview | **Convert** — Pricing with Gold upgrade visible | "What does this cost?" |
| 9 | Social Proof Strip | **Validate** — Trust signals | "Is this company legit at this price?" |
| 10 | FAQ Accordion | **Resolve** — Handle objections | "What about costs, timeline, taxes..." |
| 11 | Cross-Pillar Bridge | **Expand** — Surface related needs | "What else should I consider?" |
| 12 | Final CTA | **Close** — Drive action | "Let's do this." |

### Why This Order Differs From Privacy and Asset Protection

**Section 2 (Quick-Path Shortcut) is unique to Formation.** Neither Privacy nor AP has this section. Formation is the only pillar that serves visitors who arrive *already knowing what they want*. The Informed Buyer (Journey D) has a 1–2 page path — if the pillar page buries packages under 6 sections of education, these visitors bounce to ZenBusiness. The quick-path shortcut catches them immediately after the hero.

**Section 3 (Two Decisions) replaces the "Problem Statement" and "Framework" sections from Privacy/AP.** Formation visitors don't need to be convinced they have a problem — they already know they need an entity. They need help choosing. The two-decision framework is a decision tree, not an educational arc.

**Section 4 (What's Included) is the competitive differentiator section.** On Privacy/AP, the differentiator section comes later because visitors need education first. On Formation, the "what's included" comparison needs to appear early because it's the primary reason to choose Incorporate123 over a $0 provider. This section directly counters the ZenBusiness/LegalZoom price-anchoring.

**Section 6 (Privacy Upsell Bridge) is unique to Formation.** This is the strategic section that transforms a commodity purchase into a premium one. It appears after the visitor has chosen their entity type and state (Sections 3 + 5) but before they see pricing (Section 8). By the time they reach packages, "add privacy" is already in their mind.

**Section 7 (State Comparison) appears later** than on AP because entity type must be resolved first — the state comparison changes depending on whether the visitor wants an LLC or a corporation.

options, or that benefit from C-Corp taxation at high revenue levels.
→ Full LLC vs. Corporation comparison (/llc-vs-corporation/)

Q: Should I form in Wyoming or Nevada?
A: Wyoming for most businesses. Lower annual costs ($50/yr vs. $500+/yr),
stronger single-member LLC protection, and no public disclosure of LLC
members. Nevada excels for multi-member entities needing the deepest
case law history and for corporations seeking strong director protections.
→ Full Wyoming vs. Nevada comparison (/wyoming-vs-nevada/)

Q: Do I need to live in Wyoming or Nevada to form there?
A: No. Any U.S. or international resident can form a business entity
in Wyoming or Nevada regardless of where they live or operate. Your
registered agent (included in every package) provides the in-state
address required by law. If you operate your business in another state
(like California or Florida), you may also need to file a foreign
registration in that state.
→ Foreign state registration guide (/foreign-state-registration/)

Q: What about taxes? Will I pay Wyoming/Nevada taxes or my home state taxes?
A: Wyoming and Nevada have no state income tax, which eliminates state-level
corporate taxation on your entity. However, you still pay federal taxes
and any taxes in the state where you physically operate or have employees.
Forming in a no-tax state does not eliminate your personal income tax
obligations in your home state. We recommend consulting a CPA for your
specific tax situation.
→ Entity Tax Guide (/entity-tax-guide/)

Q: What is a shelf company and when should I consider one?
A: A shelf company is a pre-formed entity that has been "on the shelf"
for months or years. The primary advantage is established entity age,
which can improve credibility with certain banks, vendors, or partners.
Shelf companies cost more than new formations but provide immediate
access to an aged entity with a clean history.
→ Shelf companies available (/shelf-companies/)
→ Shelf vs. new formation comparison (/shelf-company-vs-new-formation/)

Q: What happens after formation?
A: After formation, your entity requires ongoing compliance: annual
reports filed with the state, registered agent service maintained,
and corporate records kept current. All Incorporate123 packages
include first-year registered agent service. Annual renewals ($325–$725/yr
depending on package and state) cover ongoing compliance so nothing
lapses.
→ Compliance services (/compliance/)

Q: Can I add privacy (nominee services) later?
A: Yes. You can start with a Silver package and upgrade to Gold at any
time. The upgrade adds year-round nominee services, offshore records
custodian, and corporate minutes maintenance. However, adding nominees
after formation requires amending existing state filings, which involves
additional paperwork and fees. Starting with Gold is simpler and more
cost-effective if you anticipate wanting privacy.

Q: What's included in the annual renewal?
A: Silver renewal ($325 WY / $525 NV) includes registered agent service
and state filing fee coverage. Gold renewal ($525 WY / $725 NV) includes
everything in Silver plus year-round nominee maintenance, offshore records
custodian, and corporate minutes. For comparison, competitors charge
$400–$5,000/year for nominee services alone — on top of registered agent
and filing fees.
