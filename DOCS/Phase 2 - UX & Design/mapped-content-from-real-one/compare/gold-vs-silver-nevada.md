The critical difference is privacy: Gold includes year-round nominee officers and directors plus offshore record storage. Silver does not. In Nevada, this distinction matters more than in any other state because Nevada requires public disclosure of officers and managers — without nominees, your name goes directly into a searchable public database.

## Why Privacy Matters More in Nevada

This is the most important point of this comparison. Nevada requires an Initial List of Officers and Managers to be filed with the Secretary of State within 30 days of formation. This list is publicly searchable on the Nevada SOS website. Every year, a new annual list is filed, and it is also publicly searchable.

**With Silver (no nominees),** your name and address appear on the Initial List and every annual list. Anyone — a potential plaintiff, a business competitor, a curious neighbor, a data broker — can search the Nevada Secretary of State website and find your name, your address, and your role in the entity. This information cannot be removed after it has been filed.

**With Gold (nominees),** our nominee officers and directors appear on every filing. Your name never enters the Nevada database. The privacy protection is complete and continuous.

In Wyoming, a Silver formation still provides reasonable baseline privacy because the state does not collect officer/manager information. In Nevada, Silver means your information is public. This makes the Gold/Silver decision in Nevada more consequential than in Wyoming.

## What Gold Adds Over Silver

The $525 price difference (LLC) buys three privacy services that are particularly important given Nevada's disclosure requirements.

**Year-round nominee directors and officers.** Nominees appear on the Initial List, every annual list, and any other state filing that requires officer or director information. Your name never enters the public record at any point during the life of the entity.

**Offshore record storage.** Corporate records — including the operating agreement, membership list, meeting minutes, and stock ledger — are maintained outside U.S. jurisdiction. This adds protection against domestic discovery requests seeking to identify the entity's beneficial owners.

**Custom operating agreement included.** A professionally drafted operating agreement or bylaws, tailored to your entity, is included in Gold at no additional cost. Silver offers this as a $99 add-on.

## Who Should Choose Gold

In Nevada, the Gold package is the right choice for a broader set of clients than in Wyoming because of the state's mandatory public disclosure requirements.

- **Anyone who values ownership privacy.** If you do not want your name searchable on the Nevada SOS website, Gold is the only option that prevents it.
- **Out-of-state business owners.** If you are forming in Nevada but live elsewhere, nominee services prevent your home state from identifying you through Nevada public records.
- **Real estate investors.** Property ownership through an LLC provides liability protection, but the benefit is reduced if the LLC's ownership is publicly searchable. Gold keeps the ownership chain private.
- **Business owners with asset protection concerns.** Privacy and protection work together — Gold provides both.

## Who Should Choose Silver

Choose Nevada Silver only if you have made a deliberate, informed decision that public ownership disclosure is acceptable.

- **Nevada-based business owners with a public-facing business.** If you operate a retail store, restaurant, or professional practice in Nevada where your identity is already publicly known, the privacy gap may not matter.
- **Entities formed for a specific, non-sensitive purpose.** If the entity is for a straightforward business venture where ownership is not sensitive information.
- **Budget-constrained formations.** Silver saves $525 at formation and $200/year on renewals. Over 5 years, the total savings is approximately $1,325.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I upgrade from Silver to Gold later?**
Yes, but your name will have already appeared on at least one public filing (the Initial List). We can file an amended list with nominee names going forward, but the original filing remains in the Nevada SOS records. Starting with Gold avoids this exposure entirely.

**Why is Nevada Gold more expensive than Wyoming Gold?**
Nevada charges higher state fees: the Initial List ($150/year) and State Business License (LLC $200/year, Corp $500/year) do not exist in Wyoming. These costs are included in both Gold and Silver Nevada packages.

**If I choose Silver, can my information be found online?**
Yes. The Nevada Secretary of State maintains a free, publicly searchable database. Your name, address, and role (officer, director, manager) will be findable by anyone with internet access.

**Is there a Bronze option for Nevada with privacy?**
The Nevada Bronze package ($699 LLC) does not include privacy services. However, you can discuss adding nominee services to a Bronze formation by calling us. The standalone nominee add-on pricing may make Gold more cost-effective overall.

**What if I need Nevada for a specific business license?**
If a Nevada state or local license requires a Nevada entity, then forming in Nevada is necessary. In that case, Gold is the recommended package to maintain privacy on all state filings associated with the entity.

**Do both packages include the Nevada Business License?**
Yes. Both Gold and Silver include the state-level Nevada Business License in the formation price. This is separate from any county or city license requirements.

**How long does Nevada formation take for either package?**
Both Gold and Silver follow the same timeline: standard processing takes 3-5 business days for the articles, with the complete package (including Initial List and Business License) typically delivered within 5-7 business days. Rush filing ($199) provides 24-hour processing.

**Can I add a virtual office to either package?**
Yes. The Virtual Office add-on ($149/year) provides a professional Reno, NV business address with mail forwarding and is available with both Gold and Silver packages.

**What happens if I choose Silver and then someone finds my information?**
Once your name has been filed on the Initial List, it remains in the Nevada Secretary of State's historical records. Upgrading to Gold replaces your name with nominees on future filings, but the original filing cannot be retroactively removed.
