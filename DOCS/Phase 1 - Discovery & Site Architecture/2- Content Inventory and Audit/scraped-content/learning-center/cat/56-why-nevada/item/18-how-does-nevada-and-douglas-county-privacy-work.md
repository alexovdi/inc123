How does Nevada and Douglas County Privacy work?

The Stockholders of a Nevada Corporation or the Members of a (Manager managed) Limited Liability Company (LLC) are not listed in the Secretary of State records! Whether you are an attorney or the IRS, you cannot determine the owner by contacting the Secretary of State. Additionally Nevada law prevents anyone (including creditors) from accessing the corporation's stock ledger. The penalty in Nevada for using stockholder information for any purpose other than to have a stockholder defend his interest in the company is up to one year in jail and a $2,000 fine.
