## What Is an Anonymous Corporation?

Like an anonymous LLC, an "anonymous corporation" is not a separate legal entity type. It is a standard C-corporation or S-corporation formed with nominee officers and directors so that the actual owner's name does not appear in public state filings.

In a typical corporation formation, the articles of incorporation list the incorporator, and subsequent filings with the Secretary of State list directors and officers. These names become part of the public record. With an anonymous corporation, each of these positions is held by a nominee — our team member whose name appears in the state database instead of yours.

You maintain full control and ownership through two mechanisms. First, you hold all issued stock (or a controlling interest) through confidential stock certificates that are not filed with any state agency. Second, you operate the company from a non-public executive position — typically CEO or Executive Vice President — which gives you complete operational authority without appearing in state records.

## Why Choose a Corporation Over an LLC?

Most privacy-focused formations use the LLC structure, and for good reason: LLCs are simpler, more flexible, and have fewer public disclosure requirements. However, there are specific situations where the corporate form is necessary or advantageous.

**Investor expectations.** Venture capital firms and institutional investors typically require a C-corporation structure. If you plan to raise outside capital, the corporate form may be a prerequisite.

**Industry requirements.** Certain regulated industries — banking, insurance, some professional services — require or strongly prefer the corporate form.

**Stock issuance.** If you need to issue stock to employees, advisors, or partners, a corporation provides the established framework for equity compensation (stock options, restricted stock, etc.).

**Established credibility.** Some business relationships — government contracts, international partnerships — carry an expectation of the corporate form.

In each of these scenarios, you can still maintain full privacy through nominee services. The corporate form does not require you to sacrifice anonymity.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the difference between an anonymous LLC and an anonymous corporation?**
The privacy mechanisms are the same — nominee services keep your name out of public records in both cases. The difference is the underlying entity structure. Corporations have directors, officers, and shareholders; LLCs have managers and members. Most business owners prefer the LLC for its simplicity and flexibility, but corporations are necessary when raising outside capital or operating in certain regulated industries.

**Can I issue stock in an anonymous corporation?**
Yes. Stock certificates are private documents — they are not filed with the state. You issue stock to yourself or others through the corporation's internal records, which are maintained by the offshore custodian. The stock ledger records ownership but is not part of the public record.

**Will investors know about the nominee structure?**
You will disclose the structure to investors as part of due diligence — this is standard practice. The anonymity applies to public state records, not private business negotiations. Investors generally understand and accept nominee structures, particularly if the corporation is domiciled in Wyoming or Nevada.

**Can I elect S-corp tax treatment with an anonymous corporation?**
Yes. S-corp election (Form 2553) is a federal tax matter between your corporation and the IRS. It does not affect the nominee structure or the privacy of your state filings. The IRS knows who owns the corporation; the general public does not.

**Are nominee directors liable for the corporation's actions?**
Our nominees serve in a limited capacity — they do not participate in business operations, decision-making, or management. The nominee role is specifically structured to provide a name for public filings without creating operational involvement or liability exposure.

**What industries cannot use nominee services?**
We do not provide nominee services for companies in specially licensed or regulated industries in Nevada or Wyoming, including insurance, mortgage lending, law practice, and gaming. If you are unsure whether your business activity is subject to special regulation, contact us before placing your order.

**How does the offshore custodian protect my stock ledger?**
Under NRS 78.105, corporations may designate a custodian for the stock ledger and provide the custodian's name and address to the registered agent. Our offshore company secretary serves as custodian, so any request for stock ledger records — whether from the Secretary of State or through a third-party subpoena — is directed to the offshore custodian, outside U.S. court jurisdiction.
