Nominee Services - Maintain Your Anonymity

There is a tremendous amount of confusion and misinformation about nominee services and what they are designed to do. Simply put, a nominee holds an office in your corporation or limited liability company that will appear in the public record. In this way you can preserve the anonymity of the assets or businesses that you control. The less the public knows about your affairs, the less engaged they can become.  
  
There are also very different levels of "nominee service": In Nevada, the most common type is "nominee for a day" where a nominee is appointed in the morning, the annual state filing is completed by lunch, and by dinner the nominee has resigned. Commonly $500.00 is charged for the one day "rental" of the nominee.  
  
In the offshore world, this type of service is considered laughable! There, the nominee serves year round, is available to execute various documents and even speak with third parties if absolutely necessary. This is the level of nominee service that we have chosen to provide and yet our annual fee can be as low as $525.00 when purchased as part of a package. We do charge for "special projects" and "document execution", although only a small percentage of our clients actually request this.

Offshore Custodian of stock ledger

As a result of the passing of [SB60 2013](/learning-center/sb60_2013_changes), Nevada Statutes [(NRS 78.105)](/learning-center/nrs-78.105) and [(NRS 86.241)](/learning-center/nrs-86.241) require all Entities to maintain records at their Registered Office and all Nevada Registered Agents to maintain records on their Corporate clients. These requirements now include maintaining copies of Operating Agreements/Bylaws as well as the Stock Ledger and/or Membership Listing for an LLC. In lieu of the stock ledger the corporation may keep a statement setting out the name of the custodian of the stock ledger, and the complete mailing or street address where the stock ledger is kept. Registered Agents now face fines for non-compliance as well as their company registration being revoked [NRS 77.443](/learning-center/nrs-77.443).  
  
Effective October 1, 2013 [NRS 86.246](/learning-center/nrs-86.246) the Secretary of State may now request copies of Operating Agreements/Bylaws as well as the Stock Ledger and/or Membership Listing from the Registered Agent....... But don't worry! The Secretary Of State promises to keep the information confidential, and only release it in certain circumstances and to certain parties [NRS 76.160](/learning-center/nrs-76.160) ... such as...The I.R.S., any agency of any state charged with the administration or enforcement of laws relating to workers’ compensation, unemployment compensation, public assistance, taxation, labor or gaming and to any law enforcement agency in the course of a criminal investigation, or to any agency or political subdivision of any State or the United States in the course of an enforcement action......Additionally, many other Government Agencies continue to use [3rd Party Record Keeper Summons](/services/3rdpartyrecordkeepersummons) to obtain this ownership information from the Registered Agent. The Registered Agent is an easy target since every company must have one, and they must be listed in the public record and must be available to receive service of process.  
  
OUR SOLUTION: - In line with our [philosophy](/about-us/philosophy) and our [internationalization](/about-us/internationalization) we have improved our services to include an [Offshore Custodian of Stock Ledger](/services/offshore-custodian): We provide an offshore company secretary in all our service packages as this is integral to our records preparation and filing service and the secretary is appointed as the custodian of the stock ledger or membership listing.  If the Secretary of State were to request the records (per NRS 86.246), it will simply receive a statement setting out the name of the custodian of the stock ledger, and the complete mailing or street address where the stock ledger/membership listing is kept. In this case by the (offshore) Secretary of your Company. The Bylaws and Operating Agreement (also improved) contain no personal information, just the rules and Regulations regarding the Company management. The capitalization and membership of the LLC is listed in a separate document that is not a part of the Operating Agreement.
