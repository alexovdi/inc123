Does Nevada have a State corporate income tax?

There is no Nevada State tax based on income. There are no taxes levied on services. For companies with no employees performing services within Nevada there is only a $100 per year tax. (effective July 1, 2009 - June 30, 2011 temporarily increased to $200.00). Effective October 1, 2009, the Business License Fee will be administered and collected by the Secretary of State's office.There is no tax return due, since the Tax is called a "state business license fee."  

For companies active in Nevada (such as ours) there is a small payroll tax of 0.5 percent of the total payroll amount per calender quarter that does not exceed $62,500, plus 1.17 percent of the actual payroll amount paid in excess of $62,500 per calender quarter. Other distributions/dividends etc. are not subject to this tax.  
  
Additionaly Nevada has no franchise tax, stock transfer tax, capital gains tax, personal income tax, inventory tax, tax on corporate shares, inheritance tax, gift tax, estate tax or minimum tax! However to take advantage of this your entity must have a [base of operations](/services/nevada-office) in Nevada.
