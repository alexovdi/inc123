For business owners who prioritize privacy and asset protection, a Wyoming LLC is the default starting point.

## Why Wyoming for Your LLC

### Lowest Formation and Maintenance Costs

Wyoming charges a $100 filing fee to form an LLC and just $62 per year for the annual report. There is no state income tax, no franchise tax, and no gross receipts tax. Compare this to Nevada's $425 filing fee and $350+ in annual state costs, or Delaware's $300 annual franchise tax for LLCs. Wyoming is the most affordable state in the country for both formation and ongoing maintenance.

### Strongest Charging Order Protection

Wyoming was the first state to adopt the LLC statute in 1977 and has consistently strengthened its protections since then. Under Wyoming law, a charging order is the exclusive remedy available to a judgment creditor of an LLC member. This means a creditor who obtains a judgment against you personally cannot seize your LLC assets, force distributions, or take over management of your LLC. The creditor can only receive distributions if and when the LLC chooses to make them.

This protection applies to both single-member and multi-member LLCs in Wyoming. Many other states, including California and Florida, do not extend charging order protection to single-member LLCs, creating a significant vulnerability for solo business owners.

### No State Income Tax

Wyoming imposes no personal income tax and no corporate income tax. LLC income passes through to your personal tax return and is subject only to federal taxes and the taxes of your state of residence (if applicable). For business owners in no-income-tax states such as Texas, Florida, or Washington, a Wyoming LLC means zero state-level tax on business income.

### Privacy Protections

Wyoming does not require member or manager names on the articles of organization filed with the Secretary of State. The only name on the public record is the registered agent. Combined with Incorporate123's nominee services (available in our Gold package), your ownership and management of the LLC can remain completely off public record.

### No Residency Requirement

You do not need to live in Wyoming, have an office in Wyoming, or conduct business in Wyoming to form an LLC there. Business owners from all 50 states and internationally form Wyoming LLCs as holding companies, investment vehicles, and operating entities.

## The Operating Agreement: Your LLC's Foundation

Every LLC needs an operating agreement, even single-member LLCs. This document establishes the rules that govern your company, and it is your primary defense against a court treating your LLC as a mere alter ego of yourself.

Your operating agreement covers:

- **Ownership structure** -- percentage interests and capital contributions of each member
- **Management framework** -- whether the LLC is member-managed or manager-managed, and who has authority to make binding decisions
- **Profit and loss allocation** -- how earnings and losses are distributed among members
- **Voting rights and procedures** -- what decisions require member approval and what threshold applies
- **Transfer restrictions** -- the rules governing how membership interests can be sold, assigned, or transferred
- **Charging order protections** -- provisions that ensure creditors remain assignees only, without management or voting rights
- **Dissolution procedures** -- what triggers dissolution and how assets are distributed

The operating agreement is where charging order protection becomes real and enforceable. In the landmark Nevada case *Paladin Commerce Center v. Thalden*, a bankruptcy court held that even a creditor who obtained 100% of an LLC's membership interest could not gain management or voting rights because the operating agreement required manager consent to admit a substitute member. The takeaway is clear: a properly drafted operating agreement can prevent a creditor from ever controlling your LLC, even if they have a judgment for the full membership interest.

## Foreign Registration: When You Need It

If you form a Wyoming LLC but conduct business in another state (such as your home state), that state may require you to file a foreign registration. Foreign registration means your Wyoming LLC registers as a "foreign" entity authorized to do business in the other state. This typically involves a one-time filing fee and an annual report in the second state.

Foreign registration does not change your Wyoming LLC's governance or protections. Your operating agreement, charging order protections, and privacy benefits remain governed by Wyoming law. The foreign state simply requires registration for tax collection and legal service purposes.
