# Wyoming Gold vs Silver: Which Package Do You Need?

The Wyoming Gold and Silver packages both form a legal Wyoming LLC or Corporation with registered agent, compliance services, and all state fees included. The difference is privacy. Gold includes year-round nominee officers and directors plus offshore record storage, which keeps your name entirely off the Wyoming public record. Silver does not include any privacy features — your name will appear on state filings. This comparison helps you determine which level of protection matches your situation.

## What You Get with Gold That Silver Does Not Include

The $400 difference between Gold and Silver (LLC pricing) buys three specific privacy services.

**Year-round nominee directors and officers.** Our nominees are listed on your articles of organization and every subsequent annual report as the officers and directors (Corporation) or managers (LLC) of your entity. This means that anyone searching the Wyoming Secretary of State database will find nominee names — not yours. This protection is continuous, not just at formation. Every time the state record is updated, nominees remain in place.

**Offshore record storage.** Your corporate record book — including the operating agreement, membership list, meeting minutes, and any stock or membership certificates — is maintained outside U.S. jurisdiction. This adds a layer of protection against domestic subpoenas or court orders that might seek to identify the beneficial owners of your entity. The records are maintained professionally and are accessible to you at any time.

**Custom operating agreement included.** Gold includes a professionally drafted operating agreement tailored to your entity at no additional cost. Silver offers this as a $99 add-on.

## Who Should Choose Gold

Choose Wyoming Gold if any of the following apply to your situation:

- **You want your name off public records.** If anyone searching the Wyoming Secretary of State website should not be able to find your name associated with this entity, you need Gold.
- **You have assets to protect.** Privacy and asset protection work together. Privacy prevents potential litigants from discovering your asset structure. Gold provides both.
- **You operate a business from your home state.** If you are forming in Wyoming but operating in another state, nominee services prevent your home state from identifying you as the manager/officer of a Wyoming entity through public record searches.
- **You value peace of mind.** Many clients choose Gold simply because they prefer to keep their business ownership private as a matter of principle, even if they do not have a specific threat.

## Who Should Choose Silver

Choose Wyoming Silver if any of the following apply:

- **You are comfortable with your name on public records.** If ownership privacy is not a concern — for example, if you are forming an entity for a business venture where your identity is already publicly known — Silver provides a professional formation at a lower price.
- **You are physically located in Wyoming.** Business owners operating from within Wyoming who do not need privacy from local searches may not need nominee services.
- **Budget is the primary concern.** Silver saves $400 at formation and $200/year on renewals. Over 5 years, the total savings is approximately $1,200 compared to Gold.
- **You plan to upgrade later.** If you are not sure whether you need privacy now but want to get your entity formed, starting with Silver and upgrading to Gold later is possible (though starting with Gold is more cost-effective).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I upgrade from Silver to Gold later?**
Yes. We can add nominee services to an existing entity. However, this requires filing amendments with the Wyoming Secretary of State to replace your name with nominee names, which incurs additional fees. Your name will have already appeared on at least one public filing, and while it can be replaced going forward, historical filings remain on record.

**Does the entity itself differ between Gold and Silver?**
No. The LLC or Corporation formed under either package has identical legal standing. The difference is in the services surrounding the entity — specifically, who appears on the public record as officers/directors and where the corporate records are stored.

**What if I only need nominees for formation, not year-round?**
Some services use nominees only for the initial filing and then replace them with the actual owner. We do not recommend this approach because it defeats the purpose — your name will appear on the first annual report. Gold provides year-round nominees precisely to avoid this gap.

**Is the operating agreement really important?**
Yes. The operating agreement is the foundational legal document for your LLC. It defines management structure, distribution rules, transfer restrictions, and dissolution procedures. While not technically required in Wyoming, operating without one leaves your LLC governed entirely by Wyoming default statutes, which may not match your intentions.

**What does "offshore record storage" actually protect against?**
It adds a jurisdictional barrier to domestic discovery requests. If a U.S. court issues a subpoena for your corporate records, the records being held outside U.S. jurisdiction creates an additional procedural step that a plaintiff must navigate.

**Are there situations where Gold is not enough?**
For advanced asset protection strategies — such as multi-entity structures, domestic asset protection trusts, or complex holding company arrangements — Gold is typically the starting point, not the complete solution. Contact us to discuss whether your situation requires additional structuring.
