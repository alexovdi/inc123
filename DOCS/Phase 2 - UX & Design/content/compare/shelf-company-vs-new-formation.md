## When a Shelf Company Makes Sense

Shelf companies serve specific, legitimate business purposes. Here are the scenarios where the premium is justified.

**Contract and vendor requirements.** Some contracts — particularly in government contracting, real estate, and professional services — require the business entity to have been in existence for a minimum number of years. A shelf company meets this requirement immediately because the entity was legally formed at an earlier date and has been continuously maintained in good standing since then.

**Financial institution requirements.** Certain banks, lending programs, and merchant account providers use entity age as one factor in their approval process. An entity that has existed for two or three years may receive different treatment than one formed last week. This is not universal — most banks do not have minimum age requirements — but where it applies, a shelf company addresses the issue.

**Industry credibility.** In some industries, potential clients, partners, or investors check the formation date of an entity as a basic due diligence step. An entity formed several years ago may carry more weight than one formed recently. This is a perception issue, not a legal one, but perception matters in business.

**Immediate availability for time-sensitive transactions.** If you need an entity with established standing for a transaction that is closing soon, a shelf company provides that immediately rather than requiring you to wait for the entity to age naturally.

## When a New Formation Is the Better Choice

For the majority of business formations, a new entity is the right choice. Here is why.

**Cost.** A Wyoming Gold LLC costs $1,275. A shelf company LLC starts at $2,500. The $1,225 premium buys entity age and nothing else — the services, protections, and privacy features are identical. Unless you have a specific, documented need for entity age, this premium does not provide additional value.

**Entity name flexibility.** With a new formation, you choose your entity name. With a shelf company, you are limited to what is available in our inventory. You can rename a shelf company after purchase, but the amendment process adds cost and time.

**No history misconceptions.** A shelf company does not come with revenue, credit history, business relationships, or operational track record. It is a dormant entity with a formation date. If your goal is to appear as if the business has been operating for years, a shelf company does not achieve that — and attempting to represent it as such would be misleading.

**Most requirements do not mandate entity age.** The vast majority of contracts, bank accounts, merchant accounts, and business relationships have no minimum entity age requirement. Before purchasing a shelf company, verify that the specific opportunity you are pursuing actually requires it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does a shelf company come with a credit history or credit score?**
No. A shelf company has never conducted any financial transactions. It has no credit history, no bank accounts, and no Dun & Bradstreet rating. Building business credit begins after you take ownership and start operating.

**Can I rename the shelf company after purchase?**
Yes. We can file a name amendment with the Wyoming Secretary of State. This adds a small fee and takes a few business days. The original formation date is preserved.

**What ages are available?**
We maintain inventory of 1 to 5-year-old entities. Availability varies by age and entity type (LLC vs Corporation). Contact us for current inventory.

**Are shelf companies legal?**
Yes. Purchasing and transferring a pre-formed entity is a standard, legal business practice. The entity has been properly formed, maintained in good standing, and never used for any business activity. The transfer is documented through corporate resolutions or operating agreement amendments.

**Will anyone know I purchased a shelf company?**
The ownership transfer is reflected in the corporate records and potentially in state filings (if officer/director names change). With nominee services in place, the public record shows only nominee names both before and after the transfer. The transfer itself is not flagged or labeled as a shelf company purchase in any public database.

**Can I get a shelf company in Nevada instead of Wyoming?**
Contact us to discuss availability. Our standard shelf company inventory is maintained in Wyoming because of its lower costs and stronger privacy framework. We may be able to accommodate Nevada shelf companies depending on current inventory.

**What if I buy a shelf company and then don't need the age?**
Standard terms apply — there is no return or downgrade option once the entity has been transferred. This is why we recommend verifying that you actually need entity age before purchasing.

**Do shelf companies include nominee services?**
Yes. Our shelf companies include the full Gold-tier service package: year-round nominee officers and directors, offshore record storage, registered agent, annual compliance, and corporate minutes maintenance.

**Can I use a shelf company to get business credit faster?**
Entity age is one factor some lenders consider, but it does not substitute for actual credit history, revenue, or financial statements. Building business credit still requires opening accounts, establishing trade lines, and demonstrating payment history after you take ownership.

**How do I verify whether my situation requires entity age?**
Review the specific contract, loan application, or vendor requirement that mentions entity age. Look for language such as "minimum years in business" or "entity must have been established for at least X years." If the requirement exists, call us to discuss whether a shelf company is the appropriate solution.
