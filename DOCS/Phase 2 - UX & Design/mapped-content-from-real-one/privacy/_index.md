# Business Privacy

Your name, home address, and personal details become public record the moment you form a business in most states. Anyone with an internet connection — competitors, disgruntled customers, data brokers, litigation attorneys — can search the Secretary of State database and find out exactly who owns and operates your company. For business owners with real estate holdings, investment portfolios, or simply a preference for keeping their affairs private, this exposure creates tangible risk.

Business privacy is not about hiding from legitimate obligations. It is about structuring your entity so that the public record does not broadcast your personal information to the world. Over the past 25 years, Incorporate123 has helped thousands of business owners form and maintain entities where their names never appear in state filings — legally, ethically, and with full compliance.

## Who Needs Business Privacy?

Business privacy is not a niche concern. It is relevant to anyone who wants to separate their personal identity from their business activities in the public record.

- **Real estate investors** who do not want tenants, opposing counsel, or the general public connecting properties to a single owner
- **Entrepreneurs with personal wealth** who want to reduce their visibility as litigation targets
- **E-commerce and online business owners** who prefer not to publish their home address in state databases
- **Professionals in sensitive fields** — physicians, attorneys, executives — who deal with the public and want a layer of separation
- **Multi-entity operators** who manage several businesses and want to keep ownership structures confidential
- **Anyone moving to or operating in a new state** who wants to start fresh without a public paper trail connecting them to prior business activity

## The Privacy + Asset Protection Connection

Privacy and asset protection work together. Privacy keeps your name and ownership details out of the public record, making it harder for potential plaintiffs to identify what you own. Asset protection — through structures like Wyoming's charging order protection and series LLCs — makes it harder for a judgment creditor to reach the assets inside your entities, even if they discover them.

The strongest structures combine both. A Wyoming LLC with nominee services provides privacy at the state level, while the Wyoming LLC Act's charging order protections provide one of the strongest asset shields available in any U.S. jurisdiction. ## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is it legal to use nominee services?**
Yes. Nominee services are a well-established legal practice used worldwide. The nominee holds a position of record on your behalf — you retain full ownership and control. This is not about evading legal obligations; it is about keeping your personal details out of publicly searchable databases.

**Will the IRS know who really owns my company?**
Yes. Your company's tax returns are filed under your Social Security Number or EIN, and you report all income as required. Privacy services affect public state records, not federal tax filings. The IRS knows exactly who owns the entity.

**What is the difference between a nominee and a registered agent?**
A registered agent receives legal documents and state correspondence on behalf of your company. A nominee actually holds a titled position — officer, director, or manager — so that your name does not appear in state filings. We provide both services in our packages.

**Do offshore nominees create foreign tax reporting requirements?**
No. The appointment of an offshore individual as a nominee officer does not create any offshore tax reporting obligations, FBAR requirements, or special IRS filings. The entity remains a domestic U.S. entity for all tax purposes.

**Can I use nominee services if I operate in California or Florida?**
Yes. We form your entity in Wyoming with full nominee services, then file a foreign registration in your home state. Your name appears in neither state's records. We have dedicated packages for both [California](/california-private-incorporation/) and [Florida](/florida-private-incorporation/).

**What is an offshore custodian of records?**
Under Nevada statute (NRS 86.246), the Secretary of State can request membership lists and stock ledgers from registered agents. Our solution: we appoint an offshore company secretary as custodian of these records. If the state requests them, it receives the name and address of the offshore custodian — not your personal ownership information. This service has been included free in all Gold packages since 2012.

**How much do nominee services cost?**

The renewal includes nominees, registered agent, and ongoing compliance support. Competitors typically charge $400 to $5,000 per year for nominee services alone.
