# Hong Kong Limited Company

Hong Kong has consistently ranked at or near the top of the Heritage Foundation's Index of Economic Freedom. As a Special Administrative Region (SAR) of the People's Republic of China with its own legislature, executive, and judicial system, Hong Kong has evolved into one of Asia's most important financial and investment centers. For clients seeking access to Asian banking, markets, and business networks, a Hong Kong Limited Company provides credibility and practical advantages that Caribbean jurisdictions cannot replicate.

## Why Hong Kong?

Hong Kong offers a combination of business freedom, favorable taxation, and institutional credibility:

- **Territorial Tax System** — Companies generating revenue from operations outside Hong Kong typically pay zero profits tax. There is no sales tax, VAT, capital gains tax, dividend tax, or withholding tax.
- **Low Domestic Tax Rate** — Profits generated within Hong Kong are taxed at just 16.5%, among the lowest rates in the region.
- **Not Widely Viewed as a Tax Haven** — Despite low tax rates, Hong Kong maintains a reputation as a legitimate business center, duty-free port, and regional financial hub. This credibility matters when dealing with banks, business partners, and regulatory bodies.
- **Free Flow of Capital** — No exchange controls. Capital moves in and out of Hong Kong without restriction.
- **Minimal Licensing** — Foreign investment and ownership are permitted in almost all sectors, with limited exceptions such as banking, broadcasting, and publishing.
- **Pass-Through Tax Election Available** — U.S. persons can elect Foreign Disregarded Entity or Foreign Partnership status to simplify IRS reporting.

## Important Cost Consideration

Hong Kong is an excellent jurisdiction, but it is not inexpensive to maintain. Beyond the formation package and annual corporate service fees, every Hong Kong company is required to:

- **Prepare audited accounts** — Even private companies must have their financial statements audited annually by a Hong Kong CPA firm.
- **File a Profits Tax Return (PTR)** — Annual tax filing is mandatory, even if the company earns no Hong Kong-sourced income and owes no tax.

**You should budget approximately $2,000 per year for these CPA services.** This is in addition to your annual renewal fee for registered office, company secretary, and nominee director services.

In Hong Kong, directors are held personally responsible for failure to file timely tax returns. Deposits may be required for CPA services to ensure compliance.

## Practical Applications

**E-commerce and technology.** Hong Kong is a preferred jurisdiction for online businesses, particularly those serving Asian markets. The combination of territorial taxation, no sales tax, and strong payment processing infrastructure makes it practical for digital commerce.

**International trade.** Hong Kong's status as a duty-free port with deep banking relationships makes it well suited for import/export businesses and international trade finance.

**Investment and asset holding.** The zero capital gains and dividend tax environment is attractive for holding investment portfolios, particularly those focused on Asian markets.

**Asset protection.** While Hong Kong provides solid corporate protections, clients whose primary goal is asset protection may find Nevis or BVI more cost-effective due to lower ongoing maintenance costs. Hong Kong is best justified when there is a genuine business or investment reason to operate in the Asian market.

## IRS Compliance for U.S. Persons

Hong Kong entities are eligible for pass-through tax election. We recommend this for most U.S. person clients. The process includes:

- Filing Form SS-4 to obtain an EIN
- Filing Form 8832 to elect Foreign Disregarded Entity or Foreign Partnership status

With pass-through status, your ongoing U.S. obligations typically include:

- **Form 8858** — For single-member FDE status, filed with your 1040
- **Form 8865** — For foreign partnership status, filed with Form 1065
- **Form 8938** — Statement of Specified Foreign Financial Assets
- **FBAR (FinCEN Form 114)** — Report of Foreign Bank and Financial Accounts

These are in addition to the Hong Kong local filing requirements (audited accounts and Profits Tax Return) described above.

We provide filing deadline reminders for both U.S. and Hong Kong obligations, and can refer you to CPAs experienced in international tax matters.

## FAQ

**Why is Hong Kong more expensive than other jurisdictions?**
Hong Kong has higher government fees, requires a nominee shareholder (since shareholder information is public), and mandates annual audited accounts and tax filings. The additional cost reflects access to one of the world's premier financial centers.

**Do I need to visit Hong Kong?**
No. Formation and bank account opening are typically handled remotely. Certain Hong Kong banks may request a video verification or, rarely, an in-person visit for larger accounts.

**What happens if I do not file the Profits Tax Return on time?**
Hong Kong takes filing deadlines seriously. Directors face personal liability for late or missed filings, including potential fines and penalties. This is why we recommend working with an experienced Hong Kong CPA firm from the outset and why deposits may be required for CPA services.

**Is my ownership information public in Hong Kong?**
Yes, shareholder information is part of the public record in Hong Kong. Our package includes a nominee shareholder to keep your name private. You maintain beneficial ownership through a declaration of trust.

**Can I use a Hong Kong company to trade on Asian stock exchanges?**
A Hong Kong Limited Company can open brokerage accounts with Hong Kong-based brokers that provide access to Asian markets. Specific brokerage requirements vary, and this should be discussed during your consultation.
