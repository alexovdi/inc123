## Why Choose California Private?

California is the largest business market in the United States, but it is one of the worst states for ownership privacy. The California Secretary of State requires disclosure of managers (LLC) or officers and directors (Corporation) on public filings, and the Franchise Tax Board collects detailed ownership information. For business owners who need to operate in California but want to keep their personal identity off searchable public databases, a direct California formation offers no viable path to privacy.

The California Private package solves this through a two-state structure. We form your entity in Wyoming — where no Initial List of Officers is required and nominee services provide complete privacy — and then file a foreign registration in California allowing your Wyoming entity to legally conduct business in the state. The Wyoming filing lists our nominees. The California foreign registration references the Wyoming entity. Your name appears in neither state's public records.

This structure provides a second important advantage beyond privacy. If a plaintiff attempts to pierce the corporate veil of your entity, the liability of officers, directors, managers, and members is determined under Wyoming law — not California law. Wyoming's LLC and corporation statutes provide significantly stronger protections for business owners than California's, giving you a meaningful legal advantage in the event of litigation.

This package is built for California-based business owners, entrepreneurs operating in the California market, and real estate investors holding California property through LLCs. We handle every filing in both states, from Wyoming formation through California foreign registration, and maintain your nominee services year-round.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Will my name appear on any public records in California or Wyoming?**
No. Our nominees appear on the Wyoming formation documents, and the California foreign registration references the Wyoming entity — not you personally. Your name does not appear in either state's public database.

**Why Wyoming instead of Nevada for the home entity?**
Wyoming offers stronger structural privacy (no Initial List of Officers required), lower state fees, and comparable legal protections. This makes it the most cost-effective and privacy-friendly foundation for a California foreign registration.

**Do I still need to pay California's $800 franchise tax?**
Yes. California imposes an $800 minimum annual franchise tax on all LLCs and corporations doing business in the state, regardless of where the entity is formed. This is a state tax obligation separate from our formation package and is paid directly to the California Franchise Tax Board.

**Is a two-state structure legal?**
Yes. Foreign registration is a standard, well-established legal process used by millions of businesses across the United States. Every state has a procedure for out-of-state entities to register and conduct business locally.

**Does this protect me from California lawsuits?**
The two-state structure means that in any attempt to pierce the corporate veil, the liability protections are determined under Wyoming law, which is significantly more favorable to business owners than California law. This is a structural advantage, not a guarantee — proper corporate formalities must still be maintained.

**How long does the full process take?**
Wyoming formation is typically completed within 1-2 business days. California foreign registration takes an additional 5-7 business days. The complete process from order to fully registered entity is usually 7-10 business days.

**Can I operate in other states besides California?**
Yes. You can add foreign registrations in any additional state for $149 each. The Wyoming entity serves as your home entity, and each foreign registration allows you to legally operate in that state.

**Do you offer payment plans?**
We accept all major credit cards, PayPal, and cryptocurrency (Bitcoin, Ethereum, XRP, Litecoin, and Monero). Contact us about payment arrangements for larger packages.

**Can I use my LLC to open a business bank account?**
Yes. We provide all the documents you need: articles of organization, EIN confirmation, and operating agreement. Most banks require these three items.

**What happens at annual renewal?**
We file your Wyoming annual report, maintain nominee services, and keep your registered agent active in both states. California annual filings and franchise tax payments are your responsibility (or your accountant's), though we can coordinate with your tax professional.
