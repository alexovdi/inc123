## Business & Legal Terms

### Entity Formation

**Articles of Incorporation / Articles of Organization**
Foundational legal document filed with the Secretary of State to officially create a corporation (Articles of Incorporation) or LLC (Articles of Organization). Includes entity name, purpose, structure, and registered agent.

**Certificate of Authority**
State-issued document confirming a business entity is properly formed and authorized to operate. Required when registering to do business in a foreign state.

**State Filing / Formation**
The process of submitting formation documents to the Secretary of State. Standard processing: 5–10 business days; expedited: 24 hours.

**Good Standing Certificate**
Official state document certifying an entity's registration is current and all filings/fees are paid. Required for foreign state registration and banking.

**Shelf Company / Shelf Entity**
A pre-formed entity that has never conducted business. Used when an established formation date matters (contracts, licensing, business credit). Incorporate123 offers shelf companies dating back to 2000.

**Domestication**
Moving an existing entity from one state to another without dissolving and re-forming. Preserves EIN, contracts, bank accounts, and entity history.

### Entity Structures & Ownership

**Member / Members**
Owners of an LLC (equivalent to shareholders in a corporation).

**Manager-Managed LLC**
LLC where designated managers run day-to-day operations; not all members participate in management. Supports nominee manager arrangements.

**Member-Managed LLC**
LLC where all members participate equally in management decisions. Default structure in many states.

**Stockholder / Shareholder**
Owner of shares in a corporation. Voting rights based on share ownership.

**Director**
Board member elected by stockholders to oversee corporate management and establish business policies. Fiduciary duty to act in corporation's best interests.

**Officer**
Corporate executive (President, Treasurer, Secretary) responsible for day-to-day operations. Three officer positions required; can be held by the same person.

**Nominee (Officer / Director / Manager)**
A person who appears on public state filings in place of the actual owner, providing privacy. Incorporate123 offers year-round nominee service (not "nominee for a day"). Included in Gold packages. Offshore nominees (outside US jurisdiction) available at no additional cost.

**Par Value / No Par Value Stock**
Par value = stated face value per share. No par = no stated value (generally recommended). Standard formation: 75,000 shares at no par value.

**Bearer Shares**
Stock certificates issued to unnamed "Bearer." Prohibited in Nevada and Wyoming since 2007 (NRS 78.235).

**Membership Interest**
Ownership stake in an LLC; equivalent to stock in a corporation.

### Governance & Compliance Documents

**Operating Agreement**
Internal governance document for an LLC. Specifies ownership percentages, profit/loss allocation, voting rights, management structure, and transfer rules. Not legally required in Nevada but critical to protecting LLC status and limited liability.

**Bylaws**
Internal governance document for a corporation. Specifies management structure, meeting procedures, board duties, and stockholder rights. Adopted at first board meeting.

**Capitalization**
The ownership structure of an entity — percentage interests and membership listing (LLC) or stock shares (corporation). Maintained in a separate document from the Operating Agreement since SB60 2013.

**Corporate Minutes / Meeting Minutes**
Formal written records of decisions made by the board of directors or LLC members. Must document reasoning for major decisions. Absence of proper minutes can lead to corporate veil piercing.

**Board Resolution**
Formal written decision passed by the board or stockholders. Used for major actions: dividend payments, salary changes, S-Corp election, mergers.

**Quorum**
Minimum number of directors or members that must be present for a meeting to be valid. Usually a majority; bylaws may specify otherwise.

**Proxy / Proxy Vote**
Authorization for another person to vote shares on a stockholder's behalf.

**Stock Ledger / Stock Certificate**
Official record of share ownership. Registered agents must maintain copies per NRS 78.105.

**Annual Report / Annual List**
Required yearly filing confirming entity information is current. Wyoming: $62/year. Nevada: $350+/year (Initial List + Annual List of Officers). Late filings risk administrative dissolution.

### Tax & Tax Classification

**Pass-Through Taxation (Flow-Through)**
Business income taxed only on the owner's personal return — not at the entity level. Default for LLCs and S-Corps.

**C-Corporation Taxation**
Entity taxed at the corporate level (21% federal rate); dividends taxed again on shareholders' returns. Known as "double taxation."

**S-Corporation Taxation (S-Corp Election)**
Pass-through structure allowing owners to split income between salary (subject to self-employment tax) and distributions (not subject). Generally beneficial when net income exceeds ~$80K/year. Elected via Form 2553.

**Disregarded Entity**
Single-member LLC treated as an extension of the owner for tax purposes. Default classification; no separate tax return required.

**Franchise Tax**
Annual state tax for the privilege of operating in that state. Wyoming: $62 annual report fee. Nevada: $350+ annual list fee. California: minimum $800 on every LLC regardless of income.

**Estimated Tax Payments (Quarterly)**
IRS payments due April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15 when business expects to owe $1,000+ in annual taxes. Underpayment triggers penalties.

**Phantom Income**
Situation where an LLC member receives no cash distribution but is taxed on their allocated share of LLC income. Can function as an asset protection strategy.

**Withholding Tax**
Taxes withheld from payments to foreign entities/persons and remitted to the IRS or state.

### Liability & Asset Protection

**Limited Liability**
Legal protection preventing members or shareholders from personal liability for the entity's debts and obligations. Core benefit of LLC and corporation structures.

**Piercing the Corporate Veil**
Legal action to hold owners personally liable despite limited liability protection. Nevada has pierced the veil only once in 20+ years (fraud case: _Polaris Indus. Corp. v. Kaplan_). Prevented by maintaining proper minutes, separating personal/business funds, and timely filings.

**Charging Order**
Court order directing a creditor to collect only from distributions made to an LLC member — not from LLC assets. The exclusive remedy in Wyoming and Nevada (NRS 86.401). Creditor becomes an "assignee" with no voting or management rights. If the LLC makes no distributions, the creditor receives nothing.

**Holding LLC (Asset Holding LLC)**
A separate LLC that owns other LLCs or valuable assets (real estate, investments, IP). Separates operating liability from asset protection.

**Joint and Several Liability**
Doctrine making each defendant liable for the full judgment. Nevada abolished this — defendants are liable only for their percentage of fault.

### Privacy & Beneficial Ownership

**Privacy vs. Secrecy**
Privacy = legally keeping ownership off public records. Secrecy = illegal concealment. Incorporate123 provides privacy through legal structures.

**Anonymous LLC**
LLC where member/manager names do not appear in public Secretary of State records. Wyoming provides the strongest anonymity; Nevada also strong.

**Beneficial Ownership**
The person who ultimately owns or benefits from an asset, even if legal title is held by another entity or nominee.

**Douglas County (Nevada)**
Only Nevada county that does not require a county business license — avoids annual applications that expose ownership via SSN. Key privacy advantage for Nevada formations.

### Registered Agent & Legal Services

**Registered Agent (Resident Agent)**
Person or company authorized to receive service of process, legal documents, and state correspondence on behalf of a business entity. Required by law in every state. Must maintain a physical address (no PO boxes). After SB60 2013, agents must also maintain Operating Agreements, Bylaws, Stock Ledgers, and Membership Listings.

**Registered Office**
Physical business address where the registered agent receives documents and maintains required records.

**Service of Process**
Legal delivery of lawsuit documents or legal notices to a business entity.

**Offshore Records Custodian**
Third-party (outside US jurisdiction) appointed to maintain corporate records. Foreign subpoenas cannot easily compel production from a foreign custodian. Included free in Gold packages.

---

### International / Offshore

**Offshore Nominee**
Nominee officer/director based outside the US, beyond reach of US court subpoena power. Included in Gold packages (typically $5,000/year elsewhere).

**Offshore Trust**
Trust established in a foreign jurisdiction for asset protection and privacy.

**International Asset Protection**
Strategies using multiple jurisdictions and entities to protect assets from creditors and litigants.

---

### Registration & Compliance

**Foreign State Registration (Foreign Qualification)**
Registering an LLC or corporation in a state where it was not formed but where it conducts business. Triggered by physical operations, employees, or public officers residing in that state. Incorporate123 offers as an add-on ($450–$600, varies by state). Annual renewal required.

**Secretary of State**
State official/office maintaining business entity records and issuing formation documents.

**Business License (NV)**
Nevada state business license required to operate with a physical office location. Included in all Nevada packages. Separate from county business licenses, which vary by county.

### Package & Service Terms

**Gold Package**
Premium tier. Includes nominees (year-round), offshore custodian, offshore nominee directors, registered agent, formation documents, and compliance support. Recommended for privacy-focused clients.

**Silver Package**
Mid-tier. Includes registered agent, operating agreement, state filing, EIN filing, and compliance support. No nominees. Recommended for straightforward formations.

**Bronze Package**
Basic formation tier. Entry-level offering with essential formation services.

**Records-Only Service / Annual Records Service**
Ongoing compliance service for existing entities: corporate minutes, filings, registered agent renewal. No formation included.

**Renewal / Annual Renewal**
Annual service to maintain entity in good standing — registered agent renewal, annual report filing, and compliance support.
