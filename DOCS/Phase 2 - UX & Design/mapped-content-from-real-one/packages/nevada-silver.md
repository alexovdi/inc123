## Why Choose Nevada Silver?

Nevada offers genuine advantages for business formation: no state income tax, no franchise tax, strong director and officer liability protections, and a well-established body of corporate case law. For business owners who need a Nevada entity and are comfortable having their name on public filings, the Silver package provides everything needed for a proper formation at a competitive price.

One of the biggest pitfalls in Nevada formation is hidden fees. Nevada requires more filings than most states — beyond the basic articles, you must file an Initial List of Officers ($150) and obtain a State Business License (LLC $200, Corp $500). Many formation services quote a low base price and then bill these "second-tier" fees separately after you have already committed. Our Silver package includes every state fee in the quoted price, so there are no surprises.

The Silver package is a strong fit for business owners who are physically located in or near Nevada, who are forming an entity for a specific venture where ownership privacy is not a concern, or who want the legal protections of a Nevada entity without the added cost of nominee services. If you later determine that privacy protection would be valuable, upgrading to the Gold package is always possible.

With 25 years of experience forming Nevada entities from our Reno office, we understand the state's requirements thoroughly and handle every detail of the formation and compliance process.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the difference between Nevada Silver and Gold?**
Gold includes year-round nominee officers and directors (keeping your name off all public records) plus offshore record storage. Silver includes formation, registered agent, all state fees, and compliance, but your name will appear on Nevada's public filings.

**Will my name be publicly searchable with Silver?**
Yes. Nevada requires an Initial List of Officers/Managers that is filed publicly. Without nominee services, your name will appear on this list and be searchable on the Nevada Secretary of State website.

**What are "second-tier" filing fees?**
These are mandatory Nevada filings beyond basic articles: the Initial List of Officers ($150) and the State Business License (LLC $200, Corp $500). Our Silver package includes all of them. Many competitors exclude these fees and bill them separately.

**Can I upgrade to Gold later?**
Yes. We can add nominee services to an existing entity, though it requires filing amended documents with the Secretary of State to replace your name with nominee names. Starting with Gold is more cost-effective if you anticipate needing privacy.

**Do I need to live in Nevada?**
No. If you plan to do business in another state under this entity, you will need a foreign registration ($149 add-on) in that state.

**How fast is Nevada formation?**
Standard processing takes 3-5 business days. Rush filing ($199 add-on) provides 24-hour processing. The complete package including Initial List and Business License is typically delivered within 5-7 business days on standard timing.

**Is the Nevada Business License included?**
Yes. The state-level Nevada Business License is included in your formation price. This is separate from any county or city license that may be required depending on your business location.

**What happens if I miss my annual report deadline?**
Most states impose late fees and may administratively dissolve your entity. Nevada charges a $150 penalty for late annual list filings. Our Silver package includes annual compliance filing, so we handle this deadline for you.

**Can I transfer an existing LLC to your management?**
Yes. We can take over registered agent, compliance, and annual filing services for existing Nevada entities. Contact us for a transfer assessment.

**Why is Nevada Silver more expensive than Wyoming Silver?**
Nevada charges higher state fees than Wyoming. The mandatory Initial List ($150) and State Business License (LLC $200 / Corp $500) add significant cost that does not exist in Wyoming. Both packages include all state fees, but Nevada simply requires more.
