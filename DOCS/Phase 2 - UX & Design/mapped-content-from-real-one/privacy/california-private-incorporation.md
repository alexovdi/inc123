# California Private Incorporation

California requires extensive public disclosure when you form a business entity. The Secretary of State publishes the names of LLC managers and members, corporate officers and directors, and the entity's principal address. For business owners operating in California who want to keep their personal information private, forming directly in California is not an option — the state's disclosure requirements are among the most demanding in the country.

The solution is straightforward: form your entity in Wyoming with full nominee services, then foreign-register in California. Your name appears in neither state's public records. Incorporate123 handles the entire process — formation, documentation, foreign registration, and ongoing nominee services — as a single coordinated package.

## How California Private Incorporation Works

California's disclosure requirements apply to entities formed in California. When you form in Wyoming and register as a foreign entity in California, the information published in California's records comes from the Wyoming filing — which lists our nominees, not you.

Here is what happens in practice:

**Step 1: Wyoming formation with nominees.** We file your articles of organization (LLC) or incorporation (corporation) in Wyoming with our nominee as the manager, officer, or director of record. Your name does not appear in Wyoming's public records. Wyoming processes electronic filings typically within 30 minutes.

**Step 2: California foreign registration.** We file a Statement of Authority to Transact Business (for LLCs) or Application for Certificate of Authority (for corporations) with the California Secretary of State. The filing lists the Wyoming entity's information — including our nominee's name, not yours. California's records reflect the same privacy structure as Wyoming's.

**Step 3: Dual registered agent service.** We provide a commercial registered agent in both Wyoming and California, ensuring that legal correspondence and state filings are handled professionally in both states without exposing your personal address.

The entire process — both state filings, all documentation, nominee appointments — is completed within approximately 7 business days. The Wyoming formation is nearly instant; the California foreign registration takes the additional processing time.

## What "Anonymous" Means in This Context

Our definition of "anonymous" is specific: your name will not be entered into the public records of either the Wyoming or California Secretary of State. Anyone searching the California business entity database will find the entity name, the registered agent, and the nominee's name — not yours.

This does not mean your identity is hidden from all parties. Federal anti-money laundering rules require banks to collect beneficial ownership information when you open an account, and the IRS knows who owns your entity through your tax filings. The privacy applies to state-level public records — the databases that competitors, litigants, data brokers, and the general public can search freely.

## Why Wyoming as the Home State?

We use Wyoming (rather than Nevada or another state) for California private formations for several specific reasons:

**Statutory member privacy.** Wyoming's LLC Act does not require member names in articles of organization. This is a statutory protection, not an administrative practice.

**Lowest costs.** Wyoming's annual report fee is $62 — compared to $350+ for Nevada. Over the life of your entity, this difference compounds significantly, especially if you maintain multiple LLCs.

**Strongest charging order protection.** If a plaintiff sues you personally and tries to reach the assets inside your LLC, the liability determination follows Wyoming law — not California law. Wyoming's charging order protection is the strongest in the country, providing significantly more protection than California's comparatively weak LLC statute.

**No state income tax.** Wyoming has no personal or corporate income tax, simplifying your multi-state tax picture.
