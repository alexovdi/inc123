## Why Choose Florida Private?

Florida is the third-largest state economy and one of the most popular destinations for business formation, thanks to its zero state income tax and business-friendly regulatory environment. However, Florida requires disclosure of managers and members (LLC) or officers and directors (Corporation) on public filings with the Division of Corporations. This information is freely searchable online through the Sunbiz.org database. For business owners who value their privacy, a standard Florida formation puts their name, and often their home address, into a publicly accessible database.

The Florida Private package eliminates this exposure through a two-state structure. We form your entity in Wyoming — where no Initial List of Officers is required and nominee services provide complete privacy — then file a foreign registration with the Florida Division of Corporations. The Wyoming formation lists our nominees on all public records. The Florida foreign registration references the Wyoming entity. Your personal name and address appear in neither state's searchable database.

Beyond privacy, this structure provides a meaningful legal advantage. If a creditor or plaintiff attempts to pierce the corporate veil, the liability standards are determined under Wyoming law rather than Florida law. Wyoming's statute provides some of the strongest business owner protections in the country, including a charging order as the exclusive remedy for LLC membership interests — meaning creditors cannot force a sale of your LLC interest or take over management.

This package is designed for Florida-based business owners, entrepreneurs entering the Florida market, snowbirds who split time between states, and real estate investors holding Florida property through LLCs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Will my name appear on any public records in Florida or Wyoming?**
No. Our nominees appear on the Wyoming formation documents, and the Florida foreign registration references the Wyoming entity. Your name does not appear in either state's public database, including Florida's Sunbiz.org.

**Why Wyoming instead of forming directly in Florida?**
Two reasons: privacy and legal protection. Wyoming does not require public disclosure of owners or officers, and its LLC statute provides stronger charging order protections than Florida's. A direct Florida formation would put your name on publicly searchable records with no privacy option.

**Does Florida have a franchise tax or state income tax?**
Florida has no personal state income tax. Florida does impose a corporate income tax (currently 5.5%) on C-Corporations earning income in the state. LLCs that are taxed as pass-through entities are not subject to Florida corporate income tax.

**Is the two-state structure more expensive long-term?**
You will have annual filing obligations in both states — the Wyoming annual report (handled by us) and the Florida annual report (which you or your accountant file). The Wyoming annual report fee is minimal ($60 for LLCs). When weighed against the privacy and legal protections gained, the incremental cost is modest.

**How long does the full process take?**
Wyoming formation completes within 1-2 business days. Florida foreign registration takes an additional 5-7 business days. The full process from order to registered entity typically takes 7-10 business days total.

**Can I hold Florida real estate through this LLC?**
Yes. Many of our Florida Private clients use this structure specifically for holding real estate. The Wyoming LLC owns the property, and the Florida foreign registration authorizes the entity to conduct business (including owning property) in Florida. Your name stays off the deed and the state corporate records.

**What about Florida's annual report?**
Florida requires an annual report filed through Sunbiz.org each year. This is a separate obligation from the Wyoming annual report. Our renewal covers Wyoming compliance and nominee maintenance. Florida annual report filing can be handled by you, your accountant, or coordinated through our office.

**Do you offer payment plans?**
We accept all major credit cards, PayPal, and cryptocurrency (Bitcoin, Ethereum, XRP, Litecoin, and Monero). Contact us about payment arrangements for larger packages.

**Can I use my LLC to open a business bank account?**
Yes. We provide all the documents you need: articles of organization, EIN confirmation, and operating agreement. Most banks require these three items.

**Can I operate in other states besides Florida?**
Yes. You can add foreign registrations in any additional state for $149 each. The Wyoming entity serves as your home entity, and each foreign registration authorizes operations in that state.
