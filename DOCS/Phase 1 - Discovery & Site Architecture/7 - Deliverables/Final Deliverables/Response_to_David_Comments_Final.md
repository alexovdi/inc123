## 10. "Where would the 'Taxation' information go?"

**Your insight was exactly right.** Tax implications should inform entity choice — it's a pre-formation decision-support topic. You asked whether taxation content belongs under Formation or Compliance, and the answer is: both, but for different reasons.

**Decision: Cross-pillar approach (Formation + Compliance)**

- **Formation** = pre-formation tax decisions → "Which entity type for your tax situation?"
- **Compliance** = post-formation tax obligations → "What are your ongoing tax requirements?"

**Three new pages added:**

| Page | Pillar | URL | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Entity Tax Guide | Formation | /formation/entity-tax-guide | How entity type affects taxes — understand before you choose |
| LLC vs S-Corp vs C-Corp Tax | Formation (Comparison) | /compare/llc-vs-scorp-vs-ccorp-tax | Side-by-side tax treatment comparison |
| Tax Filing & Obligations | Compliance | /compliance/tax-obligations | Ongoing tax requirements after formation |

**What we're NOT doing:** We're staying in Inc123's lane — formation education, not CPA-level tax advisory. Every tax page includes disclaimers ("Educational overview — not tax advice") and clear guidance on when to consult a CPA. This keeps the content valuable without creating liability.

**Page count:** 72 → 75 pages. The 4-pillar model stays intact — taxation content integrates naturally under Formation (pre-decision) and Compliance (post-formation).
