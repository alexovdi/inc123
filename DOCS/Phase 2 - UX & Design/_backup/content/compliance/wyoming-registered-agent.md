# Wyoming Registered Agent

If you have formed or are planning to form an LLC or corporation in Wyoming, the state requires you to designate a registered agent with a physical address in Wyoming. This is a legal requirement under Wyoming Statute § 17-28-101 through § 17-28-111, and your entity cannot be formed without one.

Wyoming has become one of the most popular states for business formation — and for good reason. No state income tax, no franchise tax, strong privacy statutes, low annual fees, and some of the most protective asset protection laws in the country. But regardless of where you personally live or operate, your Wyoming entity must have a Wyoming-based registered agent at all times.

## What a Wyoming Registered Agent Does

A registered agent in Wyoming serves as the official point of contact between your business entity and the state. Specifically, the agent:

- **Receives service of process** — If your entity is sued, the lawsuit papers are delivered to your registered agent's Wyoming address.
- **Accepts state correspondence** — Annual report notices, compliance reminders, and other official communications from the Wyoming Secretary of State are sent to the registered agent.
- **Forwards documents promptly** — The agent is responsible for forwarding all received documents to the entity at the most current address on file.
- **Maintains availability** — The agent must be available at the listed physical address during all normal business hours to accept delivery.

Wyoming law requires the registered agent to be either an individual resident of Wyoming or a business entity authorized to do business in the state. A P.O. box cannot be used.

## Why Wyoming Entities Need a Professional Registered Agent

Most people who form a Wyoming entity do not live in Wyoming. They chose Wyoming for its favorable laws, not because they have a physical presence there. This creates an immediate practical problem: you need someone physically in Wyoming to serve as your agent.

Beyond the basic legal requirement, there are several advantages to using a professional registered agent for your Wyoming entity:

### Privacy

Your registered agent's name and address appear on your entity's public filing with the Wyoming Secretary of State. If you designate yourself, your personal name and address become part of the public record. A professional registered agent substitutes their business address for your personal information.

Wyoming is already one of the strongest privacy states — it does not require the disclosure of LLC members or managers on formation documents. Using a professional registered agent complements this privacy by keeping your personal address off the public record as well.

### Reliability and Compliance Coordination

Wyoming's annual report is due on the first day of the anniversary month of your entity's formation. The filing fee is $62 (or $62 minimum, based on assets held in Wyoming). Missing this filing results in your entity being marked as delinquent, and after two consecutive years of non-filing, Wyoming will administratively dissolve the entity.

When your registered agent is also your compliance provider, annual report reminders, state notices, and filing coordination all happen through a single point of contact. Nothing gets lost between providers.

### Professional Handling of Legal Documents

If your entity is served with a lawsuit, the documents must be handled correctly and forwarded immediately. A missed or delayed service of process can result in a default judgment. Professional registered agents have established procedures for same-day notification and secure forwarding.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is a registered agent required for a Wyoming LLC?

Yes. Wyoming law requires every LLC and corporation to maintain a registered agent with a physical address in Wyoming at all times. Your entity cannot be formed without designating one.

### Can I be my own registered agent in Wyoming?

Only if you are a Wyoming resident with a physical address in the state and are available at that address during all business hours. If you live outside Wyoming, you must use a registered agent who is based there.

### How much does a Wyoming registered agent cost?

### What is the Wyoming annual report fee?

The Wyoming annual report fee is $62 per year (or $62 minimum, with the fee calculated based on assets located in Wyoming). This is significantly lower than most other states.

### When is the Wyoming annual report due?

The annual report is due on the first day of the anniversary month of your entity's formation. For example, if your LLC was formed on March 15, your annual report is due every March 1 thereafter.

### What happens if I do not maintain a registered agent in Wyoming?

Wyoming will send notices to your entity's last known address. If you fail to designate a new agent within 60 days, the state can revoke your entity's good standing. Continued non-compliance leads to administrative dissolution.
