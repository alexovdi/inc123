## 1. Page Purpose & Strategic Role

The Privacy pillar page is the primary educational hub for Incorporate123's strongest competitive differentiator. It serves as the top of the funnel for visitors searching for anonymous LLCs, nominee services, and business privacy — then routes them toward cluster pages for deeper research or directly to Gold packages for conversion.

Privacy is the lead pillar because it targets the highest-value keyword opportunities (5,500–9,500 monthly searches for anonymous LLC terms), justifies premium Gold package pricing ($1,275–$2,100), and showcases services no budget competitor offers: year-round nominee services, free offshore nominees (since 2012), and offshore records custodian.

### Primary Objectives

- Educate visitors on what business privacy actually means and why it matters
- Establish Incorporate123 as the authority on anonymous LLCs and nominee services
- Route visitors to cluster pages for deeper research on specific privacy topics
- Provide a direct shortcut to Gold packages for visitors ready to buy
- Cross-sell to Asset Protection pillar where relevant
- Serve as the template that proves the pillar page pattern before replicating it

### Target Audiences

| Audience | Entry Point | Expected Path |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Privacy Researcher | Organic search for "anonymous LLC" or "nominee services" | Pillar → Clusters → Comparison → Gold Package |
| CA/FL Business Owner | Referred from state-specific pages or search | Pillar → CA/FL cluster → Gold + Foreign Registration |
| AP Cross-Sell | Referred from Asset Protection pillar | Pillar → Nominee Services cluster → Gold upgrade |
| Informed Buyer | Already knows about privacy LLCs, comparing providers | Pillar → shortcuts to packages (skip education) |

## 4. "What is Business Privacy?" — Opening Content Section

The first educational section. Follows the 80/20 progressive disclosure model.

### Content Structure

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Section Headline (H2) | "What is Business Privacy?" |
| Scanner Version (visible) | 3–5 sentence overview explaining that business privacy means keeping your name off public records associated with your LLC or Corporation. Mention that most states require disclosure of members, managers, or officers — but Wyoming and Nevada offer structures that don't. |
| Reader Version (expandable) | "Read full explanation →" expands to reveal 400–600 words covering: why public records exist, what information is exposed (articles of organization, annual reports, registered agent filings), how nominees work to replace your name, and the difference between formation-day privacy and year-round privacy. |
| Inline CTA | Soft CTA after this section: "Learn about Anonymous LLCs →" → `/anonymous-llc/` |

### Progressive Disclosure Behavior

- Scanner content is always visible
- "Read full explanation →" toggles open the expanded content with smooth animation
- Collapse toggle: "Show less ↑"
- State persists during page session (stays open if opened)

## 11. FAQ Accordion Section

5–8 frequently asked questions about business privacy. Helps capture long-tail SEO queries via FAQ schema markup.

### Questions

| # | Question | Answer Summary |
|---|----------|---------------|
| 1 | "What is an anonymous LLC?" | An LLC where the owner's name does not appear on any publicly searchable state filings. Achieved through nominee officers/directors and formation in privacy-friendly states. |
| 2 | "What states allow anonymous LLCs?" | Wyoming and Nevada are the two primary states. Wyoming has the strongest statutory privacy protections. New Mexico also allows it but lacks other advantages. |
| 3 | "What is a nominee officer?" | A person or entity that appears on public records in place of the actual owner. Year-round nominees maintain this privacy on every filing, not just at formation. |
| 4 | "How much does an anonymous LLC cost?" | Wyoming Gold: $1,275 all-inclusive. Nevada Gold: $1,800 all-inclusive. Both include year-round nominees, offshore nominees, registered agent, EIN, operating agreement, and state fees. Renewal: $525/year with nominees. |
| 5 | "What's the difference between privacy and asset protection?" | Privacy keeps your name hidden. Asset protection keeps your assets safe from lawsuits and creditors. They're complementary — most clients need both. Gold packages address privacy; asset protection strategies are covered in our Asset Protection guide. |
| 6 | "Can I use an anonymous LLC for my existing business?" | Yes. You can form a new anonymous LLC and transfer your business into it, or use domestication to move an existing entity to a privacy-friendly state. |

### Behavior

- Single-expand mode (opening one closes others) or multi-expand (user preference)
- Keyboard accessible (Enter/Space to toggle, arrow keys to navigate between items)
- FAQ schema markup for SEO (generates rich results)
