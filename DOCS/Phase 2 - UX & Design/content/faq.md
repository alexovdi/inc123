# Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about business formation, privacy services, asset protection, and compliance. Questions are organized by topic so you can quickly find what you need.

## Company Formation

**How long does it take to form an LLC or corporation?**
Wyoming and Nevada both offer same-day processing. Once we receive your order, we establish your secure support ticket within hours and begin filing. The state processes the filing the same day. Your EIN (federal tax ID) is typically obtained within 1-2 business days after state filing is complete.

**Should I form an LLC or a corporation?**
For most clients focused on privacy and asset protection, an LLC is the preferred choice. LLCs offer pass-through taxation (avoiding double taxation), flexible management structures, and charging order protections. Corporations may be better for businesses planning to raise outside investment or issue stock. [Learn more about LLC vs. Corporation](/formation/llc-vs-corporation/)

**What state should I form in?**
Wyoming is the best choice for most clients due to its low cost ($100 filing fee), strong privacy protections, and exclusive charging order remedy. Nevada is preferred when you need a physical business presence, dedicated business court jurisdiction, or when your situation specifically benefits from Nevada's 50 years of case law. [Compare Wyoming vs. Nevada](/compare/wyoming-vs-nevada/)

**Can I form in Wyoming or Nevada if I live in another state?**
Yes. This is standard practice. You form your entity in the privacy-friendly state and register it as a foreign entity in your home state to operate locally. Our California Private and Florida Private packages are specifically designed for this approach.

**What is included in the state filing fees?**
All packages include every state filing fee required during the first year, including Articles of Organization/Incorporation, initial business license (Nevada), and all government processing fees. There are no hidden second-tier filings or surprise costs.

## Business Privacy

**How do nominee services work?**
A nominee officer, director, or manager appears in public state filings in place of your personal information. You maintain full ownership and control through your confidential membership interest or stock ownership. The nominee acts on your written instructions and has no independent authority over the entity.

**Will my name appear anywhere in public records?**
With our Gold or Private packages, your name does not appear in any state filing. The nominee's name and address appear in the Articles, annual reports, and any other public documents. Your ownership is documented in confidential internal records.

**What is a nominee EIN?**
We can obtain your entity's federal tax ID (EIN) using the nominee's information rather than your personal Social Security Number. This keeps your SSN off the IRS application associated with the entity.

**Can someone find out I own the company?**
With proper nominee services, your ownership is not discoverable through public record searches. A court with proper jurisdiction can compel disclosure through formal legal proceedings, but casual searches, skip tracers, and informal agency requests will not reveal your connection.

## Asset Protection

**What is charging order protection?**
A charging order is the only remedy available to a creditor of an LLC member in states like Wyoming and Nevada. The creditor receives a lien on distributions — if and when the LLC chooses to make them. The creditor cannot force distributions, cannot seize the membership interest, and cannot gain any management control of the LLC.

**Does charging order protection work for single-member LLCs?**
In Wyoming, yes. Wyoming is one of the few states that explicitly provides exclusive charging order protection for single-member LLCs. Nevada and most other states have less clear protections for single-member entities.

**Can I protect assets I already own?**
Yes, but timing matters. Transferring assets into a protective structure after a claim has been filed (or is reasonably anticipated) may be challenged as a fraudulent transfer. The strongest protection comes from structuring before any issues arise.

**Is offshore asset protection legal?**
Yes. U.S. persons have every right to hold assets internationally. The requirement is full compliance with IRS reporting obligations, which we help you manage. [Learn more about offshore services](/offshore/)

## Compliance & Maintenance

**What are the annual costs to maintain my entity?**
Annual costs vary by state and package level. Wyoming annual reports start at $62. Nevada annual list and business license renewal total approximately $350. Our registered agent and service fees are in addition to state fees and are clearly disclosed in your package pricing.

**What happens if I miss an annual filing?**
Your entity may be revoked or administratively dissolved by the state. This can result in loss of liability protection, loss of your entity name, and additional reinstatement fees. Our registered agent service includes filing reminders to prevent this.

**Do I need to file a separate tax return for my LLC?**
Single-member LLCs are typically treated as disregarded entities for federal tax purposes, meaning income is reported on your personal Form 1040 Schedule C. Multi-member LLCs file a partnership return (Form 1065). Neither Wyoming nor Nevada impose state income taxes, so no state tax returns are required in those states.

## Offshore & International

**What IRS forms do I need to file for an offshore entity?**
This depends on your structure. Common forms include 8858, 8865, 5471, 8938, FBAR, 3520, and 3520-A. We provide form-by-form guidance and filing deadline reminders. [View our complete IRS compliance guide](/offshore/irs-compliance/)

**Can I open an offshore bank account as a U.S. citizen?**
Yes, but the process requires thorough documentation due to FATCA regulations. Our international packages include bank account opening services, and our nominee director manages the application process. We cannot guarantee approval, but our success rate is high.

**Does the IRS know about my offshore accounts?**
Through FATCA and the Common Reporting Standard, many foreign banks report U.S. account holders' information to the IRS. Full disclosure and proper filing are essential. Attempting to hide accounts is increasingly futile and subject to severe penalties.
