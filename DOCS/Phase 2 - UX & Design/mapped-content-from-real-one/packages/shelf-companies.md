## Why Choose a Shelf Company?

A shelf company — also called an aged company or shelf corporation — is an entity that was formed at an earlier date and has been maintained in good standing without conducting any business activity. The entity has a clean history: no transactions, no liabilities, no liens, and no prior operations. When you purchase a shelf company, ownership is transferred to you (or to nominees acting on your behalf), and you receive an entity with an established formation date.

There are several legitimate business situations where entity age matters. Some contracts, government bids, and vendor relationships require that the business entity have a minimum operating history. Certain financial institutions and lending programs use entity age as one factor in their underwriting process. In some industries, an established formation date carries weight with potential clients or partners who may perceive a newly formed entity as less stable.

It is important to understand what a shelf company does and does not provide. A shelf company gives you a legitimate entity with a documented formation date and continuous good standing. It does not give you a fabricated business history, revenue records, or credit history. The entity has been maintained on the shelf — properly filed and in compliance, but dormant. This is a legal and transparent practice.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What does "clean history" mean?**
The entity has never conducted any business activity, entered into any contracts, incurred any debts, or had any liens or judgments filed against it. It has been maintained solely for the purpose of being available as a shelf company. We guarantee the clean history of every entity we sell.

**What ages are available?**

**Will the entity have a credit history?**
No. A shelf company has no financial history — no credit, no bank accounts, no transactions. Building business credit begins after you take ownership and start operating. The formation date provides entity age, which is one factor some lenders consider, but it does not substitute for actual credit history.

**Can I use a shelf company for government contracts?**
Entity age is one factor in some government contracting requirements. Whether a shelf company satisfies a particular requirement depends on the specific language of the solicitation. We recommend reviewing the exact requirements with your contracting officer or attorney before purchasing.

**Does the entity come with an EIN?**
No. A new EIN will be obtained after ownership transfer to associate the entity with your tax information. EIN obtainment is available as an add-on ($75) or you can apply directly with the IRS.

**Is buying a shelf company legal?**
Yes. Purchasing a pre-formed entity and transferring ownership is a standard, legal business practice used throughout the United States. The key distinction is that a shelf company provides legitimate entity age — it does not provide fabricated business history or fraudulent credentials.

**How quickly can I take ownership?**
Ownership transfer is typically completed within 2-3 business days of purchase. If you need a faster transfer, let us know and we can often expedite the process.

**What states can I foreign-register in?**
