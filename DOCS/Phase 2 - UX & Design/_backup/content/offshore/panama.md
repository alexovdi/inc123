# Panama Corporation

The Republic of Panama is one of the most developed offshore centers in the Americas. An independent, sovereign state with the fastest-growing economy and highest per-capita income in Central America, Panama has built a modern financial infrastructure that caters to the international business community. With over 100 international financial institutions maintaining a presence in Panama City, the country offers direct access to global banking, trade, and investment networks.

## Why Panama?

Panama offers a distinct set of advantages that make it particularly well suited for operating businesses with international client bases:

- **Territorial Tax System** — Companies that generate revenue from outside Panama pay no income, dividend, or capital gains tax, even if the company maintains an office in Panama. No tax filing of any kind is required unless the company operates a local Panamanian business.
- **U.S. Dollar Currency** — The local currency (Balboa) is at par with and used interchangeably with the U.S. dollar. U.S. dollar bills serve as the paper currency of Panama, eliminating exchange rate risk for American business owners.
- **No Exchange Controls** — There are no restrictions on moving capital in or out of Panama.
- **Strong Banking Sector** — Over 100 international banks operate in Panama, making it a preferred jurisdiction for offshore banking and trade finance.
- **Civil Law System** — Panama's legal system is based on civil law, with Spanish as the official language and English widely spoken as the second language.

## Important Tax Consideration

Unlike Nevis, BVI, and Hong Kong entities, a **Panama Corporation cannot elect pass-through taxation with the IRS**. U.S. shareholders of a Panama Corporation (classified as a Controlled Foreign Corporation) must file Form 5471 (Annual Information Return of Foreign Controlled Corporation) each year along with their Form 1040.

This makes Panama less suited for passive asset holding by U.S. persons (where Nevis or BVI would be more tax-efficient) but well suited for active international business operations where the additional reporting complexity is offset by Panama's practical business advantages.

## Practical Applications

**International business operations.** Panama is the strongest choice for clients who need to run an operating business serving international clients. The combination of territorial taxation, U.S. dollar denomination, robust banking infrastructure, and strategic geographic position makes it a practical base for international trade, e-commerce, and professional services.

**Banking and trade finance.** Panama's deep banking sector provides access to trade finance, letters of credit, and multi-currency accounts that support active international commerce.

**Asset protection.** While Panama offers solid asset protection through its corporate structure, clients focused primarily on asset protection may find Nevis or BVI more advantageous due to the availability of pass-through tax elections and stronger charging order protections.

## Nominee Directors

Panama's Corporation Law requires a minimum of three directors, and the names and addresses of all directors and officers are filed in the public registry. To maintain your privacy, our package includes three nominee directors and officers so that your personal information does not appear in any public records.

This is not optional in Panama. Without nominee directors, your name, address, and role would be publicly accessible through the Panama public registry.

## IRS Compliance for U.S. Persons

Because pass-through taxation is not available for Panama Corporations, U.S. shareholders face a more comprehensive reporting obligation. Expected annual filings include:

- **Form 926** — Transfer of Property to a Foreign Corporation (filed when you initially capitalize the company)
- **Form 5471** — Annual Information Return of Foreign Controlled Corporation
- **Form 8938** — Statement of Specified Foreign Financial Assets
- **FBAR (FinCEN Form 114)** — Report of Foreign Bank and Financial Accounts

We provide filing deadline reminders and can refer you to CPAs experienced in CFC reporting. While the reporting burden is greater than for entities eligible for pass-through status, it is manageable with proper professional support and no more complex than preparing a domestic corporate tax return.

Disclosing assets to the IRS does not grant the IRS the power to seize them. The IRS becomes a creditor like any other and must pursue collection through complex legal procedures in the foreign jurisdiction.

## FAQ

**Why can't a Panama Corporation elect pass-through taxation?**
The IRS does not recognize Panama Corporations as eligible entities for the check-the-box election (Form 8832). This is a limitation of U.S. tax law, not Panamanian law. If simplified tax reporting is a priority, consider a [Nevis LLC](/offshore/nevis/) or [BVI Business Company](/offshore/bvi/) instead.

**Do I need to visit Panama?**
No. Formation and bank account opening are typically handled remotely. If you plan to open accounts with certain Panama-based banks directly, some may require an in-person visit, which we will discuss in advance.

**Is Panama still a reputable jurisdiction after the "Panama Papers"?**
Yes. The Panama Papers primarily involved a single law firm's practices, not the Panamanian corporate framework itself. Panama remains a major international financial center with robust regulatory oversight. Our structures are designed for full compliance and legitimate business purposes.

**Can I operate an e-commerce business through a Panama Corporation?**
Absolutely. Panama's territorial tax system, U.S. dollar currency, and strong banking infrastructure make it well suited for e-commerce operations serving international customers.

**Why are three directors required?**
This is a requirement of Panama's Corporation Law. Our package includes all three nominee directors to keep your information private. You maintain control through your share ownership, which is not part of the public record.
