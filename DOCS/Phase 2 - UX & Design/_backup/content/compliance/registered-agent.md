# Registered Agent Services

Every LLC and corporation in the United States is required by law to designate a registered agent. This is not optional. It is a statutory requirement in all 50 states, and failing to maintain one can result in your entity being administratively dissolved.

A registered agent is the person or company authorized to receive service of process — lawsuits, subpoenas, and legal notices — as well as official correspondence from the Secretary of State on behalf of your business entity. The registered agent must have a physical street address in the state of formation and must be available during normal business hours to accept delivery of documents.

At Incorporate123, registered agent service is included in every formation and renewal package we offer. We have been providing registered agent services from our Reno, Nevada office for 25 years, and we serve as registered agent for entities across Wyoming, Nevada, and other key formation states.

## Why You Need a Professional Registered Agent

You might wonder why you cannot simply list your own name and home address as the registered agent. Technically, in most states, you can — as long as you have a physical address in the formation state and are available at that address during all business hours. But there are several important reasons why most business owners choose a professional service instead.

### Privacy Protection

Your registered agent's name and address are part of the public record. Anyone can look up your entity with the Secretary of State and find your registered agent's information. If you serve as your own registered agent, your personal name and home address become permanently searchable public data. A professional registered agent keeps your personal information private.

### Reliability

Registered agents must be available to accept service of process during all business hours. If a process server arrives with a lawsuit and nobody is there to accept it, the court may allow alternative service methods — or worse, a default judgment may be entered against your entity without your knowledge. Professional registered agents maintain staffed offices specifically for this purpose.

### Out-of-State Formation

If you form a Wyoming or Nevada entity but live in another state, you cannot serve as your own registered agent because you do not have a physical address in the formation state. You need a registered agent with a real office in that state. This is one of the most common reasons business owners use a professional service.

### Compliance Continuity

When you use a professional registered agent that also handles your annual filings and corporate records, all of your compliance obligations are coordinated through a single provider. State correspondence, filing notices, and legal documents all flow through the same office, reducing the chance that something falls through the cracks.

## Records Custodian Responsibilities

Following the passage of SB60 in 2013, Nevada statutes (NRS 78.105 and NRS 86.241) now require all entities to maintain certain records at their registered office. Nevada registered agents must maintain records on their corporate clients, including copies of operating agreements or bylaws and the stock ledger or membership listing.

This creates a potential privacy concern. The Secretary of State can request these records from the registered agent, and the information may be disclosed to various government agencies including the IRS, state tax authorities, law enforcement, and agencies overseeing workers' compensation and labor enforcement.

Our solution: every Incorporate123 package includes an offshore company secretary who is appointed as the custodian of your stock ledger or membership listing. If the Secretary of State requests records per NRS 86.246, the registered agent provides only the name and address of the offshore custodian — who is outside U.S. court jurisdiction and not subject to domestic subpoena power. This strategy provides maximum privacy protection at no additional cost.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What exactly does a registered agent do?

A registered agent receives legal documents and official state correspondence on behalf of your business entity. This includes lawsuits, subpoenas, tax notices, annual report reminders, and other official communications from the Secretary of State.

### Is a registered agent legally required?

Yes. Every LLC and corporation in every U.S. state must designate a registered agent as a condition of formation and continued good standing. Failing to maintain a registered agent can lead to administrative dissolution.

### Can I use a P.O. box as my registered agent address?

No. All states require a physical street address for the registered agent. P.O. boxes are not acceptable.

### What happens if I do not have a registered agent?

The state will send notices requesting that you designate one. If you fail to comply within the specified timeframe, the state can revoke your entity's good standing or administratively dissolve it. Additionally, if someone tries to serve your entity with a lawsuit and there is no registered agent, the court may allow alternative service methods, potentially resulting in a default judgment against you.

### Can I change my registered agent?

Yes. You can change your registered agent at any time by filing a change of agent form with the Secretary of State. There is typically a small filing fee. If you are switching to Incorporate123, we handle the paperwork for you.

### What is the difference between a registered agent and a registered office?

The registered agent is the person or company authorized to accept documents. The registered office is the physical address where the agent is located. Both must be listed on your entity's state filings.

### Does Incorporate123 serve as registered agent in all 50 states?

We provide registered agent service directly in Wyoming and Nevada, and we can arrange registered agent service in any other state through our network of trusted partners.

### How quickly will I receive forwarded documents?

We forward all received documents on the same business day via secure email. For time-sensitive legal matters, we also provide phone notification.

### What is the offshore secretary, and how does it relate to the registered agent?

The offshore secretary is a separate corporate officer appointed as custodian of your stock ledger or membership records. While the registered agent receives external documents, the offshore secretary holds your internal corporate records outside U.S. jurisdiction, providing an additional layer of privacy protection.

### Will my registered agent's address be my business address?

The registered agent address is listed on your entity's state filings as the address for service of process. It is not the same as your business mailing address. If you need a business address, our Silver and Gold packages include virtual office services with a dedicated suite number.
