# Shares & Corporate Records

Forming an LLC or corporation creates a legal entity separate from you. But that separation only holds up if you treat the entity as a separate legal person — with its own records, its own meetings, its own decisions documented in writing. This is where corporate records maintenance becomes essential.

Courts regularly "pierce the corporate veil" when business owners fail to maintain proper records. When that happens, the court disregards the entity entirely and holds the owner personally liable for business debts. The most common reasons courts pierce the veil are exactly the kinds of things corporate records are designed to prevent: no stock was issued, no meetings were held, no minutes were kept, no operating agreement was followed.

### Preserving Your Liability Protection

The entire point of forming an LLC or corporation is to create a legal barrier between your personal assets and your business obligations. That barrier exists only as long as you maintain it. Corporate records are the evidence that you maintained it.

If your entity is ever sued and the plaintiff argues that the corporate veil should be pierced, the court will look at:

- Were corporate bylaws or an operating agreement adopted and followed?
- Were annual meetings held (or written consents obtained)?
- Were meeting minutes prepared and maintained?
- Was stock actually issued (for corporations)?
- Were membership interests properly documented (for LLCs)?
- Were banking resolutions adopted before opening accounts?
- Were tax elections properly authorized by resolution?
- Were the entity's records kept separate from the owner's personal records?

Every "no" answer gives the plaintiff's attorney ammunition. Every "yes" answer strengthens your position. Proper corporate records are your best defense against veil piercing.

### IRS Compliance

Certain tax elections require corporate authorization documented in the minutes or resolutions. If the IRS audits your entity and finds that an S-Corp election, for example, was never properly authorized by the board of directors and shareholders, the election may be disallowed retroactively — creating significant tax liability.

### Banking and Business Transactions

Banks, lenders, and business partners regularly request copies of corporate resolutions, certificates of incumbency, and other documents from your corporate records. If these documents do not exist or are out of date, transactions can be delayed or denied.

## Stock Issuance and Ownership Documentation

### Uncertificated Stock

Per Nevada statutes, stock is issued "uncertificated" by default. Instead of paper stock certificates, a Stockholder Statement of Account is issued by the corporate secretary — initially at formation and annually thereafter. This document confirms who owns how many shares.

If you require certificated stock (paper certificates), a special resolution is needed to authorize the change ($75), and certificates are issued at $50 each.

### Ownership Changes

Transferring ownership — whether selling shares, adding new members, or changing membership percentages — requires specialized instruments of transfer and updated documentation. Ownership changes cannot be handled as part of a regular annual meeting. We prepare the transfer documents, updated operating agreements (for LLCs), and amended records as a special request.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I really need corporate records if I am the only owner?

Yes. Single-member LLCs and single-shareholder corporations are just as vulnerable to veil piercing as multi-owner entities. Courts look at whether the entity was treated as a separate legal person — regardless of how many owners it has.

### What happens if I never issued stock in my corporation?

This is a significant compliance gap. Failing to issue stock is one of the most common reasons courts pierce the corporate veil. We can prepare retroactive stock issuance documentation and a Stockholder Statement of Account to address this.

### What is the difference between bylaws and an operating agreement?

Bylaws govern corporations; operating agreements govern LLCs. Both serve the same essential purpose: establishing the rules for how the entity is managed, how decisions are made, and what rights the owners have.

### How are annual meetings handled if I live in another state?

We handle annual meetings by proxy through our appointed company secretary. The secretary prepares the meeting notices, conducts the meetings, prepares the minutes, and files any required state documents. You do not need to travel to Nevada or Wyoming.

### Can you prepare records for an entity formed by another company?

Yes. If another formation service set up your entity but did not prepare proper records — or sent you a generic fill-in-the-blanks kit — we can prepare custom records for your entity and take over ongoing maintenance.

### What is a Certificate of Incumbency?

A Certificate of Incumbency is a document certifying who the current officers and directors of a corporation are. Banks, lenders, and business partners may request this as proof of who is authorized to act on behalf of the entity. We prepare these as a special request starting at $75.

### Are the operating agreements and bylaws you prepare confidential?

Yes. Unlike some states that require operating agreements to be filed publicly, the operating agreements and bylaws we prepare contain no personal ownership information. Capitalization and membership details are maintained in a separate document held by the offshore secretary.

### How much do special meetings or resolutions cost?

Special meetings and resolutions are $75 each. Common examples include officer appointments, banking resolution changes, tax election authorizations, and property transaction authorizations.

### What if I want paper stock certificates?

We can issue certificated stock after adopting a special resolution authorizing the change ($75). Individual stock certificates are $50 each. By default, stock is issued uncertificated with a Stockholder Statement of Account, which is the standard approach under Nevada statutes.

### Can you help if I am being audited and my records are incomplete?

Yes. We can prepare corporate records retroactively to document past actions and establish a proper record going forward. The sooner this is addressed, the stronger your position in an audit.
