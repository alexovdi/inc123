# Nevada Business Formation & Privacy

Nevada has built over five decades of business-friendly case law and maintains one of the most developed corporate legal frameworks in the country. With no state income tax, a dedicated business court system, same-day filing, and strong liability protections, Nevada is a proven jurisdiction for entrepreneurs who need a credible business presence combined with robust legal protections. Our Reno, Nevada office has been helping clients incorporate in Nevada since 2000.

## Why Nevada?

Nevada's business laws have been tested and upheld through over 50 years of consistent judicial decisions. Here are the key advantages:

- **Dedicated Business Court** — Nevada operates a specialized business court that handles corporate disputes, ensuring cases are decided by judges with deep expertise in business law rather than general civil court judges.
- **50+ Years of Business-Friendly Case Law** — Nevada's courts have consistently upheld the corporate veil, protected officer and director liability, and enforced charging order protections. This body of precedent provides certainty that newer jurisdictions cannot match.
- **No State Income Tax** — Nevada imposes no personal or corporate income tax. There is no capital gains tax, no inheritance tax, and no franchise tax on net income.
- **Same-Day Filing** — Nevada processes business filings the same day they are submitted, allowing you to have a live entity within hours.
- **No Tax Information-Sharing with the IRS** — Nevada does not have a tax information-sharing agreement with the Internal Revenue Service.
- **Strong Nominee Protections** — Nevada's statutes support the use of nominee officers, directors, and managers, allowing you to keep your name off public records while maintaining full control through confidential ownership.

## Asset Protection in Nevada

Nevada provides strong charging order protection for LLCs, making it difficult for creditors of a member to reach assets held within the entity. While Wyoming's statutes are marginally stronger on paper (particularly for single-member LLCs), Nevada's extensive body of case law provides a level of judicial certainty that gives many attorneys and judges confidence in Nevada structures.

Nevada also offers strong protections for corporate officers and directors, including a provision that allows a corporation's articles to eliminate or limit director liability for breaches of fiduciary duty.

## FAQ

**Is Nevada or Wyoming better for my LLC?**
It depends on your priorities. Wyoming offers lower costs and marginally stronger statutory privacy. Nevada offers a dedicated business court, decades of case law, and virtual office options that provide a credible physical business presence. Many clients choose Nevada when they need a professional Nevada address or when the business court advantage matters.

**Why are Nevada filing fees higher than Wyoming?**
Nevada's $425 LLC filing fee includes the state business license ($200). The state also charges annual renewal fees that are higher than Wyoming's. The trade-off is access to Nevada's established legal infrastructure and business court system.

**Do I need a Nevada virtual office?**
Not necessarily, but a virtual office provides a professional Nevada business address, mail forwarding, and an exclusive suite number. This is valuable for businesses that want to present a credible Nevada presence to clients and partners.

**What is Douglas County and why does it matter?**
Douglas County, where our corporate office is located, does not require or issue county or city business licenses. This means one fewer public record associated with your entity, which is an additional privacy advantage over formation services based in Reno, Las Vegas, or Carson City.

**Can I form in Nevada and operate in another state?**
Yes. You can form a Nevada entity and register it as a foreign entity in your operating state. This is a standard strategy for gaining Nevada's legal protections while conducting business locally.
