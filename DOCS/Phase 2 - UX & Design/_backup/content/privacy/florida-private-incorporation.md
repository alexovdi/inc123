# Florida Private Incorporation

Florida's public records laws are among the broadest in the country. The state's Sunshine Law extends to business entity filings, making officer names, manager names, registered agent information, and addresses freely searchable through the Florida Division of Corporations database. For business owners operating in Florida who want to keep their personal details private, forming directly in Florida leaves your name exposed in one of the most accessible public records systems in any state.

The alternative: form your entity in Wyoming with full nominee services, then foreign-register in Florida. The Florida filing reflects the Wyoming nominee information — not your personal details. Incorporate123 handles the entire process as a coordinated dual-state package, and your name appears in neither state's records.

## How Florida Private Incorporation Works

Florida's broad disclosure requirements apply to entities formed in Florida. When you register a Wyoming entity as a foreign LLC or corporation in Florida, the information filed comes from the Wyoming formation — which lists our nominees.

**Step 1: Wyoming formation with nominees.** We file your articles of organization (LLC) or incorporation (corporation) in Wyoming with our nominee as the manager, officer, or director of record. Wyoming processes electronic filings typically within 30 minutes. No member names are required or filed.

**Step 2: Florida foreign registration.** We file an Application for Authorization to Transact Business with the Florida Division of Corporations. The application lists the Wyoming entity's information — our nominee's name and our registered agent addresses in both states. Your personal name and address do not appear.

**Step 3: Dual registered agent service.** We provide a commercial registered agent in both Wyoming and Florida, so all legal correspondence and state communications are handled professionally without exposing your home address.

The Wyoming formation is nearly instant. The Florida foreign registration takes approximately 7 business days to process. The total package — both state formations, all documentation, nominee appointments — is typically complete within 10 business days.

## Why Wyoming for Florida Business Owners?

We use Wyoming as the formation state for Florida private incorporations for several important reasons.

**No public member disclosure.** Wyoming's LLC Act does not require member names in articles of organization. The Secretary of State does not collect or publish this information. When Florida's records reference the Wyoming filing, there are no member names to disclose.

**Charging order protection under Wyoming law.** If someone sues you and obtains a judgment, then tries to reach the assets in your LLC, the internal affairs of the LLC are governed by Wyoming law — not Florida law. Wyoming provides the strongest charging order protection in the country: the charging order is the exclusive remedy, meaning a creditor cannot force distributions, seize LLC assets, or compel a sale of your membership interest.

**Low annual costs.** Wyoming's annual report fee is $62, compared to $350+ for a comparable Nevada filing. Over years of entity maintenance, this adds up — especially if you operate multiple LLCs.

**No state income tax in either state.** Neither Wyoming nor Florida has a personal state income tax, creating a clean multi-state structure with no additional tax filing complexity at the state level.

## What "Anonymous" Means for Florida Operations

When we say your entity is anonymous, we mean your name will not appear in the public records of either the Wyoming or Florida Secretary of State / Division of Corporations. Anyone searching Florida's Sunbiz database will find the entity name, the registered agent, and the nominee's name — not yours.

You remain fully identified to the IRS (through your tax filings and EIN), to your bank (under federal beneficial ownership rules), and to FinCEN (under the Corporate Transparency Act). The privacy applies to state-level public records — the databases that the general public, competitors, opposing counsel, and data aggregators search.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Will my name appear anywhere in Florida's Sunbiz database?**
No. The Florida foreign registration reflects information from the Wyoming filing — our nominee's name and registered agent address. Your name is not entered into Florida's or Wyoming's public records.

**Does Florida have a franchise tax like California?**
Florida does not impose an $800 annual franchise tax on LLCs like California does. Florida LLCs with corporate owners may be subject to the Florida corporate income tax (5.5%), but single-member or multi-member LLCs taxed as partnerships or sole proprietorships are not subject to a Florida entity-level tax.

**Can I hold Florida real estate through this structure?**
Yes. Holding Florida real estate through a Wyoming LLC with nominees is a widely used strategy for keeping property ownership private. Each property can be held in a separate LLC for additional asset isolation.

**What if I am sued in Florida — which state's law applies?**
For matters related to the LLC's internal governance — charging order protection, piercing the corporate veil, member liability — Wyoming law typically applies because the LLC is formed there. For the underlying claim (contract dispute, personal injury, etc.), the law where the events occurred generally governs. The Wyoming formation gives you the benefit of Wyoming's superior charging order protection.

**How long does the complete process take?**
Wyoming formation is typically completed within hours. Florida foreign registration takes approximately 7 business days. Total process is usually complete within 10 business days.

**Can I convert an existing Florida LLC to this private structure?**

**Does this work for Florida corporations as well as LLCs?**

**What about the Florida annual report?**
Foreign LLCs registered in Florida must file an annual report with the Division of Corporations. We handle this filing as part of your annual renewal, using the nominee's information. The filing is due by May 1 each year.
