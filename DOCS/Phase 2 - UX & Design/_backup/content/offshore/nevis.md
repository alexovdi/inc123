# Nevis International Corporation/LLC

Nevis, part of the Federation of St. Kitts and Nevis, has developed one of the most protective offshore frameworks available for asset protection planning. Its legal system is based on English Common Law, served by a High Court of Justice, a Court of Appeal, and the Privy Council in London. The Nevis Limited Liability Company Ordinance of 1995 is a modern statute that mirrors many of the provisions found in Nevada's LLC laws, making it a natural complement to domestic privacy and asset protection structures.

## Why Nevis?

Nevis stands out among offshore jurisdictions for several specific legal protections:

- **Charging Order Protection** — Like Nevada, Nevis limits creditor remedies against LLC membership interests to a charging order. A creditor who obtains a Nevis charging order may also face U.S. tax liability on any distributions, creating a strong deterrent.
- **No Foreign Subpoena Recognition** — Nevis will not respond to a subpoena issued by any court outside of Nevis. A creditor must bring their case in Nevis courts under Nevis law.
- **No Cooperation Without Criminal Activity** — Nevis authorities will not cooperate with foreign agencies unless the matter involves criminal conduct under Nevis law.
- **Single-Member Entities Permitted** — Both LLCs and corporations may operate with a single member or shareholder.
- **Pass-Through Tax Election Available** — U.S. persons can elect Foreign Disregarded Entity or Foreign Partnership status, simplifying IRS compliance significantly.

## Practical Applications

A Nevis entity serves two primary purposes:

**Asset protection.** A Nevis LLC or corporation is ideally suited for holding liquid assets, commodities, notes, and securities outside U.S. court jurisdiction. When combined with a [Bahamas Asset Protection Trust](/offshore/trust/), the structure provides comprehensive protection: assets are held by a foreign entity within a foreign trust, creating multiple layers of legal insulation.

**International business operations.** A Nevis entity works equally well for operating businesses with international client bases, or for business models where the principals prefer an international structure. U.S. persons who are shareholders or members will have IRS reporting requirements regardless of where the entity is formed, and we ensure these are fully addressed.

## IRS Compliance for U.S. Persons

We strongly recommend the pass-through tax election for eligible Nevis entities owned by U.S. persons. This process includes:

- Filing Form SS-4 to obtain an EIN for the company
- Filing Form 8832 to elect Foreign Disregarded Entity (single member) or Foreign Partnership (multiple members) status

Once the entity has pass-through status, the complex Controlled Foreign Corporation filing (Form 5471) is eliminated. Instead, you will work with your CPA to file:

- **Form 8858** — For single-member FDE status, filed with your Form 1040
- **Form 8865** — For foreign partnership status, along with a standard U.S. partnership Form 1065

Additional reporting requirements for foreign financial assets and accounts typically include:

- **Form 8938** — Statement of Specified Foreign Financial Assets
- **FBAR (FinCEN Form 114)** — Report of Foreign Bank and Financial Accounts (filed online)

We provide filing deadline reminders once you become a client. We also recommend working with a CPA experienced in international tax reporting, and we can provide referrals upon request.

Disclosing assets to the IRS does not grant the IRS the power to seize them. Once outside the U.S., the IRS becomes a creditor like any other and must pursue collection through complex legal procedures in the foreign jurisdiction.

## FAQ

**How long does Nevis formation take?**
Typically 2-3 weeks from receipt of completed KYC documents. Bank account opening may require an additional 2-4 weeks.

**Should I choose an LLC or corporation?**
For most asset protection purposes, the LLC is preferred due to its charging order protection and lower formation cost. Corporations may be better suited for certain business operations. We can advise based on your specific goals.

**Can I be the manager of my own Nevis LLC?**
Yes, but using our nominee director service keeps your name off public records and facilitates the banking process. You maintain full control through your membership interest.

**What if the bank denies my account application?**
If we are unable to open an account, the account opening fee is refunded. Formation package fees are not refundable, as the entity will already be formed and operational.

**Is Nevis on any international blacklists?**
St. Kitts and Nevis participates in the Caribbean Court of Appeals Circuit and maintains compliance with international regulatory standards. It is not on the FATF or OECD blacklists.
