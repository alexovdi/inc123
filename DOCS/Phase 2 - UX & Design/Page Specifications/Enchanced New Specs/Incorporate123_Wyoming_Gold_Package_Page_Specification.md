## 1. Page Purpose & Strategic Role

### 1.1 Primary Goal

**Convert visitors into buyers.** This is the single highest-value conversion endpoint on the entire site. The Wyoming Gold package at $1,275 is the hero product — the package the entire site architecture is designed to funnel visitors toward. Every pillar page, every cluster page, every comparison page, and the homepage wizard all ultimately point here (or to Nevada Gold). This page must close the sale.

### 1.2 The Page's Job Is Singular

Unlike pillar pages (educate), cluster pages (deepen), and comparison pages (compare), the package page has one job: **resolve the final buying objections and drive the visitor to checkout.** By the time someone lands here, they've already been educated on privacy, understand nominees, know they want Wyoming, and believe Incorporate123 is the right provider. This page must:

1. Confirm they're on the right page (the right package, right state, right tier)
2. Show exactly what's included — no ambiguity, no hidden fees
3. Let them choose entity type (LLC vs. Corporation)
4. Offer relevant add-ons without creating decision fatigue
5. Provide one final round of trust signals
6. Handle the last 3–5 objections via FAQ
7. Give them a clear, confident path to checkout

### 1.3 Position in User Journeys

This page is the penultimate step (before checkout) in three primary journeys:

| Journey | Path to This Page | Visitor State |
|---------|-------------------|---------------|
| **A: Privacy Researcher** | /privacy/ → /anonymous-llc/ → /nominee-services/ → /wyoming-vs-nevada-privacy/ → **HERE** | Fully educated, high confidence, ready to buy |
| **C: Asset Protector (upsold)** | /asset-protection/ → /charging-order-protection/ → /wyoming-asset-protection/ → cross-sell to privacy → **HERE** | Came for AP, discovered privacy need, converting to Gold |
| **E: Formation Researcher (upsold)** | /formation/ → /llc-vs-corporation/ → /wyoming-vs-nevada/ → privacy upsell bridge → **HERE** | Came for formation, upsold to Gold via "70% add privacy" messaging |

Additionally, informed buyers (Journey D) arrive here directly from the homepage, state hub, or mega menu with minimal preceding pages.

### 1.4 Success Metrics

- **Primary:** Click-through to `/checkout/` (checkout entry rate)
- **Secondary:** Add-on attachment rate (foreign registration, virtual office upgrades)
- **Tertiary:** Consultation booking (for visitors not ready to self-serve)
- **Conversion target:** 8–15% of page visitors enter checkout (high-intent page)
- **Bounce target:** < 30% (visitors arrive with strong purchase intent)
- **Time on page target:** 2–4 minutes (scan pricing, verify inclusions, checkout)

### 1.5 Competitive Context

This page competes directly with:

| Competitor | Comparable Product | Price | Key Weakness |
|-----------|-------------------|-------|-------------|
| Wyoming Corporate Services | Basic Privacy Package | $1,140 + add-ons | Nominee renewal $400/yr extra; no all-inclusive pricing |

Incorporate123's competitive advantage: **all-inclusive pricing with year-round nominees included, at a lower annual renewal than any comparable service.**

## 2. Section Order & Narrative Arc

Package pages are NOT persuasion arcs — the persuasion happened on pillar and cluster pages. Package pages are **decision-confirmation and transaction-facilitation pages.** The arc is: confirm → detail → customize → trust → resolve → buy.

| # | Section | Role | Visitor Mindset |
|---|---------|------|-----------------|
| 1 | Package Hero | **Confirm** — Right page, right price, right tier | "Is this the one I want?" |
| 2 | Entity Type Toggle | **Choose** — LLC or Corporation | "LLC or Corp?" |
| 3 | Tier Comparison Grid | **Context** — Gold vs. Silver vs. Bronze | "Am I picking the right tier?" |
| 4 | Full Inclusions List | **Detail** — Every item, no ambiguity | "What exactly do I get?" |
| 5 | Value Comparison Callout | **Justify** — Total value vs. competitors | "Is this a good deal?" |
| 6 | Add-On Configurator | **Customize** — Optional extras with running total | "Do I need anything else?" |
| 7 | How It Works (Post-Purchase) | **Reassure** — What happens after you buy | "What happens next?" |
| 8 | Trust & Social Proof | **Validate** — Final confidence builder | "Can I trust this company with $1,275?" |
| 9 | FAQ Accordion | **Resolve** — Last 6–8 objections | "Wait, one more question..." |
| 10 | Also Consider | **Navigate** — Related packages | "Should I look at Nevada instead?" |
| 11 | Final Conversion CTA | **Close** — Duplicate of hero CTA for full-page scrollers | "Let's do this." |

**Running Total Sidebar (persistent, desktop):** Package price + selected add-ons + total. Follows scroll. Contains the primary "Proceed to Checkout" button at all scroll depths.

### Why This Order Works

**Section 1 (Hero):** The visitor needs immediate confirmation: right package, right state, right price. The hero delivers all three in the first viewport — package name, price, renewal rate, and a "Start Now" CTA. Visitors with high confidence can click immediately without scrolling.

**Section 2 (Entity Toggle):** LLC vs. Corporation is the remaining micro-decision. Most visitors have already decided (85%+ choose LLC), but the toggle must be present because pricing and inclusions differ slightly for corporations.

**Section 3 (Tier Comparison):** Even though the visitor arrived at the Gold page, they need to see Gold in context — what Silver includes (and what it doesn't) and why Gold is worth the $400 premium. This grid is the final "yes, Gold is right for me" confirmation. It also catches visitors who arrived at the wrong tier and routes them to Silver.

**Section 4 (Full Inclusions):** The complete, itemized list of everything included. No "see details" links, no hidden footnotes. This is the all-inclusive pricing promise in action. Every line item has a brief tooltip explaining what it is.

**Section 5 (Value Comparison):** Reframes price from "what I'm spending" to "what I'm getting." Shows the $2,000+ in Year 1 service value. Briefly contrasts with competitor pricing model (without naming competitors).

**Section 6 (Add-On Configurator):** Optional extras — foreign state registration, virtual office upgrade, corporate DBA. Each add-on has a checkbox, price, brief description, and dynamically updates the running total in the sidebar.

**Section 7 (Post-Purchase):** Unique to package pages. Answers "what happens after I click buy?" — reduces checkout anxiety by showing the 4-step process from purchase to fully formed entity.

**Section 8–11 (Trust + FAQ + Also Consider + Final CTA):** Final objection handling, trust reinforcement, related package navigation, and a duplicate CTA for visitors who scrolled the full page.
