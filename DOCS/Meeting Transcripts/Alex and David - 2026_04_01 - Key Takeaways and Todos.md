## Key Decisions

### 1. Phase 1 Approved

Phase 1 (architecture, sitemap, migration plan) is approved pending minor updates from David's redline feedback. Phase 2 begins immediately with packages as the first priority.

### 2. Package Tier Structure Clarified

David provided definitive tier definitions:

- **Bronze** = basic company formation + compliance (RA, filings, records)
- **Silver** = Bronze + virtual office + mail forwarding
- **Gold** = Silver + nominee privacy service (ONLY difference)

This means:

- Virtual office is NOT an add-on -- it's what defines the Silver tier
- Compliance IS included in Bronze (our docs had it as add-on for Bronze)
- The only difference between Gold and Silver is the nominee privacy service

### 3. Six Core Packages

Bronze, Silver, Gold for both Wyoming and Nevada. CA/FL Private packages kept for convenience (= WY + foreign registration) due to high client volume from those states.

### 4. Wyoming Is Now the Default

~70% of clients are in Wyoming. Reasons:

- WY costs: ~$100/yr (corps and LLCs)
- NV costs: ~$700/yr (corps), ~$425/yr (LLCs)
- Most clients run virtual businesses -- state doesn't matter, so cheaper WY wins
- Nevada only for clients with actual NV presence or NV preference

### 5. Only ~3 True Add-Ons

David explicitly said most things we listed as add-ons are INCLUDED in packages. True add-ons:

- Foreign state filing
- Domestication
- A few others (NV telephone, NV business license -- offered post-purchase, not at checkout)

Not add-ons (included in packages):

- Virtual office (Silver/Gold)
- Mail forwarding (Silver/Gold)
- EIN -- still unclear, redline says "add-on" but old site had it free
- Operating Agreement -- needs clarification

### 6. No A La Carte Services

Packages only. Never offered a la carte. More profitable and ensures clients get what they need.

### 7. Wizard Concept Approved

David approved the wizard/guide idea. His recommended flow:

1. **Business formation or asset protection?**
2. If formation: **Virtual business or brick-and-mortar?**
   - Virtual --> Wyoming
   - Brick-and-mortar --> Which state? --> Wyoming + foreign registration
3. If asset protection: --> Almost always Wyoming
4. Must also allow direct package browsing for users who know what they want

### 8. Shelf Companies = Entity + Package

Shelf companies are a separate product within company formation. Customer buys an existing entity, THEN adds a Bronze/Silver/Gold package on top.

### 9. URL Change: "Nevada Incorporation" --> "Company Formation"

Pillar should be "Company Formation" (state-agnostic), not tied to a specific state. Individual state pages can still exist but the hub is general.

### 10. Offshore: Keep for Credibility, Don't Promote

Keep offshore pages for SEO and credibility (differentiator vs competitors). Don't actively promote or link prominently. Services not currently offered if asked.

### 11. Compliance Pillar Confirmed Separate

David confirmed compliance should be a separate pillar. Key message: "Sign up for one of our packages and we take care of everything." Everything except tax filings is included.

### 12. Post-Purchase Process Documented

No immediate credit card charge. Flow: Order --> review --> invoice (FreshBooks) --> payment --> file with state --> get tax ID --> collect client info --> prepare ~40 pages (mail-merged from ACT, ~500 fields) --> send electronically --> annual renewal.

### 13. Customer Portal: Future Phase

Discussed but deferred. Main blocker is ACT integration (~500 database fields, 40-page document mail merging). Alex mentioned potential solutions to discuss in Phase 2+.
