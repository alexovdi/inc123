01 Primary Conversion Framework

Every journey follows the same five-stage funnel. The visitor's intent determines which pillar they enter, but the progression is consistent: educate → deepen → compare → convert.

Design Principle: Not every visitor follows all five stages. The architecture must support both linear progression (researcher who reads everything) and stage-skipping (buyer who knows what they want and jumps straight to packages). Every page must offer both a "learn more" path downward and a "ready to buy" shortcut to packages.

Stage Definitions

Stage Page Types Visitor Mindset Primary Goal
1. Intent Landing Homepage, Pillar pages, State hubs "I have a problem — do you solve it?" Validate relevance, build trust, orient visitor to correct path
2. Deep Education Cluster pages (topic-specific) "Tell me exactly how this works" Build expertise authority, answer specific questions, surface related topics
3. Decision Support Comparison pages, state-vs-state "Which option is right for me?" Help visitor self-select the correct state/entity/package combination
4. Package Evaluation Package detail pages "What do I get and what does it cost?" Present clear value, differentiate tiers, drive add-on selection
5. Conversion Checkout flow (3-4 steps) "Let's do this" Minimize friction, maintain confidence, complete purchase

Journey A: The Privacy Researcher

Full Funnel · 4–7 pages

Persona: Small business owner with $500K+ assets. Heard about anonymous LLCs, wants to understand options before committing. Will read detailed content. Willing to pay premium for genuine privacy.

  1. Searches "how to form anonymous LLC" → lands on Privacy Pillar or Anonymous LLC cluster /privacy/ or /anonymous-llc/
  2. Reads pillar overview → learns about nominee services, state privacy statutes, BOI requirements CTA: "Compare Wyoming vs Nevada for Privacy →"
  3. Clicks into Wyoming Privacy cluster → reads about WY Statute 17-29-302, no public ownership disclosure /wyoming-privacy-llc/ CTA: "See How Our Nominees Protect Your Identity →"
  4. Reviews Nominee Services page → understands year-round vs "nominee for a day," Incorporate123's differentiator /nominee-services/ Cross-pillar: "Also protects your assets →" links to Asset Protection
  5. Compares states on comparison page → side-by-side table helps choose Wyoming over Nevada for privacy /wyoming-vs-nevada-privacy/ CTA: "Get Started with Wyoming Gold →"
  6. Reviews Wyoming Gold package → scans 3-tier pricing, reads progressive disclosure details, selects add-ons /wyoming-private-incorporation/ CTA: "Start My Private LLC — $1,275" / "Schedule a Consultation"
  7. Enters checkout flow → Package config → Entity details → Payment → Confirmation /checkout/

Journey B: CA/FL Resident Seeking Privacy

Variant · State-specific

Persona: California or Florida resident. Home state has public disclosure requirements. Needs WY/NV entity + foreign registration back in home state. Higher-value package ($2,500–$3,200).

  1. Searches "California anonymous LLC" or "private LLC California" → Lands on Privacy Pillar or California State Hub
  2. Discovers the problem: CA requires public LLC disclosure. Solution: form in WY/NV, register foreign in CA /privacy/ → /california-private-incorporation/
  3. Reads CA Private cluster → understands dual-state structure, ongoing compliance needs CTA: "See California Private Package Pricing →"
  4. Reviews CA Private package → all-inclusive: WY formation + nominees + CA foreign reg + registered agent /california-private-incorporation/ CTA: "Protect Your California Business — $2,500"
  5. Checkout
UX Decision: The Privacy Pillar page must feature both a general "anonymous LLC" path and a "Which state are you in?" selector that routes CA/FL visitors directly to their state-specific privacy packages. This prevents CA/FL visitors from going through the full WY vs NV comparison only to discover they need a completely different package structure.

Journey C: The Lawsuit-Averse Business Owner

Full Funnel · 4–6 pages

Persona: Real estate investor or business owner who has been sued or knows someone who has. Understands legal defense costs $30K–$100K minimum. Wants strongest possible LLC protection. May not initially realize they also want privacy.

  1. Searches "best state for LLC asset protection" → lands on AP Pillar /asset-protection/
  2. Reads about charging order protection → learns WY/NV have strongest statutes /charging-order-protection/ CTA: "Compare WY vs NV Protection →"
  3. Reads state-specific AP cluster → understands WY's sole remedy charging order provision /wyoming-asset-protection/ Cross-pillar: "Most asset protection clients also add privacy — here's why →"
  4. Cross-pillar moment: Clicks through to Privacy Pillar or Nominee Services to learn about keeping ownership hidden from potential litigants /nominee-services/ or /privacy/
  5. Returns to comparison or goes directly to Gold package (upsold from Silver intent to Gold via privacy cross-sell) /wyoming-private-incorporation/ CTA: "Maximum Protection: Gold Package with Privacy →"
  6. Checkout — Gold package with potential add-ons (virtual office, additional entities)
Cross-Sell Opportunity: Asset protection visitors who enter planning to buy Silver frequently upgrade to Gold once they understand that hiding asset ownership from potential plaintiffs (privacy) is a critical layer of asset protection strategy. Every AP cluster page must include a cross-pillar CTA that frames privacy as an extension of asset protection, not a separate service. Estimated upsell rate target: 30–40% of AP visitors converting to Gold instead of Silver.

Journey D: The Informed Buyer

Short Path · 1–2 pages

Persona: Has already decided on state and entity type. Comparing providers on price, inclusions, and trust. May have been referred by an accountant or attorney. Wants to get it done quickly.

  1. Lands on Homepage or State Hub → sees direct package links in hero or state-specific CTA blocks / or /wyoming/ or /nevada/ CTA: "Wyoming LLC from $900" / "Nevada LLC from $1,000"
  2. Goes directly to package page → scans 3-tier pricing, verifies inclusions, selects tier and add-ons /wyoming-incorporation/ Cross-sell: "Most clients add privacy protection for $375 more →"
  3. Checkout — Silver or Bronze package

Journey E: The Formation Researcher

Full Funnel · 3–5 pages

Persona: Knows they need a business entity but hasn't decided LLC vs Corp, Wyoming vs Nevada, or what level of services they need. Researching options before committing. Price-conscious but can be upsold on value.

  1. Searches "LLC vs corporation" → lands on Formation Pillar or LLC vs Corp comparison /formation/ or /llc-vs-corporation/
  2. Reads entity comparison → decides on LLC (most common for privacy and AP) CTA: "Next: Choose Your State →"
  3. Reads WY vs NV formation comparison → compares fees, speed, annual costs /wyoming-vs-nevada/ Cross-sell: "Need privacy? See why 70% of our clients add nominee services →"
  4. Selects state and views package → Silver package for formation-only, or upsells to Gold after cross-sell /wyoming-incorporation/ or /wyoming-private-incorporation/
  5. Checkout
Strategic Note: Formation is where Incorporate123 competes directly with budget providers (ZenBusiness $0 + state fees, LegalZoom $0–$299). The formation journey must immediately differentiate on what's included (registered agent, company secretary, custom records, FREE EIN/bank account) rather than competing on base price. Every formation page should include a "What's Really Included" comparison showing Incorporate123's all-inclusive pricing vs. competitors' nickel-and-diming after checkout.

05 Cross-Pillar Navigation

The four-pillar architecture must not create four disconnected silos. Fourteen pages serve multiple pillars simultaneously. These cross-pillar moments are the highest-leverage points for upselling and for ensuring visitors discover the full scope of Incorporate123's services.

Cross-Pillar Link Map

Origin Pillar Trigger Content Destination Pillar CTA / Link Pattern
Asset Protection Any AP cluster page discussing LLC ownership Privacy "Hiding your ownership is the first layer of asset protection → Nominee Services"
Formation Any formation page after entity type selection Privacy "Most clients add privacy protection — only $375 more → Wyoming Gold"
Privacy Anonymous LLC page discussing why privacy matters Asset Protection "Privacy protects your identity. Asset protection protects your wealth →"
Privacy Nominee services page Compliance "Your nominee is year-round — see what's included in annual compliance →"
Formation LLC vs Corporation comparison Asset Protection "LLCs offer charging order protection that corporations don't →"
Asset Protection Charging Order Protection page Formation "Ready to form your protected LLC? →"
All Pillars Any cluster or comparison page Compliance "Every package includes year-round registered agent + compliance →"
Anti-Pattern to Avoid: Cross-pillar links must feel like helpful recommendations, not random upsells. Each cross-pillar CTA should be placed at the content moment where the visitor naturally thinks "what else should I consider?" — typically after they've absorbed the core content of the current page and before the final package CTA. Never put cross-pillar CTAs above the primary conversion CTA on any page.

Cross-Pillar UX Patterns

Pattern 1

Contextual Callout Box

Inline within content body. Appears when a paragraph naturally introduces a cross-pillar concept. Styled as a distinct callout (not banner) so it reads as editorial recommendation.

💡 "Did you know? Privacy isn't just about anonymity — it's your first line of asset defense." → Link to AP Pillar
Pattern 2

"Also Relevant" Sidebar Block

Persistent sidebar element on cluster pages. Shows 2–3 related pages from other pillars. Contextually generated per page, not a global widget.

Related Topics: Nominee Services · Charging Order Protection · LLC vs Corporation
Pattern 3

Upgrade Prompt on Package Pages

On Silver/Bronze package pages, a styled comparison showing Gold package benefits. Framed as "Most clients choose Gold because…" social proof.

⬆ "70% of Wyoming clients add privacy protection" → Compare Silver vs Gold
Pattern 4

Post-Education Navigation Block

After the main content and FAQ section but before the footer. Shows "Where to go next" with pillar-aware suggestions based on current page context.

Next Steps: Compare States → | View Packages → | Schedule Consultation →

06 Secondary Journeys

Lower-volume but strategically important paths that the architecture must support. These visitors have specific needs that don't fit the primary pillar funnels.

Journey F: Returning Client — Compliance Services

Returning · 1–2 pages

Persona: Existing Incorporate123 client. Needs registered agent renewal, annual report filing, virtual office change, or additional entity. Knows the company, just needs to find the right service page or client login.

  1. Returns to site via bookmark, email link, or direct URL /

  3. If compliance service needed: Finds Registered Agent, Annual Reports, or Virtual Office cluster under Compliance pillar /registered-agent/ or /annual-reports/ Cross-sell: "Need an additional entity? Existing clients save on formation →"

Journey G: Offshore Inquiry

De-emphasized · Consultation funnel

Persona: HNW individual or international business seeking Nevis LLC, BVI company, or Bahamas trust. Complex needs requiring consultation. High AOV ($2,000–$12,000+) but low volume.

  1. Searches "Nevis LLC" or "offshore company formation" → lands on Offshore page (retained for SEO, not in main nav) /offshore/
  2. Reads offshore options → understands Nevis, BVI, and trust structures CTA: "Schedule Offshore Consultation" (phone/form, NOT self-service checkout)
  3. Consultation call → David or team handles complex structuring discussion

Journey H: Shelf Company Buyer

Specialty · High intent

Persona: Needs an aged entity immediately — for credit establishment, banking, contract requirements, or instant credibility. Willing to pay premium ($2,000–$12,000). Knows what they want; needs to see available inventory.

  1. Searches "buy aged LLC" or "shelf company Nevada/Wyoming" → lands on Shelf Companies page /shelf-companies/
  2. Browses available inventory → filters by state, age, entity type CTA: "Purchase This Entity" (per listing) / "Request Custom Age"

Journey I: State Hub Entry

Geographic entry · Router page

Persona: Searches for a state directly ("Wyoming LLC" or "Nevada incorporation"). Hasn't decided on intent yet. The State Hub page must quickly route them to the right pillar based on what they actually need.

  1. Searches "Wyoming LLC" → lands on Wyoming State Hub /wyoming/

Journey J: BOI Reporting Concerned Visitor

07 Navigation Requirements for UX Phase

Specific navigation design requirements derived from the journey maps. These feed directly into wireframing and information architecture decisions.

Primary Navigation (Header)

Req N-1: Intent-First Main Navigation Critical

Main nav must lead with the four pillars by intent, not geography. Recommended structure: Privacy · Asset Protection · Formation · Packages · About

Rationale: Journeys A–E all begin with intent. Geographic entry (Journey I) is handled by state hubs and SEO landing pages, not primary navigation.

Req N-2: Persistent Client Login Critical

Client Login link must be visible in the header on every page — either as a text link in the utility nav or a subtle button. Returning clients (Journey F) should never have to scroll to find login.

Req N-3: Direct Package Access Critical

Informed buyers (Journey D) must reach package pages in one click from the header. A "Packages" or "Pricing" nav item should show all packages in a dropdown/mega menu organized by state and tier.

Req N-4: Phone Number in Header High

Consultation-oriented visitors (Journeys G, K) and prospects who want to talk before buying must see a phone number in the header. Mobile: tap-to-call. Desktop: visible with business hours.

Req N-5: Mega Menu with Pillar Depth High

Each pillar nav item should open a mega menu showing key cluster pages and comparison pages. This lets mid-journey visitors jump between stages without backtracking. Include a "Featured" or "Most Popular" highlight for the Anonymous LLC and WY Gold pages.

Secondary Navigation (On-Page)

Req N-6: Breadcrumb Trail Critical

Every cluster, comparison, and package page must show breadcrumb navigation: Home → Pillar → Current Page. For cross-pillar pages, show the primary pillar path. Breadcrumbs reinforce the architecture for both users and search engines.

Req N-7: "Where to Go Next" Block High

Every content page (pillars, clusters, comparisons) must end with a contextual navigation block suggesting logical next steps. This block must be journey-aware: a privacy cluster page suggests comparison → package, while a formation cluster suggests privacy cross-sell → package.

Req N-8: Pillar-Aware Sidebar Medium

Cluster pages should include a sidebar or "Related Topics" section showing other clusters within the same pillar, plus 1–2 cross-pillar links. Mobile: collapses to end-of-content block.

Req N-9: State Hub as Router High

State Hub pages (/wyoming/, /nevada/) must function as routing pages, not content pages. Primary UX: three clear intent-based paths (Privacy / Asset Protection / Formation) with brief "why this state" overview. Secondary: links to all state-relevant packages and compliance services.

Mobile Navigation

Req N-10: Mobile Sticky CTA Bar Critical

Mobile must have a sticky bottom bar with two actions: primary CTA (context-dependent — "View Packages" on education pages, "Get Started" on package pages) and phone icon (tap-to-call). This supports both self-service and consultation funnels.

Req N-11: Mobile Hamburger with Pillar Structure High

Mobile menu must maintain the pillar-first structure, not flatten to an alphabetical list. Each pillar expands to show its clusters. Packages and Login should be above the fold in the mobile menu.

Footer Navigation

Req N-12: Complete Footer Sitemap Medium

Footer should include all four pillar sections with their top cluster pages, all package pages, state hubs, and utility pages. Offshore content is included in the footer (not main nav) to maintain SEO without primary positioning.

08 CTA Placement Recommendations

Specific CTA types, copy direction, and placement rules for each stage of the conversion funnel. Each CTA must balance moving visitors toward conversion without disrupting the education experience.

CTA Framework by Funnel Stage

Stage 1 · Pillar Pages (Education)

Soft CTAs + Direct Shortcut

Primary: Guide to next logical cluster page. Educational, not salesy. "Learn about anonymous LLCs →" or "How charging orders protect your assets →"

Secondary: "Ready now?" shortcut to packages for visitors who skip education. Positioned as a subtle text link, not competing with primary flow.

Placement: Primary CTA after each major content section. Shortcut CTA in a right-sidebar sticky block (desktop) or after the hero (mobile).

Primary: "Explore Anonymous LLC Options →"
Shortcut: "Already know what you need? View packages →"
Stage 2 · Cluster Pages (Deep Education)

Progression CTA + Cross-Pillar

Primary: Move toward comparison or package. "Compare Wyoming vs Nevada →" or "See Gold Package Details →"

Cross-pillar: One contextual cross-pillar callout per page, placed mid-content where the topic naturally connects.

Placement: Primary CTA after main content, before FAQ. Cross-pillar callout inline within content body. "Schedule Consultation" as tertiary option.

Primary: "Compare States for Privacy →"
Cross: "Also protects your assets — learn how →"
Tertiary: "Have questions? Talk to our team →"
Stage 3 · Comparison Pages (Decision)

Dual Package CTAs

Primary: Two competing package CTAs — one per state/option being compared. Must be equally weighted so the comparison feels objective, not biased.

Below table: "Not sure? Schedule a free consultation" for visitors who need human guidance.

Placement: Dual CTAs immediately after the comparison table (above the fold on desktop). Repeated at page bottom after supporting content.

"Get Started with Wyoming Gold — $1,275" | "Get Started with Nevada Gold — $1,800"
Below: "Need help deciding? Free 15-minute consultation →"
Stage 4 · Package Pages (Conversion)

Strong Conversion CTAs

Primary: "Start My [Entity Type] — $[price]" with clear pricing. Must appear above the fold in the 3-tier comparison grid. Each tier gets its own CTA.

Tier upgrade: Gold tier should be visually highlighted ("Most Popular" badge, different background color).

Dual CTA: "Start Online" button + "Call to Order" for phone-preference visitors.

Placement: Top of 3-tier grid (above fold), sticky on scroll (mobile), repeated after FAQ section.

Tier CTAs: "Start Silver — $900" · "Start Gold — $1,275 ★ Most Popular" · "Start Gold + VO — $1,675"
Secondary: "📞 Prefer to order by phone? Call (XXX) XXX-XXXX"

CTA Copy Principles

Principle Do Don't
Specificity "Start My Wyoming LLC — $900" "Get Started" / "Learn More"
Action-Oriented "Protect Your Business Now →" "Click Here" / "Submit"
Value Framing "All-Inclusive: Formation + Agent + EIN" "Starting at $900" (implies hidden costs)
Urgency (Subtle) "Same-day filing available" "Limited time offer!" / "Act now!"
Trust Reinforcement "Join 40,000+ businesses protected" "Buy now" without social proof
Consultation Option "Questions? Free 15-min consultation" No phone/consultation alternative

CTA Placement Rules

Rule Description
Above-Fold Presence Every page must have at least one CTA visible without scrolling. On education pages this can be a subtle "View Packages" link; on package pages it must be the primary conversion button.
Post-Content Repeat The primary CTA must repeat after the main content section and after the FAQ section. Visitors who read everything should find a conversion path waiting at the end.
One Primary, One Secondary Each page has exactly one primary CTA style (strongest visual weight) and one secondary CTA style (text link or outlined button). Never show three competing CTA styles on one page.
Cross-Pillar Below Primary Cross-pillar CTAs must always appear below or after the page's primary conversion CTA. The primary journey must never be interrupted by cross-sells.
Mobile Priority On mobile, the sticky bottom CTA bar takes precedence. In-content CTAs can be less prominent since the sticky bar is always accessible.
Consultation as Safety Net Every page with a self-service conversion CTA must also offer a "Talk to us" alternative. Phone number or consultation form. High-value prospects often prefer human contact before purchasing.
