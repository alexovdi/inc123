# Wyoming Business Formation & Privacy

Wyoming offers the strongest combination of privacy protections, asset protection statutes, and low costs of any U.S. state. With a $100 filing fee, no state income tax, no franchise tax, and the most protective charging order statutes in the country, Wyoming has become the preferred jurisdiction for business owners who prioritize keeping their personal information out of public records while legally shielding their assets.

## Why Wyoming?

Wyoming was the first state to create the LLC entity type in 1977, and it has continued to refine its business laws to remain at the forefront of privacy and asset protection. Here are the key advantages:

- **No Member or Manager Disclosure** — Wyoming does not require the names of LLC members or managers to appear in any public filing. Your ownership is private from the moment of formation.
- **Strongest Charging Order Protection** — Wyoming's statutes make the charging order the exclusive remedy available to a creditor of an LLC member. This means creditors cannot force a sale, force distributions, or seize your membership interest.
- **No State Income Tax** — Wyoming imposes no personal or corporate income tax. There is no franchise tax and no gross receipts tax.
- **$100 State Filing Fee** — One of the lowest formation costs in the country. Annual report fees are equally modest at $62 (or based on Wyoming assets for larger companies).
- **Lifetime Proxy** — Wyoming allows a lifetime proxy on shares or membership interests, enabling you to maintain voting control through a nominee while keeping your name completely out of the picture.
- **No Information-Sharing Agreement with the IRS** — Wyoming does not share business formation data with the IRS, adding another practical layer of privacy.

## Asset Protection in Wyoming

Wyoming's exclusive charging order remedy is the gold standard for domestic asset protection. A creditor who wins a judgment against you personally cannot force your Wyoming LLC to distribute funds, cannot seize your membership interest, and cannot gain any management control. The creditor is limited to a charging order, which entitles them only to distributions if and when the LLC chooses to make them.

This protection applies to single-member LLCs in Wyoming, a distinction that many other states do not offer.

## FAQ

**Can I use a Wyoming LLC if I live in another state?**
Yes. You can form a Wyoming LLC and register it as a foreign entity in your home state. This is a common strategy to gain Wyoming's privacy and asset protection benefits while operating locally.

**Does Wyoming require an operating agreement?**
Wyoming does not require you to file an operating agreement with the state, but having one is essential for defining management structure, ownership, and distributions. Our packages include custom operating agreement preparation.

**How quickly can I form a Wyoming LLC?**
Wyoming offers same-day processing. With our packages, you can have a fully formed entity, including EIN and bank account application, within days of completing your order.

**Is Wyoming better than Nevada for privacy?**
For most clients, yes. Wyoming offers comparable privacy protections at significantly lower cost ($100 filing fee vs. $425+ in Nevada), no business license requirement, and lower annual maintenance fees. Nevada may be preferable if you need a physical office presence or if your business specifically benefits from Nevada's court system.

**Can creditors from my home state reach my Wyoming LLC assets?**
Wyoming's charging order protection applies regardless of where the creditor obtains their judgment. The creditor must respect Wyoming law when attempting to reach assets held in a Wyoming LLC.

**Do I need to visit Wyoming to form my LLC?**
No. The entire process is handled remotely. Your registered agent provides the required Wyoming address.

**What is a lifetime proxy and how does it help with privacy?**
Wyoming allows a lifetime proxy on membership interests or shares, meaning you can assign voting rights to a nominee permanently. This enables you to maintain economic ownership while your nominee exercises all visible control — adding another layer of privacy beyond standard nominee services.

**Does Wyoming allow Series LLCs?**
Yes. Wyoming's Series LLC statute allows you to create separate "series" within a single LLC, each with its own assets, members, and liabilities. This is popular among real estate investors holding multiple properties, as each property can be isolated in its own series without forming separate entities.

**What is Wyoming's annual report fee based on?**
The annual report fee is based on the value of assets located in Wyoming. The minimum is $62 (for entities with less than $300,000 in Wyoming assets). Most out-of-state clients pay the minimum because their assets are not physically located in Wyoming.
