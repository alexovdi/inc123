# Anonymous LLC Formation

When you form an LLC in most states, your name goes directly into the Secretary of State database as the manager, member, or organizer. That record is public, permanent, and searchable by anyone. An anonymous LLC is structured so that your personal information never enters the public record — a nominee holds the titled positions, your name stays off state filings, and you retain full ownership and operational control through non-public roles.

Incorporate123 has been forming anonymous LLCs in Wyoming and Nevada for 25 years. ## What Makes an LLC "Anonymous"?

The term "anonymous LLC" is not a special entity type recognized by any state. It is a practical description of how the LLC is structured and filed. An anonymous LLC is a standard limited liability company — with the same legal protections, tax treatment, and operational flexibility — but formed in a way that keeps the real owner's name out of publicly searchable state records.

This is accomplished through two mechanisms working together. First, the LLC is formed in a state that does not require public disclosure of members. Wyoming is the strongest option here: under the Wyoming LLC Act, member names are not required in the articles of organization and are not filed with the Secretary of State. Nevada similarly allows formation without listing members in public filings.

Second, nominee services ensure that the positions which do appear in public records — the manager of a manager-managed LLC, the organizer on the articles of organization — are held by a nominee rather than the actual owner. The owner operates the company from a non-public position such as CEO or Executive Vice President, maintaining full day-to-day control.

## The Offshore Records Custodian

Nevada statutes (NRS 78.105, NRS 86.241) require registered agents to maintain certain records on their corporate clients, including membership lists and stock ledgers. Under NRS 86.246, the Secretary of State can request these records from the registered agent.

Our solution is straightforward: we appoint an offshore company secretary as the custodian of your stock ledger or membership listing. If the Secretary of State requests records, the registered agent provides the name and address of the offshore custodian — not your personal ownership information. The operating agreement is structured to contain no personal information, and the membership listing is maintained as a separate document outside the operating agreement.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is an anonymous LLC legal?**
Yes. There is nothing illegal about forming an LLC with nominee officers and managers. The entity complies with all state formation requirements. You report all income on your personal tax return as required by the IRS. The privacy applies to public state records, not federal tax filings.

**Does Wyoming really not require member names?**
Correct. Wyoming's LLC Act does not require member names in the articles of organization, and the Secretary of State does not collect or publish this information. This is a statutory protection, not a loophole.

**What if someone sues my LLC — will my name come out?**
In active litigation, a court can order discovery that may reveal ownership. Anonymous LLC formation protects you from casual public record searches — it does not provide immunity from court-ordered discovery in a lawsuit. That said, the privacy makes it significantly harder for a potential plaintiff to identify your assets in the first place, which is often the most effective deterrent.

**Can I open a bank account with an anonymous LLC?**
Yes. Banks require beneficial ownership information under federal anti-money laundering rules (the Corporate Transparency Act), but this information is reported to FinCEN — it does not become part of the public state record. You will provide your ID to the bank; your identity simply will not be searchable through state databases.

**Do I need to live in Wyoming or Nevada?**
No. You can form an LLC in Wyoming or Nevada regardless of where you live. If you operate your business in another state, you will need to foreign-register there.
