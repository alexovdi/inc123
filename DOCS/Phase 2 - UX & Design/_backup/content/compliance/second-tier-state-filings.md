# Second-Tier State Filings

When you form an LLC or corporation, filing the Articles of Organization or Articles of Incorporation is only the first step. Every state requires additional filings after formation — filings that most competitors either do not tell you about or charge extra for after the fact.

These post-formation requirements are what we call "second-tier" state filings. They are mandatory, they have strict deadlines, and failing to complete them on time results in penalties and can jeopardize your entity's good standing before it has been active for even a month.

## What Are Second-Tier State Filings?

Second-tier state filings are the mandatory government filings required after your entity's formation documents are accepted by the Secretary of State. These are not optional add-ons. They are legal requirements that must be completed within specific timeframes.

The specific filings depend on the state, but the most common include:

### Initial List of Officers or Managers

In Nevada, every newly formed entity must file an Initial List of Officers/Directors (corporations) or Managers (LLCs) with the Secretary of State. This filing is due by the last day of the month following the month of formation.

**What it includes:**
- For corporations: President, Treasurer, Secretary, and all Directors — names and addresses
- For LLCs: LLC Manager(s) — names and addresses

**Filing fee:** $150

If you purchased privacy or nominee services, we prepare and file this list with nominee officers, directors, or managers so that your personal name does not appear on the public record. This is one of the most important privacy steps in the formation process, and it happens immediately after formation.

### State Business License

Nevada requires every entity to obtain a State Business License. This is separate from any local business license your city or county may require.

**Filing fee:**
- LLCs: $200
- Corporations: $500

This fee is due at the same time as the Initial List and annually thereafter. Failure to maintain a valid business license while operating a business in Nevada can result in fines of $1,000 to $10,000 under NRS 76.180.

### Wyoming Initial Requirements

Wyoming's post-formation requirements are simpler and less expensive. The first annual report is due on the first day of the anniversary month of formation, with a filing fee of $62. There is no separate business license requirement at the state level.

### Other State-Specific Filings

Different states have different post-formation requirements:

- **California:** Statement of Information (due within 90 days of formation for LLCs), first-year franchise tax considerations
- **Florida:** Initial annual report (due by May 1 of the year following formation), fictitious name registration if applicable
- **Various states:** Publication requirements (New York requires LLCs to publish notice of formation in newspapers), additional state agency registrations, and industry-specific filings

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What happens if I do not file the Initial List on time?

In Nevada, failure to file the Initial List by the last day of the month following formation results in a $150 late penalty on top of the filing fee. Continued non-compliance leads to your entity being flagged as delinquent and eventually revoked.

### Are second-tier filing fees included in Incorporate123 packages?

Yes. Every Incorporate123 formation package includes all required second-tier filings and their associated state fees. We list these fees transparently in our package pricing so you know exactly what you are paying.

### What is the Nevada State Business License?

It is a state-level license required for every entity formed or registered in Nevada, separate from any city or county business license. The annual fee is $200 for LLCs and $500 for corporations, payable to the Secretary of State.

### Does Wyoming have second-tier filings?

Wyoming's post-formation requirements are minimal compared to Nevada. The main requirement is the annual report ($62), which is first due on the anniversary month of formation. There is no separate state business license requirement.

### Can nominees be listed on the Initial List?

Yes. When you purchase a package with privacy or nominee services, we file the Initial List with nominee officers, directors, or managers. Your personal name does not appear anywhere on the public record.

### What is a publication requirement?

Some states, most notably New York, require LLCs to publish a notice of formation in local newspapers. This can be surprisingly expensive — New York publication requirements can cost $1,000 or more depending on the county. Not all states have this requirement.

### How do I know if my post-formation filings were completed?

You can check with the Secretary of State in your formation state, or we can check for you. If you formed your entity through another service and are unsure whether second-tier filings were completed, contact us and we will review your entity's status.

### What if my entity was formed by another company and the filings were not done?

We can file the missing documents, pay any outstanding penalties, and bring your entity into full compliance. The sooner this is addressed, the less it costs — penalties and fees accumulate over time.

### Do second-tier filings need to be renewed annually?

Yes. The Initial List becomes the Annual List in subsequent years, and the business license must be renewed annually. Both have the same fees as the initial filing. Our renewal packages handle these filings automatically.

### Are second-tier filings required for foreign registrations too?

When you register a foreign entity in a new state, that state will have its own post-registration requirements — typically an initial report and potentially a business license. We handle these requirements as part of our foreign registration service.
