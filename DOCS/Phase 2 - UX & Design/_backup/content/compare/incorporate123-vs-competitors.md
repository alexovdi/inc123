## Budget Formation Services

Budget filers like LegalZoom, ZenBusiness, Incfile, and similar companies have built their business model around low advertised prices and aggressive upselling. Here is how the economics typically work.

**The $0-$99 advertised price** covers only the basic state filing. It does not include state filing fees (paid separately), registered agent service (upsold at $100-$300/year), operating agreement (upsold at $50-$200), EIN filing (upsold at $50-$100), compliance alerts (upsold at $100-$200/year), or annual report filing (upsold at $100-$200/year). By the time you add the services you actually need, the total cost often reaches $500-$1,500 in the first year.

**What budget filers cannot provide:** Nominee services, offshore record storage, privacy-focused formation, or specialized guidance for asset protection structuring. These services are not available at any price from most budget providers because their platform is designed for volume processing of standard formations, not privacy-specialized work.

**Who budget filers serve well:** Business owners who need a straightforward LLC or Corporation in their home state, have no privacy concerns, do not need asset protection structuring, and are comfortable managing their own compliance. If you are forming a simple LLC in your home state to operate a local business, a budget filer may be appropriate.

**Who they do not serve well:** Business owners who need ownership privacy, are forming in Wyoming or Nevada for strategic reasons, need nominee services, or want ongoing compliance managed for them. The services simply are not available.

## Premium Law Firms

Attorney-led formation services typically charge $3,000-$10,000+ for entity formation and structuring. Here is what the premium buys.

**Custom legal structuring.** An attorney can design a multi-entity structure, draft custom operating agreements for complex ownership arrangements, create domestic asset protection trusts, and provide legal opinions on the strength of your structure. This level of customization is genuinely valuable for high-net-worth individuals and complex situations.

**Legal advice.** Unlike formation services, attorneys can provide legal advice — guidance on your specific situation, litigation risk assessment, and strategies tailored to your circumstances. This is something neither we nor budget filers can offer.

**What the premium often does not include:** Many law firms do not provide ongoing compliance management, nominee services, or registered agent service as part of their formation fee. You may receive a beautifully structured entity with a $7,000 invoice and then need to find a separate service provider for registered agent, annual filings, and nominee maintenance.

**Who should use a law firm:** Individuals with significant assets ($1M+), complex multi-entity needs, specific litigation concerns, or situations that require legal advice to structure properly. If your situation is complex enough to need an attorney, the cost is justified.
