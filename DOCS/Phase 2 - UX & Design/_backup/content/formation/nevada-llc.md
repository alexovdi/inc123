## Why Nevada for Your LLC

### No State Income Tax

Nevada imposes no personal income tax and no corporate income tax. Your LLC income passes through to your personal tax return and is subject only to federal taxes (and taxes in your state of residence, if applicable). Nevada also has no franchise tax on LLCs.

### 50 Years of Business Case Law

Nevada's corporate and LLC statutes have been developed and tested through 50 years of litigation. This body of case law provides predictability. When disputes arise -- whether between members, with creditors, or in regulatory matters -- Nevada courts have established precedents that define how LLC protections work in practice. This legal maturity is an advantage over newer LLC-friendly states that lack the same judicial track record.

### Dedicated Business Court

Nevada operates a specialized business court that handles entity disputes, shareholder litigation, and commercial cases. Business court judges have expertise in corporate and LLC law, which leads to more consistent and informed decisions than general jurisdiction courts. For business owners, this means disputes involving your Nevada LLC are resolved by judges who understand the relevant statutes.

### Charging Order Protection

Under Nevada Revised Statutes NRS 86.401, a charging order is the exclusive remedy by which a judgment creditor of an LLC member may satisfy a judgment. A creditor cannot seize LLC assets, force a distribution, or take over management. The creditor can only receive an economic interest in distributions if and when the LLC decides to make them.

Nevada courts have upheld this protection in practice. In the landmark case *Paladin Commerce Center v. Thalden*, a Nevada bankruptcy court held that a creditor who obtained 100% of an LLC's membership interest still could not gain voting or management rights. The court ruled that only the operating agreement's process for admitting substitute members could grant those rights, and without manager consent, the creditor remained a mere assignee.

### Same-Day Filing

Incorporate123 hand-delivers formation documents to the Nevada Secretary of State's office in Carson City. This allows same-day filing and processing, which is critical for clients who need their entity active immediately for a transaction, bank account opening, or contract execution.

### Privacy Advantages

Nevada does not list LLC members on any public filing. The only names on public record are the managers (if manager-managed) or members (if member-managed) listed on the annual list of managers/members. With Incorporate123's nominee services (included in Gold and Silver packages), your name can be removed from this filing entirely. Combined with our offshore custodian of records service, your ownership information stays private even from Nevada Secretary of State requests under NRS 86.246.

**How much does it cost to form a Nevada LLC?**

**Do I need to live in Nevada to form an LLC there?**
No. Nevada has no residency requirement. If you operate your business in another state, you may need to file a foreign registration in that state.

**Is my name on public record with a Nevada LLC?**
With our Bronze package, the manager or member name appears on the annual list. With Silver and Gold packages, nominee services keep your personal name off all public filings.

**What is the annual cost to maintain a Nevada LLC?**
Nevada's state fees total approximately $350 per year (annual list + business license). Our renewal packages start at $225/year (Bronze) and include these fees plus registered agent service.

**What is the difference between the Bronze, Silver, and Gold packages?**
Bronze covers formation essentials. Silver adds nominee manager services to remove your name from public filings. Gold adds nominee EIN and offshore custodian of records for maximum privacy.

**Do I need an operating agreement for a Nevada LLC?**
Yes. While Nevada does not legally mandate one, operating without an operating agreement is risky. Without it, your LLC defaults to Nevada Revised Statutes rules, and courts may question whether the LLC is truly separate from you. Every package includes a custom operating agreement.

**What is a nominee manager?**
A nominee manager is an individual provided by Incorporate123 who appears as the manager of your LLC on public filings. You retain full control through a confidential operating agreement and power of attorney. The nominee acts on your instructions only.

**Can my Nevada LLC open a bank account anywhere?**
Yes. You can open a business bank account at any U.S. bank using your articles of organization and EIN. Many clients use national banks or online banking platforms.

**What is the offshore custodian of records service?**
Under NRS 86.241, an LLC must maintain a membership listing. NRS 86.246 allows the Secretary of State to request this information. Our Gold package designates an offshore company secretary as the custodian of your membership listing, placing this information outside the reach of domestic third-party record keeper summonses.

**Can I elect S-Corp taxation for my Nevada LLC?**
Yes. If your LLC's net income exceeds approximately $80,000 per year, an S-Corp election (IRS Form 2553) can reduce self-employment taxes. Your entity remains a Nevada LLC under state law.

**What happens if I miss the annual filing deadline?**
If the annual list is not filed by the last day of the anniversary month, Nevada imposes a $150 late fee. Continued non-filing results in revocation of the LLC's charter. Our renewal packages handle all filings to prevent this.
