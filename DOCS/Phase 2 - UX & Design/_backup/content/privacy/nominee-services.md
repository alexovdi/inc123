# Year-Round Nominee Services

There is a significant amount of confusion about what nominee services actually are and what they accomplish. The concept is straightforward: a nominee holds a titled position in your corporation or LLC — an office that appears in the public record — so that your personal name does not. You retain full ownership and operational control. The nominee's role is limited to maintaining your privacy in state filings.

What varies enormously is the quality of that service. Most Nevada incorporation companies offer "nominee for a day" — a few hours of coverage around an annual filing. Incorporate123 provides year-round nominee service, 365 days a year, for every client with a Gold or Private package. This is the standard in international corporate services, and it is the only level of nominee service we offer.

## What a Nominee Actually Does

A nominee holds one or more positions of record in your business entity. The specific positions depend on the entity type:

**For a Corporation:** We provide a nominee President, nominee Treasurer, and nominee Director. These are the positions that appear in Secretary of State filings and annual reports.

**For an LLC:** We provide a nominee Manager for manager-managed LLCs. This is the position that appears in state records for LLCs that elect manager management.

In both cases, we also appoint a company Secretary (mandatory for all our packages) who handles records preparation, filing, and serves as custodian of the stock ledger or membership listing.

You operate your company from a non-public position — typically CEO, Executive Vice President, or another executive title that carries full operational authority. You maintain 100% ownership through confidential stock certificates (for corporations) or membership interests (for LLCs) that are never filed with any state agency.

The nominee does not participate in your business operations. They do not make decisions, sign contracts on your behalf (unless specifically requested), or involve themselves in day-to-day management. The service exists for one purpose: keeping your name out of the public record.

## "Nominee for a Day" vs. Year-Round Service

The difference between these two models is fundamental, not cosmetic.

With "nominee for a day," a nominee is appointed on the morning of your annual filing. The state documents are submitted with the nominee's name. By the end of the day, the nominee resigns. You pay $500 or more for a few hours of coverage. For the other 364 days of the year, either no nominee is on file or you need to arrange (and pay for) another appointment.

With our year-round service, the nominee remains in position continuously. They are available to execute documents if needed — for example, signing a bank authorization, a lease, or a government form that requires an officer's signature. They can speak with third parties if a situation requires it. The appointment is uninterrupted from one annual filing to the next.

The annual cost for year-round nominee service is $525 when included in a Gold package renewal. That covers the entire year — not a single day.

## Offshore Nominees: Maximum Privacy

Since 2012, we have provided offshore nominees at no additional cost in every Gold and Private package. This is a service that many competitors either do not offer or charge up to $5,000 per year to provide.

An offshore nominee is a nominee officer or manager who resides outside the United States. The practical significance: an offshore nominee is outside the jurisdiction of U.S. courts and therefore not subject to domestic subpoena power. If a third party attempts to compel the nominee to produce records or appear in court, the U.S. court has no enforcement mechanism.

This does not create any offshore tax reporting obligations for you. The appointment of an offshore individual as a nominee officer does not trigger FBAR filings, FATCA reporting, or any special IRS forms. Your entity remains a domestic U.S. entity for all tax purposes.

## Offshore Custodian of Records

Following the passage of SB60 in 2013, Nevada statutes (NRS 78.105 and NRS 86.241) require entities to maintain certain records at their registered office. Registered agents must maintain copies of operating agreements, bylaws, stock ledgers, and membership listings. Under NRS 86.246, the Secretary of State can request these records from the registered agent.

The registered agent is an easy target for information requests — every company must have one, they are listed in the public record, and they must be available to receive service of process. Government agencies increasingly use third-party record keeper summonses to obtain ownership information directly from registered agents, sometimes without even notifying the entity itself.

Our solution: we appoint an offshore company secretary as the custodian of your stock ledger or membership listing. If the Secretary of State requests records under NRS 86.246, the registered agent provides a statement identifying the offshore custodian and their address. The operating agreement is structured to contain no personal ownership information — the membership listing is maintained as a separate document by the offshore custodian.

This service has been included free in all packages since 2012.
