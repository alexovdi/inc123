01 — Brand Overview

Who We Are

Incorporate123 is a deliberate repositioning from a geography-first "Nevada incorporation company" to a premium specialist in privacy, asset protection, and business formation. The company occupies a market position that no competitor has claimed.

✓ What Incorporate123 IS
A 25-year privacy and formation specialist Focused on Wyoming and Nevada. Premium but transparent — $1,275–$2,100 all-inclusive with no hidden fees. Expert-guided but self-service friendly. Privacy-first with year-round nominees, offshore records, and cryptocurrency accepted.
✗ What Incorporate123 is NOT
Not a budget filing service or a consultation-gated firm Not ZenBusiness/LegalZoom territory ($0 filing). Not Anderson Advisors territory ($2,750+ with mandatory consults). Not a generic "all 50 states" formation mill.

05 — Voice & Messaging

Brand Voice

Authoritative, educational, transparent, reassuring, and precise. The voice speaks from 25 years of specialized experience — never vague, never desperate, never competing on the wrong dimensions.

Authoritative
Do: Cite 25 years of experience. Reference specific statutes and structures.
Don't: Vague claims like "best in the business" without evidence.
Educational
Do: Explain why privacy matters. Teach the difference between privacy and asset protection.
Don't: Assume visitors already understand nominees, charging orders, etc.
Transparent
Do: Show all pricing upfront. Explain what's included and what's not.
Don't: Hidden pricing, fine-print exclusions, or auto-renewal traps.
Reassuring
Do: Emphasize 25-year track record, real office, ongoing support.
Don't: Fear-based selling — "you'll get sued if you don't act now."
Precise
Do: Use specific numbers: $1,275, 25 years, $525 annual renewal.
Don't: Round numbers, "affordable," "competitive pricing."

Messaging DOs & DON'Ts

✓ Do — Compete On

Privacy expertise — "25 years specializing in anonymous structures."

Nominee depth — "year-round nominees, not 'nominee for a day'."

Offshore capability — "nominees and records outside U.S. jurisdiction."

All-inclusive value — "what others charge $5,000/year for, we include."

Human service — "real experts, not automated forms."

Always establish context before presenting benefits.

✗ Don't — Never Compete On

Price — "we're cheaper."

Speed — "file in 5 minutes."

Ease — "simple 3-step process."

Breadth — "all 50 states."

Fear — urgency pressure or scare tactics.

Never lead with benefits before establishing relevance/context.

09 — Photography

Photography Direction

Photography must reinforce the "real office, real people" trust narrative. Every image choice supports or undermines the brand's credibility.

🏢
Office Photography

Clean, professional shots of the Reno office (Kingsbury Executive Suites). No stock photos of generic office interiors. The real office is a trust signal.

👤
Team Photography

Professional headshot of David for the About page and trust signals. Approachable but authoritative — this is the face of 25 years of expertise.

🚫
No Stock People

Avoid generic diverse-team-in-conference-room stock photography. It undermines the "real office, real people" trust signal immediately.

🎨
Abstract / Conceptual

For hero sections and pillar headers, use abstract shield/lock/vault imagery or geometric privacy-themed graphics. Preferred over stock photography.

🔒
Privacy Alignment

Never use photos showing identifiable individuals as "clients." It directly contradicts the privacy positioning the entire brand is built on.

10 — Content Patterns

Content Strategy

An 80/20 progressive disclosure model: serve the majority who scan quickly, then reward the minority who research deeply. Every page follows this dual structure.

The 80/20 Progressive Disclosure Model

👁️ For Scanners (80%)
Headlines Value prop in ≤8 words
Description 3–5 sentence overview
Comparison Visual table
Pricing Clear price + "all-inclusive"
FAQ Top 3–5 questions
CTA Prominent above fold
Trust Badge strip
🔍 For Researchers (20%)
Headlines Subheadlines with specifics
Description Expandable full explanation
Comparison Detailed prose below
Pricing Itemized breakdown
FAQ Accordion with 10–15 questions
CTA Multiple at decision points
Trust Full testimonials + case studies

CTA Hierarchy

Three button levels — primary (amber, conversion), secondary (outline, alternative path), and ghost (safety net, "talk to us").

Protect Your Privacy → View Packages & Pricing → Talk to Us
✓ Rule
One primary + one secondary per page. Every self-service CTA must have a "Talk to us" safety net nearby. Never three competing button styles in the same section.
✗ Avoid
Get Started Learn More Contact

Three competing primary-style buttons create decision paralysis and dilute conversion.
