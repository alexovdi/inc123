## The Strategic Shift

The website will be transformed from a geography-first model (organized by state: Nevada, Wyoming) to an intent-first model organized around what customers are actually searching for. This aligns the site with how David's prospects actually behave — they come looking for privacy and protection, not for a specific state.

## Key Client Insights

### How Privacy Actually Works

Incorporate123's privacy model centers on Wyoming nominee services. A client forms a WY entity with a Nominee Manager/President, then files a foreign registration in their home state (CA, FL, etc.) listing the nominee in public records. This provides full privacy while remaining compliant with state law. This mechanism must be clearly explained on the site.

### California & Florida — Untapped Growth Market

David identified CA and FL private incorporation as a major growth opportunity that has not been properly marketed. Businesses tied to those states can now achieve privacy via the WY nominee structure. Given the large populations in both states, dedicated packages and landing pages are warranted.

### Asset Protection Education

A common misconception among prospects is conflating "privacy" with "asset protection" — they think if they have privacy, they're protected. David stresses that assets need to be separated, and that LLCs are the preferred vehicle because clients want to retain ownership and control. A key educational message:

> *"A 'living trust' (that most people are comfortable with) has ZERO asset protection features." — David*

### Competitive Differentiation

Incorporate123's critical advantage over competitors is ongoing corporate records and minutes maintenance. Competitors like ShieldCorp provide only a "Nominee for a day" service without maintaining records, which David considers "pretty high risk." This must be prominently messaged to justify premium pricing.

### Self-Service Conversion

David prefers "Order Now" over "Call Now" — Wyoming packages are cheap enough to be an "impulse buy" and he does not want to spend all day on the phone. The site should prioritize self-service ordering.

## What's Deprioritized

### Banking Services

Banking was initially considered as a pillar but is now supportive only. Incorporate123 provides a "Banking Document Package" and sends clients to banks directly. They no longer have bank contacts to facilitate account opening. Content stays on the site but is not a primary SEO or navigation focus.

### International / Offshore

David is not currently offering international services to US persons. GILTI tax rules have killed demand — US residents almost always choose onshore options. Offshore content remains on the site for credibility (they operate as a Hong Kong corp) but SEO focus is entirely domestic: Wyoming, Nevada, and Shelf Companies.

## Content & UX Approach

The current site is rich with detailed content, but most visitors won't read it all. The agreed approach serves two audiences:

- **80% of visitors:** Need a concise layout to digest and convert quickly
- **20% of visitors:** Want the finer details to build trust before committing

David emphasized the details are critical for trust and differentiation. The solution is progressive disclosure: lead with concise, conversion-focused content, then make detailed content easily accessible for those who want depth.

The user journey follows: **Education** (pillar pages) → **Comparison** ("WY vs NV," "Best state for privacy") → **Packages** (specific offerings) → **Conversion** ("Order Now").

## Decisions & Commitments (Carry Forward)

The following decisions were confirmed by David during discovery and should be treated as project requirements:

1. **Privacy is the #1 draw.** All site architecture, messaging, and SEO strategy must lead with privacy.
2. **CA & FL private incorporation is a major growth opportunity** that needs prominent placement in the new site.
3. **Packages only, not a la carte.** All packages include Registered Agent and Custom Records Prep.
4. **"Order Now" over "Call Now."** Facilitate self-service ordering; WY packages are impulse-buy priced.
5. **Concise first, details accessible.** Progressive disclosure for 80% quick converters and 20% detail readers.
6. **Corporate records maintenance is THE differentiator** vs. competitors offering "nominee for a day" services.
7. **Banking is supportive, not primary.** Content stays but not a pillar or SEO focus.
8. **Offshore stays for credibility but SEO is domestic only.** GILTI killed US-person offshore demand.
9. **Clean up /services/ URL pattern.** Old keyword stuffing; 301 redirect everything.
