# **1\. Project Overview**

This Statement of Work defines the scope, deliverables, timeline, and terms for the complete redesign and redevelopment of the Incorporate123.co website. The project transforms the current geography-first website into a modern, intent-first platform organized around four content pillars: Business Privacy, Asset Protection, Company Formation, and Compliance.

The approved strategy document serves as the foundational reference for all work described in this SOW.

| Target Audience: Small business owners and entrepreneurs with $500k+ in assets who prioritize privacy and asset protection over lowest price. |
| :---- |

## **1.1 Parties**

**Client:** Incorporate123 (“Client”)

**Provider:** BIT Studios (“Provider”)

## **1.2 Project Objectives**

* Reposition Incorporate123 from “Nevada incorporation company” to “Privacy, Asset Protection & Business Formation Specialists”

* Implement a pillar/cluster site architecture organized around customer intent, not geography

* Design and develop a modern, trust-building website that reflects 25 years of expertise

* Create a clear conversion flow: Education → Specifics → Comparison → Package → Conversion

* Build SEO authority in privacy and asset protection keywords where competitors have limited presence

* Restructure packages into base packages with optional add-ons to reduce decision fatigue

# **2\. Scope of Work**

The project encompasses a complete website redesign and redevelopment across five overlapping workstreams. All work is based on the approved strategic framework including the four-pillar architecture, \~71-page site map, and intent-first positioning. The CMS platform (WordPress or Payload CMS) will be determined after Discovery and UX & Design, and agreed upon with the Client.

## **2.1 Current Site Inventory**

The current site contains approximately 171 URLs across 15 template types. Key metrics:

| Metric | Count | Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Total current URLs | \~171 | Including all paths and duplicates |
| Unique content pages | \~129 | After removing \~42 duplicate FAQ URLs |
| Current template types | 15 | Many serve only 1–3 pages |
| Learning Center FAQ items | \~111 | Across overlapping category paths |
| New site pages (target) | \~71 | Across 8–10 custom templates |
| 301 redirects required | \~170 | Every current URL mapped to new structure |

| Why 129 pages becomes 71 No content is being lost. The current site’s \~129 unique pages include significant duplication and fragmentation — the Learning Center alone has \~111 FAQ items, many appearing under multiple overlapping category URLs. Thin pages that serve only a handful of visitors sit alongside redundant service descriptions that cover the same ground from different angles. The new 71-page architecture consolidates all of that into focused, authoritative pages. FAQ content moves into contextual sections on pillar, cluster, and package pages rather than living as standalone URLs. Overlapping service pages merge into single pages that carry more weight with search engines. Every piece of content worth preserving is mapped to a specific destination in the new structure, and \~170 redirects ensure no traffic or search authority is lost in the transition. |
