# Company Formation Services

The state you choose, the entity type you select, and the way your company is structured all determine how much liability protection you receive, how your income is taxed, and whether your personal information stays off public record.

## Why Proper Formation Matters

Many entrepreneurs start as sole proprietors because it requires zero paperwork. The trade-off is significant: as a sole proprietor, you have unlimited personal liability for every business obligation. A single lawsuit, unpaid vendor invoice, or employee injury claim can reach your personal savings, home, and other assets.

Forming an LLC or corporation creates a legal barrier between your business obligations and your personal wealth. This is not a technicality. It is the foundational layer of asset protection that every other strategy builds upon. Courts have consistently upheld this separation when the entity is properly formed and maintained.

Beyond liability protection, the right entity structure can reduce your tax burden. An LLC taxed as an S-Corp, for example, can save a business owner earning $120,000 in net income thousands of dollars per year in self-employment taxes. A properly structured holding company can shield investment income and real estate from operating business liabilities.

The state of formation also matters. Wyoming charges a $100 filing fee with a $62 annual report and has no state income tax, no franchise tax, and the strongest charging order protection in the country. Nevada charges a $425 filing fee with higher annual costs, but offers a dedicated business court, same-day filing capability, and 50 years of corporate case law. Each state has specific advantages depending on your situation.

## Our Formation Services

### LLC Formation

A limited liability company combines the liability protection of a corporation with the tax flexibility and operational simplicity that most small business owners need. LLCs offer pass-through taxation by default, meaning business income flows directly to your personal tax return without entity-level taxation. Management structure is flexible -- you choose between member-managed and manager-managed, and your operating agreement sets the rules.

We form LLCs in Wyoming, Nevada, California, and Florida. Each formation includes articles of organization, a custom operating agreement, EIN registration, and first-year registered agent service.

### Corporation Formation

Corporations are the right structure when you plan to issue stock, bring on investors, establish a formal board of directors, or pursue a future IPO. Nevada and Wyoming both offer strong corporate statutes, with director and officer liability protections codified in state law.

Our corporation formations include articles of incorporation, custom bylaws, initial board resolutions, stock certificates, and first-year registered agent service.

### Shelf Companies

A shelf company is a pre-formed entity that has been maintained in good standing since its date of incorporation but has never conducted business. Incorporate123 maintains the largest shelf company inventory in Nevada, with entities dating back to 2000. Shelf companies are used when business history, transactional urgency, or corporate image matters. Every shelf company in our inventory has a clean history with no prior liabilities, no stock or membership interest ever issued, and an active EIN.

Shelf company pricing starts at $2,500 for LLCs and $2,800 for corporations, with annual renewals at $525.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need to live in Wyoming or Nevada to form a company there?**
No. Neither Wyoming nor Nevada requires residency. You can form and operate an entity in either state from anywhere in the world. If you conduct business in your home state, you may need to file a foreign registration there.

**What is the difference between an LLC and a corporation?**
An LLC offers pass-through taxation and flexible management structure with fewer formalities. A corporation issues stock, requires a board of directors, and follows more formal governance procedures. Most small business owners choose an LLC. Corporations are better suited for businesses seeking investors or planning a future public offering.

**How long does formation take?**
Wyoming formations complete in 5-7 business days with standard processing. Nevada offers same-day filing -- Incorporate123 hand-delivers your documents to the Secretary of State's office in Carson City.

**Are all state fees included in your packages?**
Yes. Every formation package includes all first-year state filing fees: articles of organization or incorporation, initial list of officers or managers, state business license (where applicable), and registered agent service.

**What is the cheapest way to form a company?**

**Do I need an operating agreement for my LLC?**
Yes. An operating agreement establishes ownership percentages, management structure, and the rules that govern your LLC. Without one, your LLC defaults to state statute rules, and courts may question whether your LLC is truly separate from you personally. Every Incorporate123 formation package includes a custom operating agreement.

**Can I upgrade to privacy services later?**
Yes. You can start with a standard formation and add privacy services such as nominee officers, nominee EIN, or offshore custodian of records at any time. Many clients begin with our Silver package and upgrade to Gold when they are ready.

**What ongoing costs should I expect?**
Annual costs depend on your state and package. Wyoming renewals start at $325 per year (Silver) or $525 (Gold). Nevada renewals start at $225 (Bronze), $325 (Silver), or $525 (Gold). These cover your registered agent, state filings, and compliance requirements.

**What is a registered agent and do I need one?**
A registered agent is a person or company designated to receive legal and state documents on behalf of your entity. Every state requires one. Your formation package includes first-year registered agent service, and renewal packages continue this service.
