# International & Offshore Services

International structuring places your assets beyond the reach of U.S. courts, adds geographic diversification to your wealth, and opens access to banking and investment platforms unavailable domestically. When implemented with full IRS compliance, offshore structures are entirely legal and represent a proven strategy used by individuals and businesses worldwide.

## What Is Offshore Asset Protection?

The core principle is straightforward: assets held outside the United States sit beyond the jurisdiction of U.S. courts. A domestic creditor with a U.S. judgment cannot simply seize funds in a foreign bank account or compel a foreign entity to surrender assets. The creditor must instead initiate proceedings in the foreign jurisdiction, facing unfamiliar legal systems, higher costs, and statutory protections designed to shield legitimate asset holders.

Offshore asset protection typically involves three components working together:

- **An international company** (LLC, corporation, or business company) to hold and manage assets
- **An offshore bank account** to secure liquid assets outside U.S. jurisdiction
- **An asset protection trust** (optional but recommended) to provide the strongest layer of protection by removing ownership entirely

## Pass-Through Tax Elections

For U.S. persons, we can elect to have your international entity treated as a "Foreign Disregarded Entity" (single member) or "Foreign Partnership" (multiple members) for IRS purposes. This means income flows through to your personal Form 1040, eliminating the need for complex Controlled Foreign Corporation (Form 5471) filings.

This election is available for Nevis, BVI, and Hong Kong entities. Panama corporations are not eligible and require annual Form 5471 filing.

Pass-through election setup is included free with every eligible package.

## IRS Compliance Is Not Optional

We believe that marketing offshore entities to U.S. persons without full disclosure of IRS reporting requirements is unethical. Many providers avoid this topic entirely, leaving clients exposed to penalties that can exceed the value of the assets being protected.

Every U.S. person with offshore structures has reporting obligations. The specific forms depend on your structure, but may include Forms 8858, 8865, 5471, 8938, FBAR, 3520, 3520-A, and 926. We provide detailed guidance on every applicable form and send filing deadline reminders throughout the year.

Disclosing assets to the IRS does not give the IRS the ability to seize them. Once assets are held offshore, the IRS becomes a creditor like any other and must pursue collection through the same complex legal procedures that protect you from private creditors.

## FAQ

**Is offshore asset protection legal?**
Yes. U.S. persons have every right to hold assets internationally, form companies in foreign jurisdictions, and maintain offshore bank accounts. The legal requirement is full compliance with IRS reporting obligations.

**Do I need to travel to open an offshore bank account?**
In most cases, no. Our nominee director handles the banking process remotely. Certain banks may require an in-person visit for larger accounts, which we will discuss with you in advance.

**How long does the formation process take?**
Most formations are completed within 2-4 weeks, depending on the jurisdiction and the speed of KYC document collection. Bank account opening may take an additional 2-4 weeks.

**Which jurisdiction is right for me?**
This depends on your goals. Nevis is strongest for pure asset protection. BVI offers the widest international banking acceptance. Panama is ideal for operating businesses. Hong Kong provides access to Asian markets. We recommend a consultation to discuss your specific situation.

**What happens to my offshore trust after my death?**
For U.S. person settlors, the trust becomes a true foreign trust for tax purposes. The trust fund may grow tax-free for generations with no U.S. tax due and no U.S. reporting requirements, except reporting distributions made to U.S. beneficiaries.

**Can I combine offshore and domestic structures?**
Absolutely. Many clients pair a Wyoming or Nevada LLC with an offshore entity and trust to create layered protection across multiple jurisdictions.
