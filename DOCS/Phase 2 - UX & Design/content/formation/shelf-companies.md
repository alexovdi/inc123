# Shelf Companies for Sale

A shelf company is a pre-formed business entity that was incorporated and then maintained in good standing without ever conducting business. The entity sits "on the shelf" -- fully formed, compliant, and aging -- until a buyer is ready to use it. Shelf companies are also called aged companies or pre-formed companies.

Incorporate123 maintains the largest shelf company inventory in Nevada. Our inventory includes Wyoming LLCs and corporations dating back to 2000, each with a clean history, no prior liabilities, no stock or membership interest ever issued, and an active IRS EIN number. We have invested in every shelf company in our inventory each year, paying for annual Secretary of State filings and state business licenses to keep them in continuous good standing.

### Established Business History

A shelf company carries an incorporation date that goes back years or even decades. When a bank, lender, vendor, or potential business partner reviews your company, they see an entity that has existed for an extended period. While a shelf company does not come with business credit history or revenue, the incorporation date itself can contribute to a perception of stability and longevity.

### Transactional Urgency

Some business situations require an active entity immediately -- a real estate closing, a contract that must be executed under a company name, or a licensing application that requires a corporate entity. A shelf company is already formed, has an active EIN, and is in good standing with the Secretary of State. Transfer of ownership can be completed the same day.

### Government Contract Eligibility

Certain government contracts and requests for proposals require that the bidding entity has been in existence for a minimum number of years. A shelf company with the right age can meet this threshold. Note that other qualifications (revenue history, past performance, certifications) typically apply as well, and a shelf company alone does not satisfy all requirements.

### Corporate Image

For some businesses, presenting an entity with a long formation history supports the perception of an established, experienced company. This can matter in industries where longevity signals reliability.

## Important Disclosures

**Legitimate use only.** There are many perfectly legitimate and legal uses for shelf companies. We will not complete a sale to any client we believe intends to use the entity for fraudulent or deceptive purposes.

**No fabricated history.** A shelf company provides an authentic incorporation date and continuous good standing. It does not provide business credit history, revenue history, financial statements, or operational track record. Any representation of business activity prior to your acquisition would be inaccurate and potentially fraudulent.

**Due diligence.** We encourage all buyers to consult with their attorney and accountant regarding the use of a shelf company for their specific purpose. Tax and legal implications vary depending on how the entity will be used.
