## Wireframe Improvement Notes

### 1. Founder Section Needs a "Direct Access" Framing, Not Just Bio

The wireframe structures David's bio as a traditional founder section (photo, name, title, 3 paragraphs). This is fine structurally, but the content direction should emphasize David's accessibility — the fact that you can call him directly. This is the #1 competitive differentiator against both budget mills (no human contact) and premium firms (gated behind sales consultations).

**Recommendation:** Lead the bio with David's accessibility, not his resume. First paragraph should establish "you can call David directly" before diving into credentials. The direct phone number should be visually prominent — not buried at the bottom of the bio.

### 2. Timeline Milestones Need David's Verification — Flag This as Blocker

The wireframe includes approximate years (~2000, ~2003, ~2005, etc.) for major milestones. If these dates are wrong, they undermine the credibility the timeline is supposed to build. This content cannot be placeholder at launch.

**Recommendation:** Elevate timeline verification to a Phase 2 blocker. David must confirm or correct every year and milestone before the About page moves to design. Include this in the next client review agenda.

### 3. "What Makes Us Different" Section Overlaps with Homepage Differentiators

The wireframe acknowledges this ("not a repeat of the homepage differentiator grid") and frames it as philosophy instead of features. Good — but the content direction should ensure zero copy-paste overlap. Visitors who saw the homepage and clicked through to About expect new information.

**Recommendation:** Homepage differentiators = feature-based ("Year-Round Nominees Included"). About page philosophy = values-based ("Specialists, Not Generalists"). The overlap is in intent, not content. Make sure David writes distinct copy for each.

### 4. "By the Numbers" Stats Need at Least One Verifiable Metric David Can Confirm

The wireframe offers "25+ Years," "2 States," "$525 Renewal," and "100% Transparent Pricing." These are all factual but none is a customer-volume metric. If David can supply a verified entity count (even "1,000+ entities" is better than nothing), it adds a credibility dimension the other stats lack.

**Recommendation:** Push David for a verified entity count. Even a conservative number is powerful. If unavailable, the current four stats work — just flag this as a post-launch improvement.

### 5. Office Section Missing "We're Real" Hook

The wireframe lists address, phone, hours, and photos — all correct. But it misses the strongest trust line: "Our office is minutes from the Nevada Secretary of State — we hand-deliver filing documents for same-day processing." This line appears in the wireframe's Nevada Note field but deserves to be the section's headline hook, not a footnote.

**Recommendation:** Lead the office section with the hand-delivery / same-day filing angle. This transforms the office from "we have a real address" to "our physical location gives you a tangible service advantage."

| **Direct Contact** | **"Call David directly: (775) xxx-xxxx"** — large, prominent, tap-to-call. Above or immediately after the name/title, not buried at the bottom. |
| **Trust Signals** | "25+ years · Reno, NV office · Direct access — no call center" |

### Bio Direction (3 paragraphs, David to write or approve)

**Paragraph 1 — Accessibility first:**
Open with what makes David different from every other incorporation company founder: you can call him. Directly. No phone tree, no receptionist gatekeeping, no "schedule a paid consultation." This is the hook — it's what distinguishes Incorporate123 from both budget mills and premium consultancies.

**Paragraph 2 — Expertise:**
What David has built over 25 years. The nominee service infrastructure, the offshore records custodian capability, relationships with Wyoming and Nevada Secretary of State offices. Specific, not generic — "25 years forming Wyoming and Nevada entities" not "decades of experience in the industry."

**Paragraph 3 — Philosophy:**
Why David accepts cryptocurrency. Why pricing is transparent. Why he doesn't gate services behind consultations. The philosophy should feel principled, not marketing — David does these things because they're right for privacy-conscious clients, not because they're a business strategy.

### Bio Writing Rules

- Third person ("David has..." not "I have...")
- Specific numbers always ("25 years" not "decades")
- Human language ("answers his own phone" not "committed to client excellence")
- No fear-based language
- No superlatives without evidence

## "What Makes Us Different" — Philosophy Section

3 cards. Values-based, not feature-based (distinct from homepage differentiators).

| Card | Title | Content Direction |
|------|-------|------------------|
| 1 | "Specialists, Not Generalists" | We don't cover 50 states. We don't sell $0 formations. Wyoming and Nevada only — 25 years of depth in two states beats surface knowledge of fifty. |
| 2 | "Transparent, Not Gated" | Every price is on the website. You can order now or call to discuss — your choice. We never hide pricing behind a "free consultation" that's really a sales pitch. |
| 3 | "Real People, Not Automation" | When you call, a real person answers. Your formation is handled by specialists who know your name. This is what premium service actually means. |

**David's writing brief:** These three cards should feel like company values, not marketing bullet points. Each one implicitly contrasts with a competitor type: Card 1 vs. LegalZoom/ZenBusiness (all 50 states, no depth). Card 2 vs. Anderson Advisors (consultation-gated, no public pricing). Card 3 vs. budget mills (850,000 filings/year, zero human contact). Never name competitors — the contrast should be obvious from the descriptions alone.
