# Nevada Registered Agent

Every LLC and corporation formed in Nevada must designate a registered agent with a physical street address in the state. This is required under NRS 77.310, and the Secretary of State will not accept formation documents without a registered agent designation on file.

Incorporate123 has been providing registered agent services from our Reno, Nevada office for over 25 years. ## What a Nevada Registered Agent Does

A registered agent in Nevada is the authorized representative of your entity for receiving legal and official documents. The specific duties, as defined by Nevada law, include:

- **Receiving service of process** — Lawsuits, subpoenas, and legal notices directed at your entity are delivered to the registered agent's physical address.
- **Accepting state correspondence** — Annual list reminders, business license renewal notices, and compliance communications from the Nevada Secretary of State.
- **Forwarding documents** — The agent must promptly forward all received documents to the entity at the most recently provided address.
- **Providing required notices** — Certain statutory notices must be delivered to the entity through the registered agent.

Under NRS 77, Nevada registered agents have additional regulatory obligations beyond those in most other states. Commercial registered agents must maintain a physical office, keep records of all represented entities, and comply with specific recordkeeping and reporting requirements.

## Nevada's Expanded Registered Agent Obligations

Nevada imposes more responsibilities on registered agents than most other states. Following the passage of SB60 in 2013, Nevada statutes NRS 78.105 and NRS 86.241 require:

- All entities must maintain certain records at their registered office
- Registered agents must maintain records on their corporate clients, including copies of operating agreements or bylaws
- Registered agents must maintain the stock ledger or membership listing — or a statement identifying who holds these records and where they are kept
- The Secretary of State can request these records from the registered agent under NRS 86.246

Failure to comply can result in the registered agent facing fines and having their registration revoked under NRS 77.443. This creates a potential privacy issue: records held by a Nevada registered agent can be disclosed to the IRS, state tax authorities, law enforcement, labor agencies, and other government bodies.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is a registered agent required for a Nevada LLC?

Yes. NRS 77.310 requires every Nevada LLC, corporation, and limited partnership to maintain a registered agent with a physical street address in Nevada. The entity cannot be formed without one.

### What is a Commercial Registered Agent?

A Commercial Registered Agent is a person or entity that has filed a listing with the Nevada Secretary of State to serve as registered agent for multiple entities. Commercial registered agents are subject to additional regulatory requirements under NRS 77, including specific recordkeeping and reporting obligations.

In addition to the registered agent service, your Nevada entity will need to pay: $150 for the annual list (LLC), $200 for the state business license (LLC), and potentially the initial list filing fee of $150 + $200 in the first year. ### What happens if my Nevada registered agent fails to comply with SB60?

Under NRS 77.443, a registered agent that fails to meet Nevada's recordkeeping requirements can face fines and have their agent registration revoked. If your registered agent is revoked, your entity loses its designated agent and must file a new one or risk losing good standing. Incorporate123 has been in full compliance with SB60 requirements since their inception in 2013.
