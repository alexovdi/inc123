## 2. Strategic Direction — Confirmed by Client

### 2.1 Core Concept: Geography-First to Intent-First

The fundamental strategic shift agreed upon is moving the site from being a "Nevada incorporation company that also does other stuff" to an intent-first platform organized around what prospects are actually looking for: privacy, asset protection, and business formation.

Jeffery proposed this framing and David confirmed it aligns with how prospects actually find them. David emphasized that most prospects come for PRIVACY, and the main problem is that people confuse "asset protection" with "privacy" — they don't understand assets need to be separated.

### 2.2 Agreed Pillar Structure

| Pillar | Sub-Topics | Key Notes from David |
|---|---|---|
| **Company Formation** | WY, NV, Shelf Companies | Wyoming & Nevada specialists. Prefers "Business Incorporation" as more American. WY packages cheap enough to be "impulse buy." |
| **Business Privacy** | CA Private, FL Private, Anonymous LLCs, Nominee Services | Main selling point. Customers want Privacy but need to learn it comes from Nominee Services. Big untapped opportunity in CA & FL private incorporation. |
| **Asset Protection** | WY Holding LLC, NV Asset Protection, Charging Order Protection | Target audience: anyone with ~$500K+ in assets. LLC is favored over trusts because clients want ownership & control. |
| **Compliance** | Registered Agent, Annual Reports | Minimum necessary compliance. ALL packages include Registered Agent and Custom Records Prep. |

### 2.3 Banking Pillar — Deprioritized

Banking was initially proposed as a 5th pillar but was deprioritized after David explained the landscape has changed. They no longer have direct bank contacts and instead focus on preparing the paperwork ("Banking Document Package") and sending clients to banks directly. Banking content will remain on the site but is not a primary pillar for SEO or navigation.

### 2.4 International / Offshore — De-emphasized for SEO

David confirmed he is not currently offering international services to US persons. While offshore content will stay on the site (it demonstrates expertise and they operate as a Hong Kong corp), SEO focus should concentrate on Wyoming and Nevada services plus Shelf Companies. GILTI tax rules have effectively killed offshore entity demand for US residents — they almost always opt for the onshore option.

## 3. Privacy & Nominee Services — Critical Business Logic

This section captures David's detailed explanation of how their privacy services actually work, which is essential for accurate website content and messaging.

### 3.1 How Privacy Is Achieved

The mechanism for achieving business privacy through Incorporate123 works as follows:

1. Client forms a Wyoming company with Privacy Services (Nominee Manager/President/Treasurer provided)
2. The Nominee "creates" the privacy by appearing in corporate records
3. When registering (foreign filing) in CA/FL, the Nominee Manager/President is listed in the PUBLIC record
4. This gives the client privacy while being "Fully Compliant" with CA/FL law
5. The client can now operate from their home state with privacy

### 3.2 CA & FL Private Incorporation — Major Growth Opportunity

David identified a significant untapped market: California and Florida private incorporation services. This is important because:

- Those seeking WY/NV incorporation for business are typically running virtual businesses
- A business tied to a state (CA or FL) can NOW also have "privacy" via the WY nominee structure
- CA and FL have large populations, so specific packages were developed for those states
- Advertising FL Private Corp/LLC and CA Private Corp/LLC "expands our market substantially"

### 3.3 Nominee Services vs. Competitors

David explained a critical competitive differentiator: Incorporate123 maintains ongoing corporate records and minutes for nominee clients. Competitors like ShieldCorp likely only provide a "Nominee for a day" service and do NOT maintain corporate records/minutes, which David considers "pretty high risk." This needs to be prominently explained on the website to justify the premium.

## 4. Asset Protection — Educational Messaging

### 4.1 LLC vs. Trust

David provided key educational points that should inform website content:

- Always recommends holding assets in an LLC rather than directly in a trust
- For estate planning, a trust can become the member of the LLC
- Best asset protection = give up ALL ownership via irrevocable trust, but "few have the guts for that"
- LLC is the favored solution because clients want ownership AND control

### 4.2 Critical Distinction About Living Trusts

> *"A 'living trust' (that most people are comfortable with) has ZERO asset protection features." — David*

This is a powerful educational message that should be prominently featured in asset protection content. It directly addresses a common misconception among the target audience.

### 4.3 Target Audience for Asset Protection

Small business owners and "wantabees" (small start-ups) for formation services. For asset protection specifically: anyone with approximately $500,000+ in assets.

## 6. Content Strategy & UX Direction

### 6.1 The 80/20 Content Problem

Jeffery identified that the current site is "super content rich and most people won't read it." The agreed approach:

- **80% of visitors:** Need a concise layout to digest and convert quickly
- **20% of visitors:** Want to read the finer details for trust-building

David agreed but emphasized: "The details are important... to gain trust." The solution is progressive disclosure — start concise, make details accessible for those who want depth.

### 6.2 User Journey: Education → Comparison → Conversion

Jeffery proposed a content flow confirmed by David:

1. **Education:** Pillar pages that educate on privacy, asset protection, formation
2. **Comparison:** "If you need X... Consider Y" pages (WY vs NV, Best state for privacy, CA Private, FL Private, etc.)
3. **Packages:** Specific package pages (/wyoming-llc-package/, /nevada-llc-package/, /california-private-package/)
4. **Conversion:** "Order Now" button (not "Call Now")

### 6.3 Landing Page Strategy

David noted that on landing pages, they can be more "general" and lead visitors to the services. The separation of "asset protection" and "business incorporation" on the site is the right structural approach, but landing pages should be broader funnels. New landing page needed for "Business Privacy" since the current privacy page is too nuts-and-bolts about nominee services.

### 6.4 Main Selling Points

David was emphatic about the two main selling points:

- **PRIVACY** — The primary draw
- **MINIMUM and NECESSARY COMPLIANCE** — The essential add-on

## 10. Key Takeaways to Carry Forward

**Strategic Positioning**
Privacy is the #1 draw. Everything else (formation, compliance, asset protection) supports the privacy story. The site must educate visitors on how privacy is actually achieved through nominee services and Wyoming incorporation structures.

**CA/FL Opportunity**
David sees California and Florida private incorporation as a major growth market that has not been properly marketed. This should be given significant prominence in the new site architecture and content strategy.

**Content Approach**
Concise first, details accessible. Never remove the detailed content (it builds trust and differentiates from competitors), but present it progressively. 80% of users should be able to digest and convert quickly.

**Conversion Preference**
David prefers "Order Now" over "Call Now" — he doesn't want to spend all day on the phone. WY packages are priced as impulse buys. The site should facilitate self-service ordering wherever possible.

**Competitive Differentiation**
The key differentiator vs. competitors (especially ShieldCorp) is ongoing corporate records/minutes maintenance. "Nominee for a day" services are high-risk. This must be explained prominently to justify premium pricing.

**Banking — Supportive, Not Primary**
Banking services stay on the site but are supportive. Incorporate123 provides the Banking Document Package; clients go to banks themselves. With nominee services, banking is harder (especially with NV entities), so WY is recommended. Not a primary pillar.

**International — On Site but Not SEO Priority**
Offshore content stays for credibility (they're an HK corp) but SEO focus is domestic: Wyoming, Nevada, and Shelf Companies. GILTI has killed US-person offshore demand.

**Technical Debt**
Old /services/ URL structure must be cleaned up. Fresh GA and GSC installation needed. Review platform needed to replace BBB.
